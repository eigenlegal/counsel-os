# Data Privacy

## Trigger Conditions

Load this area when a matter involves: personal data, personal information, end-user records, hosted processing of customer data, data processing agreements, vendors or service providers handling user data.

## Key Constraints

- **GDPR Art. 28(3):** processing by a processor must be governed by a binding contract setting out subject matter, duration, nature and purpose, data types, and the mandatory processor obligations (instructions-only processing, confidentiality, security, sub-processor controls, assistance, deletion/return, audit). Processing EU personal data through a vendor without these contract terms is itself a violation.
- **CCPA/CPRA — service provider contracts (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100(d)):** disclosing California consumers' personal information to a vendor requires a written contract restricting retention, use, and disclosure to the contracted business purpose. Without the contract terms, the disclosure can constitute a "sale" or "sharing."

## Consequence

GDPR Art. 83(4) fines up to EUR 10M or 2% of worldwide turnover for Art. 28 violations; CCPA enforcement and statutory damages exposure for unprotected disclosures.
