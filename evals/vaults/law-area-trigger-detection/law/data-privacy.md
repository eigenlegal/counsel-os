# Data Privacy

## Trigger Conditions

Load this area when a matter involves: personal data, personal information, user profile data, birthdates or age data, children, minors, users under 13, parental consent, kids' accounts, data sharing with vendors or service providers, data processing agreements.

## Key Constraints

- **COPPA — verifiable parental consent (16 CFR 312.5):** an operator of an online service directed to children (or with actual knowledge it collects from children under 13) must obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from a child. Birthdates, account identifiers, and transaction histories of under-13 users are personal information. Disclosure to a payment vendor is a "disclosure" requiring consent coverage.
- **COPPA — service-provider assurances (16 CFR 312.8):** the operator may release children's personal information only to service providers and third parties who are capable of maintaining its confidentiality, security, and integrity, **and who provide written assurances that they will do so**. The vendor agreement must contain these written assurances — the operator stays liable for the vendor's handling.
- **COPPA — retention and deletion (16 CFR 312.10):** children's personal information may be retained only as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose collected, then must be deleted with reasonable measures against unauthorized access. Vendor contracts need matching retention/deletion obligations.
- **State overlay:** CCPA/CPRA requires opt-in consent for selling or sharing personal information of consumers under 16; service-provider contract terms under Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100(d) are required when personal information is disclosed to a vendor.

## Consequence

COPPA civil penalties are assessed per violation (per child, per day for continuing violations) — $53,088 per violation as adjusted for 2025 — and FTC consent decrees routinely impose decades of compliance monitoring.
