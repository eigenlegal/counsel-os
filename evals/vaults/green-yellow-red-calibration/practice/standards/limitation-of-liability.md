# Limitation of Liability

## Our Position

**Our standard:** Vendor agreements must cap each party's liability at no less than twenty-four (24) months of fees. We buy mission-critical software and services with concentrated vendor dependencies; in any realistic dispute we are the claimant, and a 12-month cap routinely leaves recovery short of our switching, remediation, and line-downtime costs. The symmetry cost is deliberate — our own exposure as customer is essentially fee payment.

**We'll accept:** A cap of at least twelve (12) but below twenty-four (24) months of fees, with head-of-legal sign-off, where the vendor is fungible or annual fees exceed $2M.

**We won't accept:** Caps below twelve (12) months of fees, or any cap that applies to the vendor's indemnification obligations.

## Classification Guide

- **GREEN**: Cap at twenty-four (24) months of fees or higher, or uncapped.
- **YELLOW**: Cap at least twelve (12) but below twenty-four (24) months — sign-off required.
- **RED**: Cap below twelve (12) months, or a cap applying to vendor indemnification obligations.

## Practical Guidance

Vendors will push back that 12-month caps are market standard. True, and irrelevant — the 24-month floor is priced into our vendor selection, and the guide above sets the classification.
