# Most Favored Nation

## Our Position

**Our standard:** We do not grant most-favored-nation, most-favored-customer, or pricing-parity commitments. They constrain pricing across the entire book, are operationally impossible to audit, and create retroactive refund exposure on every future discount decision.

**We'll accept:** Nothing in this category without General Counsel approval — see the escalation thresholds in the practice profile.

**We won't accept:** Any MFN or pricing-parity clause, including "no less favorable pricing" representations, automatic price-matching, or retroactive true-ups.

## Classification Guide

- **GREEN**: No MFN or pricing-parity language.
- **RED**: Any MFN, most-favored-customer, pricing-parity, or price-matching commitment in any form. There is no YELLOW for this clause type.

## Practical Guidance

Customers usually accept a volume-based discount schedule as the substitute — it gives procurement a defensible price story without binding our book. Offer that, not a narrowed MFN.
