## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- ADA Title III prohibits disability discrimination in the full and equal enjoyment of the goods, services, and facilities of a place of public accommodation; no contractual disclaimer can waive a covered entity's accessibility obligation (42 U.S.C. 12182). Private Title III actions are limited to injunctive relief and attorney's fees, but state-law claims (e.g., California Unruh) routinely add monetary damages.
- Section 508 requires ICT developed, procured, maintained, or used by federal agencies to be accessible; a materially inaccurate VPAT/ACR submitted in federal procurement can create False Claims Act exposure (29 U.S.C. 794d).
- For state and local government web content and mobile apps, the DOJ Title II rule mandates conformance to WCAG 2.1 Level AA; compliance dates were extended by a one-year interim final rule (effective Apr. 20, 2026) — Apr. 26, 2027 for entities with population 50,000+, and Apr. 26, 2028 for smaller public entities and special district governments (28 CFR Part 35).
- For recipients of HHS federal financial assistance, the Section 504 rule (May 9, 2024) requires web/mobile conformance to WCAG 2.1 Level A and AA; compliance dates were extended by a one-year interim final rule (issued May 7, 2026) — May 11, 2027 for recipients with 15+ employees, and May 10, 2028 for smaller recipients (45 CFR Part 84).
- WCAG conformance is evaluated at the level of full pages and complete processes; partial-page conformance cannot be claimed, and non-conforming content must not interfere with the accessibility of conforming content (W3C WCAG conformance requirements).
- The ADA does not preempt state disability laws providing equal or greater protection; plaintiffs may stack federal injunctive relief with state monetary-damages claims (e.g., California Unruh's $4,000 statutory minimum per occurrence).
