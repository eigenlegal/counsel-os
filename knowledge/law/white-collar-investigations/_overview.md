## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- A litigation hold must be implemented the moment litigation or a government investigation is reasonably anticipated; failure to preserve relevant evidence exposes the organization to spoliation sanctions regardless of any internal policy or contract.
- Upjohn warnings (the lawyer represents the company, not the employee; the privilege belongs to the company and the company may waive it) must be given before substantive employee interviews in an internal investigation; the attorney-client privilege belongs to the entity, not the individual.
- Retaliation against an employee for protected whistleblowing or for participating in an investigation is independently unlawful under SOX 806, Dodd-Frank, the False Claims Act, and state law; no compliance program or settlement can authorize it.
- SOX Section 1107 (18 U.S.C. § 1513(e)) imposes criminal liability for retaliating against any person who provides truthful information to law enforcement about a federal offense, and applies to all entities, not just public companies.
- A financial institution must file a Suspicious Activity Report when it knows or suspects fraud, money laundering, or a BSA violation meeting the regulatory threshold; the existence and content of a SAR is confidential and may not be disclosed (the SAR no-disclosure rule).
- DOJ cooperation credit requires disclosure of all non-privileged facts about the individuals involved in the misconduct; selective or "drip" disclosure of individual culpability forfeits credit.
- The federal anti-bribery provisions of the FCPA, the books-and-records and internal-controls provisions, and the anti-fraud statutes apply regardless of DOJ's prevailing enforcement priorities; a shift in charging emphasis (including the 2025 FCPA enforcement guidelines and pause) does not repeal the statutes or the underlying legal obligations.
