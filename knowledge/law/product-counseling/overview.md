## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- CPSC reporting obligations (Section 15(b) of the CPSA) require manufacturers, importers, distributors, and retailers to report products that contain defects that could create a substantial product hazard, or that create an unreasonable risk of serious injury or death. Reporting must occur within 24 hours of obtaining reportable information. Failure to report is a federal offense with civil penalties up to $120,000 per violation ($17.15 million cap) and potential criminal prosecution.
- Implied warranty of merchantability under UCC Article 2 cannot be disclaimed in consumer transactions in some jurisdictions. Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prohibits disclaimer of implied warranties when a written warranty is provided.
- Strict product liability (in jurisdictions that follow it) cannot be contractually disclaimed or limited between the manufacturer and the injured consumer — limitation-of-liability clauses do not protect against third-party product liability claims.
- FDA pre-market approval or clearance requirements for regulated products (drugs, medical devices, biologics) cannot be waived or contracted around.
- Product recall obligations, once triggered, are regulatory mandates that cannot be deferred by contractual provisions between supply chain parties. Recall costs cannot be shifted to consumers.
- Failure-to-warn liability attaches when a manufacturer knows or should know of product risks and fails to provide adequate warnings, regardless of contractual disclaimers. Post-sale duty to warn (Restatement Third, Section 10) is an ongoing obligation triggered by newly discovered risks.
- CPSIA lead content limit (100 ppm in accessible parts of children's products) and phthalate limits (0.1% in children's toys and child care articles) are absolute limits — products exceeding these thresholds are banned hazardous substances.
- Children's products require third-party testing by a CPSC-accepted laboratory and a Children's Product Certificate (CPC) before sale or importation. Self-certification is not permitted for children's products.
- California Proposition 65 requires clear and reasonable warnings for products containing listed chemicals above safe harbor thresholds, with civil penalties up to $2,500 per day per violation enforceable through private bounty actions.
