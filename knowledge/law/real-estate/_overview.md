## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- A deed must be recorded in the county where the property sits to give constructive notice; in race-notice jurisdictions a later bona fide purchaser who records first can defeat a prior unrecorded interest. No contract clause cures a recording failure.
- CERCLA Section 107 imposes strict, joint-and-several, retroactive liability on current owners/operators regardless of fault. The innocent-purchaser, bona fide prospective purchaser, and contiguous-property defenses require All Appropriate Inquiry (a Phase I meeting ASTM E1527-21 for non-forestland) completed before acquisition plus post-acquisition continuing obligations.
- Mechanic's lien and bond-claim rights are creatures of statute: missing a state preliminary-notice, recording, or enforcement-suit deadline (or, on federal projects, a Miller Act notice or suit deadline) permanently forfeits the claim. The deadlines cannot be waived forward by private agreement.
- Discriminatory covenants (race, religion, national origin) are unenforceable under the Fair Housing Act even if recorded in CC&Rs.
- Use and development must conform to applicable zoning and land-use approvals; operating outside a permitted use without a variance, conditional use permit, or rezoning is a continuing violation, and a legal nonconforming use generally cannot be expanded or revived after statutory abandonment.
- Transfer (documentary) taxes are owed on a covered conveyance regardless of how the parties allocate them by contract; improperly claimed exemptions trigger back taxes, penalties, and interest.
