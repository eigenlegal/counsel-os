# Government Contracts

## Trigger Conditions

Load this area when the matter involves ANY of:
- Government contract, federal contract, state/local procurement
- FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) or DFAR (Defense FAR Supplement)
- Procurement, federal acquisition, competitive bidding
- Bid protest (GAO, COFC)
- Set-aside programs (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB)
- FOIA (Freedom of Information Act) requests or responses
- Sovereign immunity, Tucker Act, FTCA
- GSA Schedule, IDIQ, BPA

## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- A government contract or order may be awarded only through full and open competition or a recognized exception with the required documentation (e.g., a written Justification and Approval under FAR 6.302); an unauthorized sole-source award is subject to protest and corrective action.
- An entity must hold active SAM.gov registration to receive a federal contract award; misrepresentations in SAM and in required representations and certifications carry False Claims Act exposure.
- A debarred or suspended party is ineligible for new contracts, subcontracts, and federal assistance government-wide, and knowing award to an excluded party violates the FAR.
- Mandatory FAR/DFARS clauses flow down to subcontractors at all tiers as required; a prime cannot contract away its responsibility for subcontractor compliance, and the prime remains liable for flow-down failures.
- The government retains the right to terminate for convenience and to direct in-scope changes; the contractor must continue performance and seek an equitable adjustment rather than stop work (FAR 52.243, 52.249).
- A contractor claim against the government must run through the Contract Disputes Act process — written claim to the contracting officer, certification for claims over $100,000, and a final decision — before Tucker Act / Board of Contract Appeals jurisdiction attaches; the government's sovereign immunity is waived only by an express statutory waiver, strictly construed.
- Knowingly false claims, certifications, or size/status representations trigger the False Claims Act ($14,308–$28,619 per claim plus treble damages, 2025 rate carried into 2026) and may trigger suspension/debarment and criminal liability; no contract term can disclaim this.
- Executive Order 11246 (race/sex/national-origin affirmative action for contractors) was revoked by EO 14173 (Jan. 21, 2025); only the statutory Section 503 (disability) and VEVRAA (protected veteran) affirmative-action obligations survive. Do not advise that EO 11246 obligations still apply.
