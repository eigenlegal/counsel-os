## Tcpa

# Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)

## Applicability

This sub-file applies whenever a matter involves telemarketing calls, marketing text messages (SMS/MMS), automated dialing systems (ATDS), prerecorded or artificial voice messages, robocalls, do-not-call registry compliance, consent-to-contact clauses in contracts, lead generation that involves phone or text outreach, or any agreement allocating responsibility for TCPA compliance between parties (e.g., vendor agreements, lead buyer/seller contracts).

## Key Requirements

### Statutory Framework (47 U.S.C. § 227)

- **What**: The TCPA restricts the use of automated telephone equipment to make calls or send text messages. It prohibits (1) calls using an automatic telephone dialing system (ATDS) or prerecorded/artificial voice to wireless numbers without prior express consent, (2) prerecorded/artificial voice calls to residential landlines without prior express consent, and (3) unsolicited fax advertisements.
- **Threshold**: The statute applies to any call or text message. A single non-compliant call or text is actionable. The TCPA does not require a pattern of conduct or minimum number of contacts.
- **Consequence**: $500 per violation (per call or text). Trebled to $1,500 per violation for willful or knowing violations. Private right of action. No statutory cap on aggregate damages. Class actions are common and can result in exposure of hundreds of millions of dollars.

### ATDS Definition (Post-Facebook v. Duguid, 2021)

- **What**: The Supreme Court in Facebook, Inc. v. Duguid, 593 U.S. 395 (2021), narrowed the definition of an ATDS. An ATDS must have the capacity to (1) store or produce telephone numbers using a random or sequential number generator and (2) dial such numbers. Equipment that merely stores and dials telephone numbers — such as a typical smartphone or CRM with a click-to-dial feature — is not an ATDS.
- **Threshold**: The key question is whether the device uses a random or sequential number generator to either store or produce the numbers it calls. Systems that dial from pre-existing contact lists without random/sequential generation do not qualify as an ATDS under the narrowed federal definition.
- **Consequence**: The narrowed ATDS definition reduces federal TCPA liability for calls/texts made from curated contact lists, but does NOT eliminate liability for prerecorded voice messages or for violations of the do-not-call provisions, which do not require ATDS use.

### Prior Express Written Consent (Marketing Calls/Texts)

- **What**: Telemarketing calls or texts using an ATDS or prerecorded voice to wireless numbers require "prior express written consent" under FCC regulations (47 C.F.R. § 64.1200(a)(2)).
- **Threshold**: Prior express written consent must be (1) in writing (including electronic signatures under E-SIGN), (2) signed by the consumer, (3) include the telephone number to which calls may be placed, (4) include a clear disclosure that the consumer is authorizing telemarketing calls/texts using an ATDS or prerecorded voice, and (5) state that consent is not required as a condition of purchase. Consent obtained as a condition of sale is not valid consent. Consent is revocable at any time through any reasonable means.
- **Consequence**: Calls or texts without valid prior express written consent are actionable at $500-$1,500 per call/text. The burden of proving consent rests on the caller.

### Prior Express Consent (Informational Calls/Texts)

- **What**: Non-marketing calls or texts using an ATDS to wireless numbers (e.g., appointment reminders, account notifications, delivery alerts) require "prior express consent" — a lower standard than prior express written consent.
- **Threshold**: Prior express consent may be implied from the consumer providing their phone number in the context of the transaction. For example, providing a phone number when scheduling an appointment implies consent to receive appointment-related calls/texts. Consent must be specific to the type of communication — giving a number for shipping updates does not imply consent for marketing messages.
- **Consequence**: Calls or texts exceeding the scope of consent (e.g., marketing messages sent under informational consent) are actionable at $500-$1,500 per violation.

### Do-Not-Call Requirements

- **What**: The TCPA established the National Do-Not-Call Registry (administered by the FTC) and requires telemarketers to maintain internal do-not-call lists. Telemarketers may not call numbers on the National DNC Registry unless they have prior express written consent or an established business relationship.
- **Threshold**: An established business relationship (EBR) exemption lasts 18 months from the last transaction or 3 months from the last inquiry. Companies must honor internal do-not-call requests within 30 days. The National DNC Registry must be checked at least every 31 days.
- **Consequence**: Violations are actionable at $500-$1,500 per call. The FTC can impose civil penalties up to $50,120 per violation under the Telemarketing Sales Rule.

### Prerecorded Voice Messages (Robocalls)

- **What**: Prerecorded or artificial voice calls to residential landlines require prior express consent (with limited exemptions). Prerecorded or artificial voice calls to wireless numbers for marketing require prior express written consent.
- **Threshold**: Exemptions exist for calls made for emergency purposes, calls made with prior express consent of the called party, calls made by or on behalf of tax-exempt nonprofit organizations, calls delivering health care messages (subject to HIPAA compliance), and calls from package delivery services with relevant information.
- **Consequence**: $500-$1,500 per call. FCC enforcement actions can impose forfeiture penalties of up to $23,727 per call, with a cap of $118,632 for a single continuing violation.

### State Mini-TCPA Laws

#### Florida Telephone Solicitation Act (Fla. Stat. § 501.059)

- **What**: Florida enacted a significantly stricter telephone solicitation law (effective July 2021) that goes beyond the federal TCPA. It defines ATDS more broadly than the federal definition post-Duguid, restricts calls and texts to Florida numbers, and requires prior express written consent for all telephonic sales calls.
- **Threshold**: Written consent must include a "clear and conspicuous" disclosure, be signed and dated, and specifically authorize the caller. The law restricts calls to the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. local time. No more than 3 calls in a 24-hour period per person. Consent expires 18 months after given. "Telephonic sales call" is broadly defined.
- **Consequence**: $500 per violation, trebled to $1,500 for willful violations. Private right of action with attorney's fees.

#### Oklahoma Telephone Solicitation Act (24 Okla. Stat. § 1101 et seq.)

- **What**: Requires prior express written consent for automated calls and texts to Oklahoma numbers. Broadly defines automated systems.
- **Threshold**: Written consent must specifically authorize automated calls or texts and be obtained in compliance with state requirements.
- **Consequence**: $500-$1,500 per violation with private right of action.

#### Washington Automatic Dialing and Announcing Device Statute (RCW 80.36.400)

- **What**: Restricts use of automatic dialing and announcing devices. Requires the called party to consent before the message is delivered.
- **Threshold**: Live operator must introduce the call and obtain consent before playing a prerecorded message. Calls restricted to 8:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m.
- **Consequence**: Knowing violations are a misdemeanor. Civil remedies available under Washington Consumer Protection Act.

### Consent Revocation

- **What**: The FCC has confirmed that consumers may revoke consent through any reasonable means, including orally, in writing, or by any standard method within a reasonable time. Callers cannot restrict revocation to a single method (e.g., "text STOP" only).
- **Threshold**: Any clearly expressed desire to stop receiving calls or texts constitutes revocation. The caller must honor revocation within a reasonable time, generally interpreted as within a few days. The caller cannot impose unreasonable barriers to revocation.
- **Consequence**: Calls or texts made after valid revocation are actionable at $500-$1,500 per violation.

## Interaction with Other Areas

- **Consumer Protection — State Laws**: State mini-TCPA laws (Florida, Oklahoma, Washington, and others) may impose stricter requirements than the federal TCPA, including broader ATDS definitions, shorter consent expiration periods, and additional restrictions. Always apply the strictest applicable standard.
- **Consumer Protection — CAN-SPAM**: Text messages that constitute commercial electronic messages may also trigger CAN-SPAM requirements, though the FCC has primary jurisdiction over wireless marketing messages under the TCPA.
- **Data Privacy**: Phone numbers are personal data under CCPA, GDPR, and other privacy frameworks. TCPA consent management intersects with privacy consent management and data minimization obligations. Consent records must be retained and producible.
- **Consumer Protection — Dark Patterns**: Consent obtained through dark patterns (pre-checked boxes, misleading disclosures) may not constitute valid prior express written consent under the TCPA.
- **Litigation**: TCPA class actions are among the most common consumer class actions filed in federal court. Exposure is driven by per-violation statutory damages multiplied across the class. Cases frequently settle for tens of millions of dollars.
