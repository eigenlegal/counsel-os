# Automatic Renewal and Subscription Billing

## Overview
Federal and state laws regulate automatic renewal, continuous service, and negative option offers to protect consumers from being charged for products or services they did not knowingly agree to continue. These laws impose specific disclosure, consent, and cancellation requirements on businesses offering subscription-based or recurring billing services.

## Key Requirements
- **ROSCA (Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act, 15 U.S.C. 8401-8405):** Prohibits charging consumers for goods or services sold through negative option features unless: (1) all material terms are clearly and conspicuously disclosed before the transaction, (2) the consumer's informed affirmative consent is obtained, and (3) a simple mechanism for stopping recurring charges is provided.
- **FTC Negative Option Rule (16 C.F.R. Part 425):** Governs pre-notification negative option plans (book-of-the-month type offers). Requires clear disclosure of terms and easy rejection mechanisms.
- **FTC Click-to-Cancel Rule (finalized 2024):** Requires businesses to make cancellation at least as easy as sign-up. Prohibits requiring consumers to speak with a live agent if they signed up online. Requires annual reminders for auto-renewing subscriptions.
- **California Automatic Renewal Law (ARL, Bus. & Prof. Code 17600-17606):** Requires clear and conspicuous disclosure of auto-renewal terms, affirmative consent (recorded or documented), acknowledgment/confirmation after sign-up, easy online cancellation for online sign-ups, and free gift/trial disclosures. Violations render the auto-renewal provision void and unenforceable.
- **New York General Business Law 527:** Requires clear disclosure and easy cancellation for auto-renewal consumer contracts.
- **Illinois Automatic Contract Renewal Act (815 ILCS 601):** Requires written disclosure of auto-renewal terms and consumer acknowledgment. Applies to contracts with automatic renewal provisions.
- **Other state laws:** Virginia, Oregon, Vermont, North Carolina, Georgia, and many other states have enacted auto-renewal laws with varying requirements. The strictest standards should be applied when operating nationally.
- **Common requirements across jurisdictions:** (1) Clear and conspicuous pre-purchase disclosure of recurring charges, renewal terms, and cancellation procedures, (2) affirmative opt-in consent separate from general terms acceptance, (3) confirmation/acknowledgment of auto-renewal terms, (4) easy-to-use cancellation mechanisms, (5) disclosure of free trial conversion terms and dates.

## Common Contract Issues
- Auto-renewal terms buried in general terms of service rather than presented clearly and conspicuously before the billing commitment.
- Failure to obtain affirmative consent specifically for the auto-renewal provision separate from general terms acceptance.
- Cancellation procedures that are more burdensome than the sign-up process (e.g., requiring phone calls when sign-up was online).
- Free trial terms that do not clearly disclose when the trial ends, what the charges will be, and how to cancel before conversion.
- Confirmation/acknowledgment emails or screens that do not include all required disclosures (renewal terms, cancellation procedures, contact information).
- Price increase provisions in auto-renewal contracts that do not provide adequate notice before the new rate takes effect.
- Contracts that renew for the same term as the initial term rather than converting to month-to-month (many state laws require disclosure of renewal term length).
- B2B auto-renewal contracts that may inadvertently trigger consumer protection requirements if the contracting party qualifies as a "consumer" under applicable law.

## Interaction with Other Areas
- **Consumer Protection (FTC):** ROSCA is enforced by the FTC under its Section 5 authority; FTC enforcement actions frequently target auto-renewal practices as deceptive or unfair.
- **Consumer Protection (State):** State auto-renewal laws supplement general state UDAP statutes; violations may trigger both auto-renewal-specific penalties and general consumer protection remedies.
- **Data Privacy:** Auto-renewal disclosures and cancellation mechanisms must be accessible and functional, which may interact with cookie consent, account deletion rights, and data retention obligations.
- **IP and Technology:** SaaS and digital subscription agreements must comply with auto-renewal laws even in B2B contexts when the customer may qualify as a consumer. Subscription billing logic must be designed to facilitate compliance with disclosure and cancellation requirements.
- **Litigation:** Auto-renewal violations are a frequent basis for consumer class actions, particularly in California under the ARL's void-and-unenforceable remedy.
