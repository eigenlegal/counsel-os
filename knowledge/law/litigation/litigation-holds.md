# Litigation Holds and Document Preservation

## Overview
A litigation hold (also called a legal hold or preservation notice) is a directive to preserve all documents and electronically stored information (ESI) potentially relevant to anticipated, threatened, or pending litigation, investigation, or regulatory proceeding. The duty to preserve arises before formal litigation commences and failure to comply can result in severe spoliation sanctions.

## Key Requirements
- **Duty to preserve trigger:** The duty to preserve arises when litigation is "reasonably anticipated" — not merely when a complaint is filed. Common triggers include: receipt of a demand letter, threat of litigation, filing of a complaint, receipt of a subpoena or CID, internal discovery of facts likely to lead to litigation, regulatory inquiry, and media reports or whistleblower complaints suggesting potential claims.
- **Scope of preservation:** Must encompass all documents and ESI that are potentially relevant to the claims or defenses in the anticipated matter. Includes: email, text messages, chat/Slack messages, documents (paper and electronic), databases, backup tapes (in some circumstances), social media, voicemail, metadata, and ephemeral messaging (Signal, disappearing messages).
- **Litigation hold notice:** Written notice must be issued to all employees (and relevant third parties) who may possess relevant information. The notice should: identify the matter, describe the types of information to be preserved, instruct recipients to suspend routine document destruction and auto-delete policies, identify specific data sources to be preserved, provide a point of contact for questions, and be reissued periodically with reminders.
- **Key custodian identification:** Identify all individuals likely to possess relevant information (key custodians), including current and former employees, executives, and relevant third parties. Interview key custodians to understand their data sources and practices.
- **Suspension of routine destruction:** Routine document retention and destruction policies must be suspended for relevant categories of documents. Auto-delete features on email, messaging platforms, and document management systems must be disabled for custodians and relevant data sources.
- **ESI preservation:** Specific attention to: email archives, cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, SharePoint), collaboration platforms (Slack, Teams, Confluence), mobile devices, personal devices used for work (BYOD), CRM systems, financial systems, HR systems, and engineering repositories (Git, JIRA).
- **Spoliation sanctions (FRCP Rule 37(e)):** For ESI that should have been preserved: (1) if lost due to failure to take reasonable steps and the ESI cannot be restored or replaced, the court may order measures no greater than necessary to cure the prejudice; (2) if the party acted with intent to deprive the opposing party of the ESI, the court may presume the information was unfavorable, instruct the jury accordingly, or enter default judgment. State rules vary.
- **Third-party preservation:** Companies may need to issue preservation notices to third parties (vendors, service providers, cloud storage providers) that hold relevant data. Contracts should include provisions for third-party cooperation with litigation holds.
- **Documentation and defensibility:** Maintain a record of all litigation hold activities: the decision-making process, hold notices sent and acknowledged, custodian interviews, data sources identified and preserved, and ongoing compliance monitoring. This documentation is essential to demonstrate good faith if preservation adequacy is challenged.

## Common Contract Issues
- Cloud service and SaaS agreements that do not provide adequate data retention, export, or legal hold capabilities for the customer's data.
- Vendor agreements that do not require the vendor to cooperate with litigation holds and document preservation requests.
- Data retention and destruction clauses that conflict with litigation hold obligations — contracts should acknowledge that retention schedules are subject to suspension for legal hold purposes.
- Employment agreements and separation agreements that do not include document return and preservation obligations.
- Bring-your-own-device (BYOD) policies that do not address the company's ability to preserve and collect relevant data from personal devices.
- Messaging platform terms of service that auto-delete messages and do not support preservation exports.
- Indemnification provisions that do not address costs of litigation hold compliance and e-discovery.
- Data processing agreements that address the processor's obligations regarding data preservation and legal hold when the controller is subject to litigation.

## Interaction with Other Areas
- **Litigation (Subpoenas):** Receipt of a subpoena triggers a litigation hold for all materials within the scope of the subpoena.
- **Litigation (Regulatory Inquiries):** Regulatory investigations trigger preservation obligations similar to litigation holds.
- **Litigation (Demand Letters):** Receipt or sending of a demand letter may trigger the duty to preserve if it makes litigation reasonably foreseeable.
- **Data Privacy:** Litigation hold obligations may conflict with data privacy rights (right to deletion under GDPR, CCPA). Legal hold obligations generally take priority over routine deletion but require careful analysis when specific deletion requests are received during a hold.
- **IP and Technology:** SaaS and cloud providers must support litigation hold functionality; technology systems must be configured to suspend auto-deletion.
- **Employment:** Departing employees must be reminded of preservation obligations; devices and accounts must be preserved before deprovisioning. BYOD policies must address preservation.
