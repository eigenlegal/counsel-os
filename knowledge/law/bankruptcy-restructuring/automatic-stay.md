## Automatic Stay

# Automatic Stay

## Applicability

Applies immediately upon the filing of a bankruptcy petition under any chapter (7, 11, 12, or 13). The automatic stay under Section 362 of the Bankruptcy Code is one of the most fundamental protections for debtors, halting virtually all collection and enforcement actions against the debtor and property of the estate.

## Key Requirements

### Scope of the Stay (Section 362(a))

- **Actions Stayed** / The stay prohibits: (1) commencement or continuation of lawsuits against the debtor, (2) enforcement of judgments, (3) acts to obtain possession of estate property, (4) acts to create, perfect, or enforce liens, (5) setoff of pre-petition debts. / **Consequence**: Actions taken in violation of the stay are void (majority view) or voidable (minority view, including the Second Circuit); the distinction matters for whether the violating party can cure by returning to the status quo.
- **Property of the Estate** / The stay protects all property of the estate (Section 541), which includes all legal and equitable interests of the debtor as of the petition date, wherever located. / **Consequence**: Property of the estate is broadly defined — includes causes of action, contract rights, intellectual property, and contingent interests. The stay extends to property even if held by third parties.
- **Co-Debtor Stay** / In Chapter 12 and 13 cases only, the stay extends to co-debtors (guarantors, co-signers) on consumer debts. / **Consequence**: In Chapter 7 and 11, there is NO co-debtor stay; creditors may pursue guarantors and co-obligors notwithstanding the debtor's bankruptcy filing.

### Exceptions to the Stay (Section 362(b))

- **Police and Regulatory Power** / Government exercise of police or regulatory power is not stayed, including criminal proceedings, tax audits, and environmental enforcement. / **Consequence**: EPA can pursue environmental cleanup orders against a debtor notwithstanding the stay (but money judgments for pre-petition contamination ARE stayed). The distinction between regulatory action and money collection is heavily litigated.
- **Family Law** / Domestic support obligations (child support, alimony), paternity proceedings, and domestic violence proceedings are not stayed. / **Consequence**: Family courts retain jurisdiction over these matters; income withholding orders for domestic support continue in effect.
- **Perfection of Security Interests** / Creditors may perfect a security interest within the applicable post-petition grace period (typically **30 days** after the petition) under Section 362(b)(3). / **Consequence**: A creditor who files a UCC continuation statement or mortgage within the grace period preserves its priority; missing the window may result in the lien being avoided as unperfected.
- **Setoff in Financial Contracts** / Safe-harbored financial contracts (swaps, repos, securities contracts) are exempt from the automatic stay under Sections 555-561. / **Consequence**: Counterparties to financial contracts may exercise contractual rights of setoff, netting, and liquidation without violating the stay; this exception is a critical protection for financial markets.

### Relief from the Automatic Stay (Section 362(d))

- **For Cause (Section 362(d)(1))** / Court may grant relief from stay "for cause, including the lack of adequate protection." / **Consequence**: "Cause" is not defined in the Code and is determined on a case-by-case basis. Lack of adequate protection (declining collateral value without protection payments) is the most common ground. Relief may be granted as to specific property or specific creditors.
- **Lack of Equity + Not Necessary for Reorganization (Section 362(d)(2))** / Relief granted if the debtor has no equity in the property AND the property is not necessary for an effective reorganization. / **Consequence**: Both elements must be satisfied; if the debtor has even minimal equity, this ground fails. If the property is necessary for reorganization (e.g., the debtor's primary operating facility), relief under (d)(2) is unavailable.
- **Single Asset Real Estate (Section 362(d)(3))** / In single asset real estate cases, the debtor must file a plan with a reasonable possibility of confirmation or commence monthly payments equal to the non-default contract rate of interest within **90 days** of the petition date (or 30 days after the court determines the case is a SARE case). / **Consequence**: Failure to meet the 90-day deadline results in automatic relief from stay for the secured creditor; this provision prevents debtor abuse of Chapter 11 to delay foreclosure on passive real estate investments.
- **Adequate Protection** / Forms include: periodic cash payments, additional or replacement liens, and "indubitable equivalent" of the creditor's interest. / **Consequence**: If adequate protection proves insufficient (collateral declines in value despite protection payments), the creditor has a superpriority administrative claim under Section 507(b); this claim has priority over all other administrative expenses.

### Willful Violation of the Stay

- **Standard** / A violation is "willful" under Section 362(k) if the creditor knew of the stay and intentionally took the action — knowledge that the action violated the stay is NOT required. / **Consequence**: Individuals injured by willful violations may recover actual damages, costs, attorney's fees, and in appropriate cases, **punitive damages**. Corporate debtors may also recover damages in some circuits.
- **Damages** / Actual damages include: loss of property, emotional distress (individuals), lost business opportunity, and attorney's fees incurred to enforce the stay. / **Consequence**: Punitive damages for egregious violations have ranged from **$25,000 to $1 million+** in reported cases; repeated violations or violations in bad faith increase the likelihood of punitive awards.
- **Contempt** / Courts may hold stay violators in civil contempt under Section 105(a). / **Consequence**: Contempt sanctions include per-day fines (commonly **$1,000-$10,000/day**) until the violation is cured, attorney's fees, and in extreme cases, imprisonment for individuals.

### Duration and Termination

- **Chapter 7** / Stay terminates as to property of the estate when the property is no longer property of the estate (abandonment or closing of the case); stay as to the debtor terminates at discharge or closing. / **Consequence**: Property abandoned by the trustee is no longer protected by the stay; creditors may pursue foreclosure or collection against abandoned property.
- **Chapter 11** / Stay continues until the case is closed, dismissed, or discharge is granted; plan confirmation may modify the stay as to specific property. / **Consequence**: The plan of reorganization may provide for the stay to continue as to certain property or may lift the stay to allow creditors to pursue rights against specific collateral.
- **Serial Filers** / If a debtor files a second case within **1 year** of dismissal, the stay terminates after **30 days** unless extended for cause. Third filing within 1 year: **no stay at all** unless the debtor demonstrates the filing is in good faith. / **Consequence**: Serial filing provisions (Section 362(c)(3) and (4)) prevent debtor abuse; creditors should monitor for serial filings and promptly move for stay termination if applicable.

## Interaction with Other Areas

- **Environmental & ESG** — Government environmental enforcement actions are exempt from the stay under the police power exception, but money judgments for pre-petition environmental liability are stayed
- **Employment** — Employee wage claims may be partially exempt from the stay; WARN Act violations may be litigated
- **Real Estate** — Relief from stay is the primary mechanism for lenders to resume foreclosure in bankruptcy
- **Financial Services** — Financial contract safe harbors provide complete exemption from the automatic stay for qualifying contracts
