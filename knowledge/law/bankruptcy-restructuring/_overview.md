# Bankruptcy & Restructuring Law

## Trigger Conditions

This area applies when the matter involves any of the following:
- **Keywords**: bankruptcy, insolvency, restructuring, Chapter 7, Chapter 11, automatic stay, executory contract, preference, creditor, proof of claim, DIP financing, cramdown, plan of reorganization
- **Document types**: proofs of claim, motions for relief from stay, assumption/rejection notices, DIP financing agreements, plans of reorganization, disclosure statements
- **Transaction types**: distressed acquisitions (363 sales), debtor-in-possession financing, pre-petition credit agreements with bankruptcy-related provisions, supply agreements with insolvent counterparties

## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- The automatic stay arises by operation of law upon the petition filing (Section 362(a)) and halts collection, enforcement, lien, and setoff actions against the debtor and estate property; no contract provision can waive a debtor's stay protection in advance.
- Ipso facto clauses — provisions terminating or modifying a contract solely because of a bankruptcy filing or insolvency — are unenforceable in the assumption/assignment context (Section 365(e)), except for safe-harbored financial contracts (Sections 555-561), where bankruptcy-triggered close-out and termination rights ARE enforceable.
- A bankruptcy plan cannot discharge claims against a nondebtor without the affected claimant's consent; Section 1123(b)(6) does not authorize nonconsensual third-party releases (Harrington v. Purdue Pharma, 603 U.S. 204 (2024)). Section 524(g) asbestos channeling injunctions are a separate statutory exception.
- Priority and dollar-cap figures (wage/benefit, customer-deposit, preference, venue, debt-limit thresholds) are set by statute and adjusted every three years under Section 104; the figure applicable to a case is the one in effect on the petition date, and adjusted amounts do not apply to cases commenced before the adjustment date.
- Subchapter V eligibility depends on a statutory debt limit. The CARES Act $7,500,000 limit expired June 21, 2024 and the limit reverted to the inflation-adjusted Section 101(51D)/1182(1) figure (approximately $3.0M as of the 2022 adjustment); confirm the current limit and any restoration legislation before advising on eligibility.
- Trustee/DIP avoidance powers (preferences under Section 547, fraudulent transfers under Sections 548 and 544(b)) are statutory; a Section 544(b) action against a federal governmental unit is constrained by sovereign immunity as to the nested state-law claim (United States v. Miller, 604 U.S. ___ (2025)).
- Cramdown confirmation over a dissenting class requires that the plan not discriminate unfairly and be fair and equitable, including the absolute priority rule for unsecured classes (Section 1129(b)).
