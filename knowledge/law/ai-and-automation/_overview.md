## Quick Reference

As of June 2026, this area is moving faster than any other: Colorado repealed and replaced its AI Act before it took effect, the EU is finalizing delays to AI Act high-risk deadlines, federal policy reversed course in 2025 (EO 14110 revoked; disparate-impact enforcement deprioritized; a December 2025 EO targets state AI laws for preemption challenges), and the first AI-training fair use rulings landed. Treat every date and enforcement posture in the sub-files as requiring verification.

- **eu-ai-act.md** — EU risk-based AI regulation: prohibited practices, high-risk system obligations (delays pending), GPAI model rules in force since August 2025, fines up to EUR 35M/7% turnover
- **us-state-ai-laws.md** — State AI legislation: Texas TRAIGA, Colorado SB 26-189 ADMT framework, Connecticut CART Act, California frontier-model and transparency laws, Illinois employment AI, NYC bias audits — plus federal preemption pressure
- **algorithmic-accountability.md** — Bias testing frameworks, impact assessments, NIST AI RMF, withdrawn-but-still-instructive EEOC guidance, shifted federal enforcement posture
- **training-data.md** — Copyright and fair use for training data (Bartz, Kadrey, Ross), the lawful-acquisition vs. piracy line, data licensing, web scraping, GDPR lawful basis
- **model-ownership.md** — AI output copyrightability (Thaler affirmed on appeal 2025), human authorship requirement, fine-tuned model IP, trade secret protection, patent eligibility
- **deepfakes-synthetic-media.md** — TAKE IT DOWN Act platform removal duties (binding May 2026), state deepfake laws and their First Amendment/§230 setbacks, EU AI Act disclosure rules, provenance mandates
- **automated-decision-making.md** — GDPR Art. 22 rights, ECOA fair lending, FCRA adverse action, Title VII (post-guidance-withdrawal), state human-review mandates, OMB M-25-21 for federal agencies
