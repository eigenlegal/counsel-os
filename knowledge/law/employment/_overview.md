## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- Minimum wage and overtime requirements under FLSA and state law cannot be waived by agreement between employer and employee.
- Anti-discrimination and anti-harassment protections under Title VII, ADA, ADEA, and state laws are non-waivable and cannot be prospectively released (though past claims can be released with adequate consideration and knowing/voluntary consent).
- WARN Act notice requirements (60 days for covered plant closings and mass layoffs) cannot be shortened by contract.
- Workers' compensation coverage is mandatory in nearly all states and cannot be waived by agreement.
- Employee classification (exempt vs. non-exempt, employee vs. contractor) is determined by the economic reality/totality of circumstances under applicable law, not by contract labels.
- NLRA Section 7 rights (to organize, engage in concerted activity) cannot be waived, and employer policies that chill protected activity are unlawful.
- ADEA release requirements (OWBPA) mandate specific provisions including a 21-day consideration period (45 days for group terminations), 7-day revocation period, and advice-to-consult-counsel language.
- OSHA general duty clause requires employers to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards likely to cause death or serious physical harm.
- I-9 verification must be completed within 3 business days of the employee's first day of work; no exceptions by contract.
- ACA employer shared responsibility applies to all applicable large employers (50+ FTEs) and cannot be contracted around.
- ERISA fiduciary duties cannot be disclaimed or indemnified against by plan sponsors.
- COBRA election rights (60-day election period, 18/36-month continuation) are statutory minimums that cannot be reduced.
