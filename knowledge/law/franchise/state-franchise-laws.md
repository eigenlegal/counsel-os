# State Franchise Laws

## Applicability

Applies to franchisors offering or selling franchises in states that require franchise registration, impose additional disclosure requirements, or regulate business opportunities more broadly. While the FTC Franchise Rule applies nationwide, state franchise laws add registration requirements, merit review, and enforcement mechanisms that vary significantly by jurisdiction.

## Key Requirements

### Registration States

- **14 Registration States** / California, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Virginia, Washington, and Wisconsin require franchise registration before offering or selling franchises / **Consequence**: Offering or selling a franchise without registration = violation; state may impose stop orders, rescission, penalties; franchisor and its officers may be personally liable. NOTE: The District of Columbia is NOT a franchise registration state (no franchise registration or disclosure statute); confirm a state's current status before relying on this list. Separately, several states (Connecticut, Maine, North Carolina, South Carolina) impose FDD filing/disclosure only when the franchisor's principal marks are not federally registered.
- **Filing Review** / State examiners review FDD for completeness, accuracy, and compliance; may issue comment letters requiring amendments / **Timeline**: Initial review typically 30-90 days; comment responses extend timeline; some states require pre-effective review / **Consequence**: Cannot sell in the state until registration is effective; selling during review period = violation
- **Annual Renewal** / Most states require annual renewal of registration, typically 30-120 days after fiscal year end (concurrent with FDD update) / **Consequence**: Lapsed registration = cannot sell; must file renewal before expiration or cease sales
- **Effectiveness** / Registration effective upon state approval (or deemed effective after review period in some states); some states allow registration by coordination with other states / **Consequence**: Must track effectiveness dates; offering before effective date = violation

### Material Change Amendments

- **Trigger** / Any material change to FDD items requires amendment filing within prescribed period / **Timeline**: Most states require filing within 30 days of material change; some require pre-sale filing before using amended FDD / **Consequence**: Selling with outdated FDD = violation; even if FTC-compliant, state amendment timing may differ
- **Material Changes** / Changes to: fees, litigation, bankruptcy, financial statements, officers, business model, territory, and any information that would reasonably affect a prospective franchisee's decision / **Consequence**: Failure to identify material change = continuing violation for each franchise sold with stale disclosure

### State-Specific Requirements

#### California
- **Registration** / Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI); California Franchise Investment Law (Corp Code 31000-31516) / **Consequence**: Among the most rigorous review processes; examiners may require substantive changes to FDD
- **Franchise Relations** / California Franchise Relations Act (Bus & Prof Code 20000-20043) provides relationship protections (see `relationship-laws.md`) / **Consequence**: Cannot terminate, refuse to renew, or restrict transfer without good cause and proper notice
- **AB 676 (eff. Jan 1, 2023)** / Amended both the CFIL and the CFRA for agreements entered into, amended, or renewed on/after Jan 1, 2023: voids FDD/acknowledgment/questionnaire provisions that disclaim the franchisee's reliance on franchisor representations or the FDD; bars discrimination in granting franchises or financial assistance based on protected characteristics; restricts post-termination/non-renewal offsets to amounts the franchisee agrees to or that are finally adjudicated; bars requiring a general release or modifying terms in exchange for emergency-related assistance; sets a 60-day window for the franchisor to approve/disapprove a transfer and makes the reasonableness of a transfer denial a question of fact / **Consequence**: Standard FDD reliance-disclaimer and questionnaire language is unenforceable in California; review transfer, release, and offset clauses against AB 676

#### New York
- **Registration** / Department of Law, Investor Protection Bureau / **Consequence**: Detailed review; frequently issues comment letters on Item 19 and financial statement issues
- **Surety Bond** / May require surety bond as condition of registration if franchisor's financial condition is weak / **Consequence**: Bond requirement can be substantial; failure to post = registration denied

#### Illinois
- **Registration** / Office of Attorney General; Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act (815 ILCS 705) / **Consequence**: Broad definition of franchise; captures arrangements some other states might not

#### Minnesota
- **Registration** / Department of Commerce; Minnesota Franchise Act (Minn. Stat. 80C) / **Consequence**: Extensive relationship protections; restricts certain franchise agreement terms (see `relationship-laws.md`)

#### Virginia
- **Registration** / State Corporation Commission, Division of Securities and Retail Franchising; Virginia Retail Franchising Act (Va. Code 13.1-557 et seq.) / **Consequence**: Registration required before offering or selling
- **HB 69 / SB 240 (signed Apr 13, 2026; eff. July 1, 2026)** / For franchise agreements offered or entered into after July 1, 2026: (1) makes it unlawful to offer or enter a franchise agreement containing a post-termination/post-expiration noncompete restricting a franchisee from offering, selling, or distributing competing goods or services at retail — with a narrow exception allowing up to a 2-year restriction where the franchisee sells the franchise at a mutually agreed price to a third party or back to the franchisor; and (2) requires that any franchise agreement contemplating a place of business in Virginia be governed by Virginia law (no out-of-state choice-of-law) / **Consequence**: Prospective only — does not impair agreements entered into, extended, or modified on or before July 1, 2026; revise VA franchise forms to drop post-term noncompetes and out-of-state governing-law clauses (see `franchise-agreements.md` and `relationship-laws.md`)

### Filing Fee States (Non-Registration)

- **States with Filing Requirements** / Several states require notice filings or exemption filings without full registration review (e.g., Connecticut, Florida, Kentucky, Nebraska, Oregon, Texas, Utah) / **Consequence**: Filing requirements vary; some require filing before first sale; others require annual notice; failure to file = potential violation
- **Business Opportunity States** / Some states regulate franchises through broader business opportunity statutes rather than dedicated franchise laws (e.g., Connecticut, Florida, Maine, Nebraska) / **Consequence**: Business opportunity laws may have different definitions and requirements; must analyze whether arrangement triggers both franchise and business opportunity coverage

### Exemptions by State

- **Large Franchisee/Sophisticated Investor** / Many states exempt sales to experienced, well-capitalized franchisees (e.g., net worth >$5M, entity >5 years in business) / **Consequence**: Exemption must be properly claimed; reliance on exemption without meeting criteria = violation
- **Existing Franchisee** / Sales to existing franchisees for additional units or renewal may be exempt in some states / **Consequence**: Exemption conditions vary; must confirm applicability in each state
- **Minimal Sales** / Some states exempt first sale or very limited number of sales in the state / **Consequence**: Very narrow; not practical for franchise expansion
- **Large Investment** / Some states exempt transactions where the franchisee's initial investment exceeds a high threshold / **Consequence**: Thresholds vary by state; must confirm current amounts

### Franchise Broker Registration

- **State Requirements** / Several states require franchise brokers (persons who sell franchises on behalf of the franchisor) to register or be identified in the FDD / **Consequence**: Using unregistered brokers in states requiring registration = violation; franchisor may be liable for broker's actions
- **FDD Disclosure** / FTC Rule requires disclosure of franchise brokers and their compensation in the FDD / **Consequence**: Failure to disclose broker relationships = FDD deficiency

### State Enforcement and Remedies

- **State AG/Regulator Authority** / State franchise administrators may: issue stop orders, revoke/suspend registration, deny renewal, impose civil penalties, seek injunctive relief / **Consequence**: State enforcement is independent of FTC; states may be more aggressive on relationship issues
- **Private Right of Action** / Most state franchise statutes provide franchisees with private right of action for disclosure violations: rescission, damages, attorney's fees / **Consequence**: Franchisee can seek rescission of franchise agreement and recovery of investment if disclosure requirements were violated
- **Personal Liability** / Many states impose personal liability on franchisor officers and directors who authorize or participate in violations / **Consequence**: Individual liability creates strong incentive for compliance; D&O insurance may not cover franchise regulation violations
