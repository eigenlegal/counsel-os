## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- A franchisor must furnish a compliant Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD) with all 23 required items at least 14 calendar days before the prospective franchisee signs a binding agreement or makes any payment (FTC Franchise Rule, 16 CFR Part 436); a sale in violation is voidable and exposes the franchisor to FTC enforcement.
- Financial performance representations may be made only in Item 19 with a reasonable written basis; earnings claims outside the FDD — including informal statements by sales personnel — violate the FTC Rule regardless of any disclaimer.
- In the 14 franchise registration states (CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI), a franchisor may not offer or sell a franchise until its FDD registration is effective; selling during the review period or with a lapsed registration is a violation and may carry personal liability for officers.
- State franchise relationship-law protections (good cause for termination/non-renewal, notice-and-cure, anti-waiver) cannot be waived by contract; agreement terms less protective than the governing state law are void in that state.
- For franchise agreements offered or entered into in Virginia after July 1, 2026, post-term noncompetes are prohibited (narrow 2-year exception only on sale of the franchise) and the agreement must be governed by Virginia law (Va. Retail Franchising Act, HB 69/SB 240).
- Anti-fraud and FTC Act Section 5 prohibitions apply to all franchise sales; undisclosed fees and contract clauses that bar a franchisee from reporting law violations to the government are treated by the FTC as unlawful (July 2024 staff guidance and Policy Statement — confirm current FTC posture before relying).
