## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- Data processing agreements are legally required under GDPR Article 28 and most US state privacy laws when a processor handles personal data on behalf of a controller.
- Data breach notification obligations are statutory and cannot be contractually waived or extended beyond legally mandated timelines.
- Data subject rights (access, deletion, correction, portability) are statutory rights that cannot be waived by contract.
- Cross-border data transfers require a valid transfer mechanism (SCCs, adequacy decision, BCRs, or certified framework).
- Children's data collection requires verifiable parental consent under COPPA for children under 13.
- Consent for processing must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous under GDPR; pre-checked boxes or bundled consent are invalid.
