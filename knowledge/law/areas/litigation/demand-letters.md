# Demand Letters and Pre-Litigation Communications

## Overview
Demand letters and cease-and-desist communications are formal pre-litigation tools used to assert legal claims, demand specific action or compensation, and create a record of the dispute. They serve both as a potential catalyst for resolution and as groundwork for litigation if negotiations fail. Many claims have statutory notice requirements that must be satisfied before filing suit.

## Key Requirements
- **Purpose and strategy:** Demand letters serve to put the recipient on formal notice of a claim, satisfy pre-suit notice requirements where applicable, preserve evidence of demand and refusal, establish a timeline for the dispute, and create a record for potential fee-shifting or damages enhancement.
- **Statutory notice requirements:** Many causes of action require pre-suit notice or demand as a condition precedent to filing: breach of warranty (UCC notice of non-conformity), government tort claims (state and federal tort claims acts), insurance coverage disputes (proof of loss), state consumer protection claims (some states require notice and opportunity to cure), construction disputes (many states require pre-suit notice), contractual notice-of-claim provisions (specified form and timing).
- **Cease-and-desist letters:** Commonly used for IP infringement (trademark, copyright, patent), trade secret misappropriation, unfair competition, and contract breaches (non-compete violations). Should identify the specific rights being infringed, the infringing activity, and the required corrective action.
- **Content best practices:** Clearly identify the sender's legal authority, state the factual basis for the claim, cite the legal basis for the claim, specify the relief sought (monetary damages, specific performance, cessation of activity), set a reasonable deadline for response, preserve all claims and rights (no inadvertent waiver), and avoid threats that could constitute extortion or abuse of process.
- **Tone and legal ethics:** Demand letters must comply with professional responsibility rules. Threats of criminal prosecution to gain advantage in a civil matter are prohibited or restricted in many jurisdictions. Communications must be truthful and not misleading.
- **Preservation of claims:** Demand letters should include language reserving all rights and claims; statements in demand letters can be used as admissions if not carefully drafted.

## Common Contract Issues
- Contracts with notice-of-claim provisions that require specific form (written notice), method (certified mail, email to designated address), timing (e.g., within 30 days of discovering the issue), and content (detailed description of claim). Failure to comply may waive or limit claims.
- Indemnification agreements requiring prompt notice to the indemnitor as a condition of coverage — late notice may forfeit indemnification rights.
- Pre-dispute arbitration clauses that require demand or negotiation as a condition precedent to arbitration initiation.
- Settlement communications and their admissibility — FRE 408 and state equivalents generally exclude settlement offers and negotiations from evidence, but the protections have limitations.
- Tolling agreements that may be necessary to preserve claims while negotiations proceed after a demand letter is sent.
- Releases and settlements that arise from demand letter negotiations — must adequately define the scope of released claims and comply with applicable requirements (e.g., OWBPA for age discrimination claims).

## Interaction with Other Areas
- **Litigation (Litigation Holds):** Sending or receiving a demand letter may trigger document preservation obligations if litigation is reasonably anticipated.
- **IP and Technology:** Cease-and-desist letters for IP infringement require careful drafting to avoid creating declaratory judgment jurisdiction in an unfavorable forum.
- **Employment:** Demand letters in employment disputes must account for protected activity (potential retaliation claims), EEOC charge requirements, and OWBPA requirements for age-related claims.
- **Consumer Protection:** Some state consumer protection statutes require a pre-suit demand letter with an opportunity to cure before filing a private action (e.g., Massachusetts Chapter 93A 30-day demand letter requirement).
- **Insurance:** Notice of claim to insurers must be timely and in the form required by the policy; demand letters received should be promptly tendered to applicable insurance carriers.
