# Insurance Coverage Types

## Overview
Commercial insurance programs typically include multiple coverage types designed to address different categories of risk. Understanding the scope, triggers, and limitations of each coverage type is essential for risk management, contract negotiation (insurance requirements), and claims handling. Coverage types are broadly categorized into first-party (covering the insured's own losses) and third-party (covering the insured's liability to others).

## Key Requirements
- **Commercial General Liability (CGL):**
  - Covers third-party claims for bodily injury, property damage, personal injury (libel, slander, invasion of privacy), and advertising injury.
  - Standard ISO form with occurrence trigger (injury must occur during the policy period).
  - Key exclusions: intentional acts, professional services (E&O), pollution, employment practices, contractual liability (except insured contracts), damage to the insured's own work/product.
  - Additional insured endorsements extend coverage to third parties (landlords, clients, project owners) for liability arising from the named insured's operations.
  - Waiver of subrogation endorsements prevent the insurer from pursuing recovery against a designated party after paying a claim.

- **Directors and Officers (D&O) Liability:**
  - Side A: Covers individual directors/officers when the company cannot indemnify them (insolvency, statutory prohibition).
  - Side B: Reimburses the company for indemnification payments made to directors/officers.
  - Side C (entity coverage): Covers the entity itself for securities claims (public companies) or all claims (private companies).
  - Claims-made trigger (claim must be first made during the policy period and reported during the policy period or extended reporting period).
  - Key exclusions: fraud/dishonesty (after adjudication), prior knowledge, pending/prior litigation, insured-vs-insured.

- **Errors and Omissions (E&O) / Professional Liability:**
  - Covers claims arising from professional services, including negligent acts, errors, and omissions in the performance of professional duties.
  - Claims-made trigger. Essential for professional service providers, technology companies, consultants, and financial services firms.
  - Technology E&O covers claims arising from technology products and services (software failures, data loss, system outages).

- **Cyber Liability / Privacy and Network Security:**
  - First-party coverage: data breach response costs (notification, credit monitoring, forensics), business interruption from cyber events, cyber extortion/ransomware, data restoration.
  - Third-party coverage: liability for data breaches, privacy violations, network security failures, and media liability.
  - Claims-made trigger. Increasingly essential for all businesses handling personal data or relying on technology.
  - Key considerations: war/terrorism exclusions (cyber warfare), social engineering fraud coverage, dependent business interruption, regulatory proceeding coverage.

- **Employment Practices Liability (EPLI):**
  - Covers claims by employees for wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, retaliation, wage and hour disputes (if included), and other employment-related claims.
  - Claims-made trigger. Covers the entity and individual insureds (directors, officers, managers, supervisors).

- **Workers' Compensation and Employers' Liability:**
  - Part A (workers' compensation): Statutory no-fault coverage for employee workplace injuries. Benefits include medical expenses, disability payments, rehabilitation, and death benefits.
  - Part B (employers' liability): Covers employer liability for employee injuries not subject to the workers' compensation exclusive remedy (third-party over actions, dual-capacity doctrine, deliberate intent).
  - Mandatory in nearly all states. Monopolistic state fund in a few jurisdictions (Ohio, North Dakota, Washington, Wyoming).

- **Property Insurance:**
  - Covers damage to or loss of the insured's real and personal property from covered perils (fire, theft, windstorm, etc.). Named perils vs. all-risk (special form) policies.
  - Business interruption coverage: lost income and extra expenses due to covered property damage. Dependent/contingent business interruption covers losses from damage to third-party property (suppliers, customers).

- **Umbrella and Excess Liability:**
  - Umbrella: provides additional limits above underlying policies (CGL, auto, employers' liability) and may cover claims that fall within the umbrella's coverage but outside the underlying policies (drop-down coverage) subject to a self-insured retention.
  - Excess: follows form of the underlying policy and provides additional limits only; does not drop down or broaden coverage.

## Common Contract Issues
- Insurance requirements in commercial contracts that do not specify the appropriate coverage types, limits, and endorsements for the risk being transferred.
- Missing or inadequate additional insured endorsement requirements — must specify the ISO form number or equivalent language to ensure the endorsement covers completed operations and ongoing operations.
- Primary and non-contributory requirements that are not supported by appropriate endorsements on the named insured's policy.
- Waiver of subrogation requirements that must be secured by endorsement before a loss occurs; post-loss waivers are generally unenforceable.
- Certificate of insurance provisions that are confused with actual coverage — certificates are informational only and do not modify policy terms or confer coverage.
- Self-insured retention provisions that create gaps between the contract's insurance requirements and the insured's actual coverage.
- Indemnification provisions that are broader than available insurance coverage, creating uninsured exposure.
- Failure to require tail/extended reporting period (ERP) coverage for claims-made policies when the contract terminates.

## Interaction with Other Areas
- **Corporate (Governance):** D&O insurance must align with indemnification and advancement provisions in corporate governance documents and indemnification agreements.
- **Employment:** EPLI and workers' compensation coverage must be maintained in compliance with applicable employment laws. Wage and hour claims are increasingly covered by EPLI endorsements.
- **Data Privacy:** Cyber liability insurance should be coordinated with the company's data privacy compliance program and incident response plan.
- **Litigation:** Insurance coverage is a threshold consideration when evaluating litigation risk and settlement strategy. Duty-to-defend and coverage disputes are a frequent source of litigation between insureds and insurers.
- **IP and Technology:** Technology E&O policies cover claims arising from technology products and services; IP infringement claims may be covered under CGL advertising injury or specialty IP coverage.
- **Consumer Protection:** Product liability claims and consumer class actions implicate CGL and product liability coverage.
