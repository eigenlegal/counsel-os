## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- Fiduciary duties of directors, officers, managers, and controlling stockholders/members cannot be fully eliminated by contract (though Delaware LLCs permit modification to the extent allowed by DRULPA/DLLCA).
- Corporate formalities (e.g., board/stockholder approvals) required by statute for fundamental transactions (mergers, dissolution, sale of all/substantially all assets) must be followed regardless of contractual provisions.
- HSR Act pre-merger notification filing requirements apply when transaction size and party size thresholds are met; closing before HSR clearance is illegal.
- CFIUS review and national security considerations cannot be waived by contract when applicable.
- Tax classification elections (entity classification, S-corp election, QSBS) have statutory deadlines and requirements that override contractual flexibility.
- Annual franchise tax and reporting obligations must be maintained to preserve entity good standing and liability protection.
