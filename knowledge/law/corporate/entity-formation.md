# Entity Formation

## Applicability

Load when any of the following arise: new entity formation, entity conversion or domestication, choice-of-entity analysis, LLC vs. corporation comparison, tax classification elections (check-the-box, S-corp), series LLC structures, professional entity requirements, foreign qualification, or multi-entity structuring.

## Key Requirements

### Entity Type Comparison

- **C Corporation**: Formed under state corporation statutes (DGCL, MBCA). Taxed at entity level (21% federal corporate rate as of 2024) with potential double taxation on distributions. Unlimited number and types of stockholders. Preferred for venture capital and institutional investment. Required formalities: certificate of incorporation, bylaws, organizational resolutions, stock issuance, annual meetings, annual reports, franchise tax.
- **S Corporation**: Tax election (IRS Form 2553) on an eligible corporation. Maximum 100 shareholders, one class of stock (voting differences permitted), only eligible shareholders (individuals, certain trusts, estates -- no partnerships, corporations, or non-resident aliens). Must file Form 2553 within 75 days of formation or by March 15 for calendar-year election. Pass-through taxation eliminates double taxation. Inadvertent termination occurs if eligibility requirements are violated.
- **LLC**: Formed under state LLC acts. Default tax treatment as partnership (multi-member) or disregarded entity (single-member). May elect C-corp or S-corp taxation via IRS Form 8832 (check-the-box) or Form 2553. Maximum governance flexibility through operating agreement. Fewer mandatory formalities. Delaware LLCs permit broad modification of fiduciary duties.
- **Limited Partnership (LP)**: One or more general partners (unlimited liability, management authority) and limited partners (limited liability, no management authority). Common for PE/VC fund structures. Tax-transparent. Governed by partnership agreement and state LP acts (DRULPA in Delaware).
- **General Partnership**: Formed by association of two or more persons in business for profit. No filing required in most states. All partners have unlimited joint and several liability. Governed by RUPA and partnership agreement.

### Formation Requirements by State

- **Delaware**: Certificate of Incorporation (corporations) or Certificate of Formation (LLCs) filed with Division of Corporations. Filing fee: $89 (corp) or $90 (LLC). Annual franchise tax required -- corporations: minimum $400, calculated by authorized shares or assumed par value capital method. LLCs: flat $300 annual tax. No requirement to conduct business in Delaware.
- **California**: Articles of Incorporation (corporations) or Articles of Organization (LLCs) filed with Secretary of State. Filing fee: $100 (corp) or $70 (LLC). Annual minimum franchise tax: $800 for all entities (waived first year for some). Statement of Information required annually (corps) or biennially (LLCs). California imposes entity-level tax on LLCs with gross receipts exceeding $250,000.
- **New York**: Certificate of Incorporation (corporations) or Articles of Organization (LLCs) filed with Department of State. Filing fee: $125 (corp) or $200 (LLC). LLCs must publish formation notice in two newspapers designated by the county clerk within 120 days of formation -- estimated cost $500-$2,000+. Biennial statement filing required.

### Tax Classification (Check-the-Box)

- **What**: IRS default classification rules under Treas. Reg. Section 301.7701-3 determine how an entity is taxed absent an election.
- **Threshold/Timeline**: Default classification applies automatically. A domestic entity with two or more members defaults to partnership; single-member defaults to disregarded entity. Election to change classification via Form 8832 is effective on the date specified (up to 75 days retroactive or 12 months prospective). Once changed, cannot re-elect for 60 months.
- **Consequence**: Incorrect or untimely classification election can result in unexpected entity-level taxation, loss of pass-through treatment, or recognition of gain on deemed conversion transactions.

### S-Corp Election Requirements

- **What**: Eligible corporations elect S-corp status by filing IRS Form 2553 with consent of all shareholders.
- **Threshold/Timeline**: Must be filed within 2 months and 15 days of the beginning of the tax year for which the election is to be effective. Late election relief available under Rev. Proc. 2013-30 if requirements met.
- **Consequence**: Termination triggers a 5-year waiting period before re-election. Passive investment income exceeding 25% of gross receipts for 3 consecutive years terminates S status if the corporation has accumulated E&P from C-corp years.

### Series LLCs

- **What**: Certain states (Delaware, Illinois, Nevada, Texas, others) permit formation of series LLCs where each series can hold separate assets, incur separate liabilities, and have separate members/managers.
- **Threshold/Timeline**: Delaware: authorized by DLLCA Section 18-215. Must be established in the operating agreement with proper notice in the certificate of formation.
- **Consequence**: Liability segregation between series is recognized in the forming state but may not be recognized in other jurisdictions. Federal tax treatment remains uncertain -- IRS proposed regulations (REG-119921-09) treat each series as a separate entity for tax purposes.

### Professional Entities

- **What**: Professionals (attorneys, physicians, accountants, architects, engineers) must form professional corporations (PC), professional LLCs (PLLC), or professional limited liability partnerships (PLLP) in most states.
- **Threshold/Timeline**: State-specific requirements; typically require all owners/members to hold applicable professional licenses. May require approval from the relevant licensing board before formation.
- **Consequence**: Formation as a standard entity without professional designation may be void or voidable. Professional liability remains personal regardless of entity form -- the entity shield does not protect against malpractice claims.

### Foreign Qualification

- **What**: Entities must register (foreign qualify) in each state where they "transact business" beyond their state of formation.
- **Threshold/Timeline**: Definition of "transacting business" varies by state. Generally triggered by maintaining an office, employees, or substantial business activity in the state. Filing fees range from $50-$750+ depending on the state.
- **Consequence**: Failure to qualify can result in inability to maintain lawsuits in that state, fines, penalties, and liability for back taxes. Does not affect the validity of contracts entered into in the state (in most jurisdictions).

## Interaction with Other Areas

- **Securities**: Issuance of equity interests (stock, membership interests, partnership interests) implicates federal and state securities laws regardless of entity type. Exemption availability may depend on entity structure.
- **Employment**: Entity type affects employment tax obligations, worker classification, and benefit plan eligibility. Multi-entity structures require analysis of joint employer risk.
- **IP and Technology**: IP ownership must be properly assigned to the correct entity at formation. Founder IP assignment agreements should be executed contemporaneously with formation.
- **Data Privacy**: Entity structure affects data controller/processor relationships in multi-entity organizations.
- **Tax**: Entity type determines available tax treatment, deduction eligibility (QSBS under Section 1202 requires C-corp status), and transaction structuring options.
