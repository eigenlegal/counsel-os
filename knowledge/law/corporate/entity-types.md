# Business Entity Types

## Overview
Choosing the appropriate business entity type is a foundational legal and tax decision that affects liability protection, governance structure, tax treatment, ability to raise capital, and regulatory compliance. The primary entity types are C corporations, S corporations, limited liability companies (LLCs), partnerships, and sole proprietorships.

## Key Requirements
- **C Corporation:** Formed under state corporation statutes (e.g., DGCL, MBCA). Taxed at entity level (corporate income tax) with potential double taxation on distributions. Unlimited number and types of stockholders. Preferred for venture capital and institutional investment due to stock structure. Governed by board of directors elected by stockholders. Required formalities include articles/certificate of incorporation, bylaws, organizational resolutions, stock issuance, annual meetings, and annual reports.
- **S Corporation:** A tax election (IRS Form 2553) available to eligible corporations with no more than 100 shareholders, one class of stock, and only eligible shareholders (individuals, certain trusts, estates — no partnerships, corporations, or non-resident aliens). Pass-through taxation eliminates double taxation. Must comply with all corporation-level governance requirements. S-election can be inadvertently terminated by violating eligibility requirements.
- **Limited Liability Company (LLC):** Formed under state LLC acts. Default tax treatment as partnership (multi-member) or disregarded entity (single-member), with option to elect C-corp or S-corp taxation. Maximum flexibility in governance and economic arrangements through operating agreement. Fewer mandatory formalities than corporations. Can have different classes of membership interests. Delaware LLCs permit broad modification of fiduciary duties by contract.
- **Limited Partnership (LP):** One or more general partners (with unlimited liability and management authority) and limited partners (with limited liability and no management authority). Common structure for private equity and venture capital funds. Tax-transparent. Governed by partnership agreement.
- **General Partnership:** Formed by association of two or more persons carrying on a business for profit. All partners have unlimited joint and several liability. No formation filing required in most states. Governed by the Revised Uniform Partnership Act (RUPA) and partnership agreement.
- **Foreign qualification:** Entities must register (foreign qualify) in each state where they are "doing business" beyond their state of formation. Failure to qualify can result in inability to bring lawsuits in that state, fines, and back taxes.

## Common Contract Issues
- Operating agreements or bylaws that fail to address key governance, transfer, and dissolution scenarios.
- Entity type mismatches in investment transactions — VCs typically require C-corporation structure; converting from LLC to C-corp has tax consequences.
- Failure to maintain corporate/LLC formalities leading to veil-piercing risk.
- Inadvertent S-corp election termination through prohibited stock transfers or issuance of a second class of stock.
- Confusion between membership interest transfer and management rights transfer in LLC operating agreements.
- Multi-entity structures lacking clear inter-company agreements, consolidated governance, and consistent entity maintenance.
- Missing or inadequate buy-sell provisions (drag-along, tag-along, right of first refusal, call/put rights) in governing documents.
- Tax classification elections not aligned with business objectives or investor requirements.

## Interaction with Other Areas
- **Securities:** Issuance of equity interests (stock, membership interests, partnership interests) implicates federal and state securities laws regardless of entity type.
- **Employment:** Entity type affects employment tax obligations, worker classification, and benefit plan eligibility. Multi-entity structures require careful analysis of joint employer risk.
- **IP and Technology:** IP ownership must be properly assigned to the correct entity in multi-entity structures. Entity formation should include IP assignment agreements from founders.
- **Data Privacy:** Entity structure affects data controller/processor relationships and obligations in multi-entity organizations.
- **Securities / Corporate (M&A):** Entity type determines available acquisition structures (statutory merger, stock purchase, asset purchase, interest purchase) and their tax and legal consequences.
