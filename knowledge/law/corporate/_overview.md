## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- Fiduciary duties of directors, officers, managers, and controlling stockholders/members cannot be fully eliminated by contract (though Delaware LLCs permit modification to the extent allowed by DLLCA Section 18-1101(c); the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing cannot be eliminated).
- Corporate formalities (e.g., board/stockholder approvals) required by statute for fundamental transactions (mergers, dissolution, sale of all/substantially all assets) must be followed regardless of contractual provisions.
- HSR Act pre-merger notification filing requirements apply when transaction size and party size thresholds are met ($119.5M size-of-transaction threshold for 2024); closing before HSR clearance is illegal with penalties up to $51,744 per day.
- CFIUS review and national security considerations cannot be waived by contract when applicable; mandatory declarations are required for certain TID US business transactions.
- Tax classification elections (entity classification, S-corp election, QSBS) have statutory deadlines and requirements that override contractual flexibility (S-corp: 75-day filing window; check-the-box: 60-month re-election bar).
- Annual franchise tax and reporting obligations must be maintained to preserve entity good standing and liability protection; failure results in administrative dissolution or voiding.
- Asset distribution in dissolution must follow the mandatory priority order (creditors before equity holders); directors authorizing distributions in violation face personal liability.
- The 3-year statutory survival period for dissolved Delaware corporations (DGCL Section 278) cannot be shortened by contract and governs post-dissolution litigation rights.
