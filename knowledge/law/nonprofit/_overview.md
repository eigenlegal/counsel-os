## Key Constraints

These are non-overridable legal requirements from this area. They cannot be modified by practice/ or matters/ overrides.

- A 501(c)(3) is subject to an absolute prohibition on participating or intervening in any political campaign for or against a candidate for public office; violation can cost exempt status and trigger excise tax under § 4955. The July 2025 proposed consent decree in *National Religious Broadcasters v. IRS* was REJECTED by the district court on March 31, 2026 (Anti-Injunction Act/Declaratory Judgment Act bar), so the political-campaign prohibition remains fully in force; appeal pending. Verify current status before relying on any "house of worship" carve-out.
- No part of a 501(c)(3) or (c)(6) organization's net earnings may inure to a private shareholder or individual; excess-benefit transactions with disqualified persons trigger § 4958 intermediate-sanctions excise taxes on the recipient and knowing managers.
- Failure to file a required Form 990-series return for three consecutive years results in automatic revocation of exempt status (§ 6033(j)); this is mechanical and not discretionary.
- Income from a trade or business regularly carried on and not substantially related to exempt purpose is unrelated business taxable income, taxed at the corporate rate, unless a § 512(b) exclusion or statutory exception applies (§§ 511-514).
- Permanently restricted (endowment) gift principal cannot be spent except as authorized by donor terms, UPMIFA, donor consent, or court order; unauthorized invasion of principal is a breach the state Attorney General has standing to enforce.
- Donor restrictions on a gift bind the organization; using restricted funds for another purpose is a breach of charitable trust/fiduciary duty regardless of organizational need.
