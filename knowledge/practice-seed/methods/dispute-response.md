# Dispute Response

Use when the company receives a demand letter, threat of litigation, notice of breach, regulatory inquiry, or any formal or informal dispute communication from a counterparty, regulatory body, or third party.

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Threshold | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Lawsuit filed, regulatory enforcement action, injunction/TRO sought, exposure >$500K, criminal referral | Same-day assessment. Engage outside counsel within 24 hours. |
| **High** | Formal demand with litigation threat, regulatory inquiry or CID, breach notice with termination threat, class action threat | Assessment within 48 hours. |
| **Medium** | Informal complaint, fee dispute, performance issue, warranty claim, individual consumer complaint | Assessment within 5 business days. |
| **Low** | Customer complaint, minor contractual disagreement, invoice dispute (<$25K) | Assessment within 10 business days. |

## Deadline Reference Ranges

- **Answer to complaint:** typically 20-30 days from service (check jurisdiction-specific rules)
- **Grand jury subpoena:** 14-21 days
- **Civil investigative demands (CIDs):** 30-60 days
- **Wells notice response:** 30 days
- **SEC comment letters:** 10 business days
- **TRO/preliminary injunction:** may require response within days -- check court order
- **Cure periods:** per the contract's breach/termination provisions
- **Regulatory response dates:** per the specific inquiry or subpoena

## Privilege Assessment

When litigation is reasonably anticipated (demand letter received, complaint filed, regulatory investigation opened):
- Mark all internal communications "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT" from that point forward
- Engage outside counsel early to establish attorney-client privilege
- Do not forward the dispute communication broadly -- limit distribution to need-to-know

## Preservation Scope by Severity

- **Critical/High:** Broad litigation hold. All custodians who interacted with the counterparty or subject matter. Preserve email, messaging, documents, and system logs. Issue hold notice within 24 hours.
- **Medium:** Targeted hold. Primary business contacts and their direct reports. Preserve email and contract files.
- **Low:** Document preservation reminder to the primary business contact. No formal hold unless the dispute escalates.

## Settle vs. Defend Threshold

**Settle if:**
1. Exposure exceeds $250K AND defense strength is Weak (<40%), OR
2. Cost of defense (legal fees + management time) exceeds 60% of the demand, OR
3. The dispute threatens a critical business relationship worth more than the settlement amount, OR
4. Reputational risk of public litigation outweighs the settlement cost

**Defend if:**
1. Defense strength is Strong (>70%), OR
2. The claim is without merit and settling would create precedent for future claims, OR
3. Insurance covers defense costs with no impact on premiums, OR
4. A counterclaim has significant value

**Hybrid approach:** Defend on the merits while simultaneously exploring settlement through the contractual dispute resolution process.

## Response Tone

- Professional and measured. Do not escalate. Do not make admissions. Do not use inflammatory language.
- Acknowledge receipt of the communication (but not the merits)
- Reserve all rights and defenses explicitly ("without waiving any rights or defenses")
- If disputing: state grounds clearly with reference to specific contract provisions and facts
- If seeking cure: outline the cure plan, timeline, and verification method
- If settling: propose terms without admitting liability; use "without prejudice" or "for settlement purposes only"
- Reference the dispute resolution clause and invoke required procedures

## Escalation Timeline

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| **Day 1** | Triage, classify, initiate litigation hold (Critical/High) |
| **Day 2-3** | Fact gathering, preliminary legal analysis |
| **Day 5** | Strategy recommendation to senior counsel / GC |
| **Day 7-10** | Response drafted and reviewed |
| **Day 14** | Response sent (or per contractual/legal deadlines if sooner) |

## Strategy Options

- **Resolve:** Claim has merit or cost-benefit favors settlement. Negotiate a resolution. Prepare settlement authority range.
- **Defend:** Claim lacks merit. Prepare formal response disputing allegations. Engage outside counsel for Critical/High.
- **Defer:** More information needed. Respond acknowledging receipt, reserving all rights, without admitting or conceding. Request clarification.
- **Escalate:** Critical exposure (>$1M), potential criminal liability, or board-level risk. Notify executive leadership and/or board within 48 hours. Brief D&O insurer.
