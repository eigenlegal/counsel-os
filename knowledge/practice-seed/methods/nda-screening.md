# NDA Screening Checklist

## Initial Classification

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Identify NDA type: mutual or one-way (disclosing/receiving) — direction determines which protections matter
- Confirm the NDA type matches the business need — a one-way NDA when mutual disclosure is planned leaves one party unprotected
- Check if we have an existing NDA with this counterparty — duplicate NDAs create ambiguity about which governs
- Determine if our template should be proposed instead — using our template avoids negotiating unfavorable third-party terms

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Identify the business purpose and requesting stakeholder
- Counterparty legal entity name verified
- Jurisdiction of incorporation confirmed
- Authorized signatory identified

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Affiliate coverage addressed (does the NDA cover subsidiaries?)
- Prior relationship history with counterparty

## Definition of Confidential Information

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Definition broad enough to cover oral, written, electronic, and visual disclosures — narrow definitions create gaps in protection
- No overly restrictive marking/labeling requirements — marking requirements for oral disclosures are impractical and create loopholes
- Permitted use limited to the stated business purpose — broad use rights undermine the purpose of the NDA
- Standard exclusions present: public domain, prior knowledge, independent development, rightful third-party disclosure — missing exclusions create overbroad obligations

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Definition includes derivative information and analyses
- Oral disclosures covered without unreasonable follow-up requirements
- Exclusion for compelled disclosure includes notice and cooperation obligations
- Trade secret carve-out for perpetual protection

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Definition covers metadata and embedded data
- Samples, prototypes, and physical materials included

## Red-Flag Provisions

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- No residuals clause (permits use of retained ideas/concepts) — effectively nullifies confidentiality for intangible information
- No non-compete clauses (unless specifically required) — hidden non-competes restrict future business activities
- No IP assignment or broad license grants — NDAs should not transfer IP rights
- No indemnification obligations (unusual for NDAs) — indemnification in an NDA is disproportionate to the transaction

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- No standstill provisions (M&A context only)
- No exclusivity obligations
- No liquidated damages provisions
- No non-disparagement clause
- No audit or inspection rights (unusual for NDAs)
- No non-solicitation of employees (unless specifically required)

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- No data processing obligations beyond standard confidentiality
- No governing law in an unusual or inconvenient jurisdiction

## Duration and Survival

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Confidentiality period between 2-5 years with trade secret carve-out — periods under 2 years provide inadequate protection; over 5 years may be unreasonable
- Term of the agreement (not just confidentiality period) defined — the agreement term and confidentiality period are distinct and both must be addressed

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination for the stated period
- Return or destruction of confidential information upon request or termination
- Certification of destruction available upon request

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Automatic termination vs. termination on notice
- Renewal mechanism (if any) clearly stated

## Protective Provisions

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Compelled disclosure permitted with prior notice and cooperation — without this, a subpoena forces breach or contempt
- Injunctive relief acknowledged as an available remedy — establishes that monetary damages alone are inadequate

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Need-to-know dissemination limits (employees, contractors, advisors)
- Binding confidentiality obligations for permitted recipients
- Specific technical safeguards requirements (if sensitive data)
- Return or destruction upon termination with certification

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Notification of unauthorized disclosure
- Restrictions on copying or reproduction

## Legal and Administrative

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Governing law and jurisdiction acceptable — litigation in an inconvenient forum is costly and disadvantageous

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Dispute resolution mechanism reasonable
- Assignment restrictions present
- Amendment and waiver provisions included
- Counterparts and electronic signature permitted

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Relationship of parties clause (independent contractors, no partnership)
- Entire agreement clause

## Approval and Execution

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Classified as FAST TRACK / MINOR EDITS / FULL REVIEW — ensures appropriate level of review for risk level
- Signing authority confirmed per delegation of authority — unauthorized signatures may not bind the organization

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- If MINOR EDITS: changes identified and redline prepared
- If FULL REVIEW: issues list prepared with risk classifications
- NDA logged in contract management system with key metadata

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Business stakeholder notified of execution
- Calendar reminder set for expiration date
- Template added to counterparty file for future reference
