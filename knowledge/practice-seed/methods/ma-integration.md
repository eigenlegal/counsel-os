# M&A Integration

Reference guide for post-closing legal integration following a completed acquisition, merger, or significant asset purchase. Covers the legal workstreams required to integrate the acquired entity or assets into the buyer's operations, compliance programs, and corporate structure.

---

## Contract Consent Priority Classification

| Priority | Timeframe | Contracts | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Day 1-30 | Key customer agreements, critical vendor/supplier contracts, technology licenses essential to operations, facility leases | Failure to obtain consent could result in termination, material breach, or loss of rights |
| **Important** | Day 31-90 | Non-critical vendor agreements, partnership agreements, distribution agreements | Consent required but counterparty unlikely to terminate |
| **Administrative** | Day 91-180 | Contracts requiring notice only, or where consent is a technical requirement | No practical enforcement risk |

### Consent Tracking Fields
Contract | Counterparty | Consent Status | Follow-Up Date | Risk if Refused

For Critical contracts: personal outreach from the relationship owner before sending the formal consent letter. If consent is refused: assess remedies (negotiate, find alternative supplier/partner, invoke dispute resolution).

## Anti-Assignment Clause Analysis by Transaction Type

| Transaction Type | Assignment Trigger? | Key Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| **Stock purchase / Merger** | Usually no — the contracting entity has not changed | But some clauses specifically cover "change of control" — read each clause carefully |
| **Asset purchase** | Yes — virtually all contracts require assignment consent | Prioritize based on business criticality |

## Non-Contractual Change-of-Control Triggers

### Regulatory Approvals and Licenses
- Government contracts: may require novation or re-certification
- Regulated industry licenses (banking, insurance, healthcare, securities, telecom): may require regulatory approval for change of control; some pre-closing, others post-closing notifications
- Export control licenses: specific licenses may not transfer automatically
- Data privacy registrations: DPA registrations, GDPR representative appointments, CCPA service provider notifications

### Employee Agreements
- Executive employment agreements with change-of-control provisions (acceleration of vesting, severance triggers, golden parachute payments)
- Retention agreements and stay bonuses tied to the transaction
- Non-compete agreements: assess enforceability post-acquisition (does the buyer's broader business scope make the non-compete unreasonable?)

### Financial Instruments
- Loan agreements and credit facilities with change-of-control provisions (may trigger mandatory prepayment or default)
- Bond indentures with change-of-control put rights
- Lease agreements with change-of-control consent requirements

## Integration Speed Decision Framework

| Approach | When Appropriate | Key Actions |
|---|---|---|
| **Rapid (0-90 days)** | Bolt-on acquisitions where the target's operations will be fully absorbed | Merge entities quickly, consolidate contracts, terminate redundant employees |
| **Phased (3-12 months)** | Larger acquisitions where operational continuity requires maintaining separate structures temporarily | Keep target as subsidiary initially; integrate in stages |
| **Maintain as subsidiary (indefinite)** | Regulatory requirements mandate a separate entity, the target has a distinct brand, or liability isolation benefits exist | Retain separate entity structure |

## Compliance Program Merger

### Comparison Areas
Compare the buyer's and target's compliance programs across:
- Anti-corruption / anti-bribery (FCPA, UK Bribery Act)
- Data privacy (GDPR, CCPA, sector-specific)
- Antitrust/competition
- Export controls and sanctions
- Industry-specific compliance (healthcare, financial services, government contracts)

Identify the higher standard for each area and adopt it as the unified standard.

### Gap Remediation Timeframes
| Severity | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Critical gaps | Within 30 days |
| High gaps | Within 90 days |
| Medium gaps | Within 180 days |

If the target had no formal compliance program: implement the buyer's program immediately. Priority order: code of conduct rollout, anti-corruption training, data privacy assessment.

## Employment Agreement Approach

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Assumption of existing agreements** | Lower disruption, faster integration | May result in inconsistent terms across the combined workforce |
| **New agreement rollout** | Creates uniform terms | More disruptive; requires consent from each employee (offer continued employment conditional on signing) |
| **Hybrid** | Balances consistency and disruption | Complexity in managing two tiers |

Hybrid approach: assume existing agreements for most employees; issue new agreements for executives and key employees with updated terms.

### Employment Integration Checklist
- Review all acquired employee offer letters and employment agreements (at-will status, non-competes, non-solicitation, IP assignment, severance)
- Harmonize compensation structures (salary bands, bonus programs, commission plans)
- Harmonize benefits (health insurance, retirement plans, PTO, equity)
- Assess WARN Act implications if workforce reductions are planned
- Verify work authorization for all acquired employees; assess H-1B transfer requirements
- Conduct employee communication (explain changes, timelines, points of contact)

### Common Obligations
- Tax filings
- Regulatory notifications
- Employee benefit transitions
- Non-competition covenants
- Indemnification claims process
- Earnout milestones
- Working capital adjustment

### Indemnification Management
- Monitor for indemnification claims (from buyer against seller, or third-party claims against the acquired business)
- Track claim notification deadlines and procedures per the acquisition agreement
- Manage escrow/holdback releases per the agreed schedule

### IP Integration Actions
- Record assignments of all acquired IP with relevant registries (USPTO, WIPO, Copyright Office)
- Update trademark ownership records and specimens of use
- Transfer domain name registrations to the buyer's registrar
- Consolidate patent portfolios: identify overlapping patents, assess cross-licensing opportunities
- Review the target's open-source usage and integrate into the buyer's open-source compliance program
- Verify all acquired employees have executed IP assignment agreements; remediate gaps
