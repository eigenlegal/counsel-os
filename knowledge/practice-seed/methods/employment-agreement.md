# Employment Agreement Checklist

## Offer Letter Fundamentals

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- At-will status clearly stated (if applicable) — absent at-will language may create implied contract claims
- Job title, reporting structure, and start date specified — ambiguity creates disputes about scope of role
- Base compensation clearly stated (salary or hourly rate) — compensation disputes are among the most common employment claims
- Classification as exempt or non-exempt correct under FLSA — misclassification triggers overtime liability and penalties
- Benefits eligibility and start date specified — verbal promises of benefits can create enforceable obligations

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Work location specified (office, remote, hybrid) with flexibility provisions
- Bonus or commission structure described (if applicable)
- Equity compensation referenced with separate grant documents
- Probationary or introductory period defined (if applicable)
- Relocation assistance terms (if applicable)

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Expense reimbursement policy referenced
- Professional development and training provisions
- Company property and equipment provisions

## Restrictive Covenants

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Non-compete scope reasonable in duration, geography, and activity — overbroad non-competes are unenforceable in many states and banned in some
- Non-compete compliant with state law of employee's work location — FTC and state legislatures are actively restricting non-competes
- Non-solicitation of customers limited to those the employee actually served — broad customer non-solicits may be treated as de facto non-competes
- Non-solicitation of employees reasonable in scope and duration — 12-24 months is typical; longer may be challenged
- Adequate consideration provided for restrictive covenants — in many states, continued employment alone is insufficient consideration

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Non-compete includes garden leave or compensation during restriction period
- Non-disclosure obligations clearly distinguished from non-compete
- Carve-outs for general skills and industry knowledge
- Enforceability savings clause (court may modify rather than void)
- Choice of law provision for restrictive covenants matches employee's state

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Non-compete notification obligations to future employers
- Injunctive relief provision for breach of restrictive covenants
- Step-down provisions (restrictions narrow over time)

## Confidentiality and IP Assignment

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Confidentiality obligations cover all proprietary information — gaps in coverage leave trade secrets unprotected
- Confidentiality survives termination of employment — time-limited confidentiality for trade secrets is inadequate
- Invention assignment covers all work-related inventions created during employment — absent assignment, employee may own inventions under default law
- State-specific carve-outs for personal inventions (e.g., California Labor Code 2870) — failure to include required carve-outs can void the entire assignment
- Work-for-hire designation for copyrightable materials — ensures employer owns copyrights from creation

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Pre-existing IP disclosed and excluded from assignment
- Moral rights waiver (to extent permitted by law)
- Cooperation obligation for IP registration and enforcement
- Return of company materials upon termination
- Obligation to disclose inventions during employment

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Open-source contribution policy referenced
- Social media and personal brand IP boundaries
- Assignment covers inventions conceived during employment but reduced to practice after

## Compensation and Benefits

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Total compensation package complete and consistent with offer — discrepancies between verbal offer and written agreement create claims
- Bonus eligibility, metrics, and payment timing specified — vague bonus language creates implied obligation to pay
- Commission plan attached or referenced with specificity — commission disputes are a leading source of employment litigation
- Equity vesting schedule and acceleration events specified — single-trigger vs. double-trigger acceleration has significant value implications

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Clawback provisions for bonuses and commissions
- Change-of-control provisions for equity acceleration
- Severance eligibility and amount (if applicable)
- Deferred compensation compliant with Section 409A
- Benefits continuation rights (COBRA, conversion)

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Sign-on bonus with repayment obligation if early departure
- Tuition reimbursement with service commitment
- Retention bonuses tied to specific milestones

## Termination Provisions

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Termination for cause defined with specific grounds — vague "cause" definitions create disputes about whether termination was proper
- Notice period requirements specified — inadequate notice can trigger breach claims and damage relationships
- Severance conditioned on execution of separation agreement and release — release must be supported by adequate consideration and comply with ADEA/OWBPA

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Cure period for performance-based termination
- Resignation notice requirements
- Garden leave provisions during notice period
- Treatment of unvested equity upon termination
- Treatment of accrued but unpaid bonuses/commissions

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Cooperation obligations post-termination
- Transition assistance requirements
- Reference and non-disparagement provisions

## Compliance and Regulatory

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Background check and drug testing provisions compliant with state law — "ban the box" and marijuana legalization create compliance traps
- Arbitration clause (if included) compliant with state requirements — some states restrict mandatory employment arbitration
- Governing law appropriate for employee's work location — employment law is generally governed by the state where work is performed

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Equal opportunity and non-discrimination provisions
- Whistleblower protection acknowledgment
- DTSA notice of immunity for confidential disclosures to government
- Electronic monitoring and privacy disclosures (where required)

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Compliance training requirements
- Code of conduct referenced and acknowledged
- Conflicts of interest disclosure requirements

## Execution and Administration

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- All documents in the employment package identified and attached — missing documents create ambiguity about what was agreed
- Adequate time provided for review (21 days for OWBPA; reasonable for all) — rushed execution may invalidate restrictive covenants

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Employee acknowledged receipt and understanding of all documents
- Separate signature pages for distinct agreements (NDA, IP assignment, non-compete)
- Executed copies filed in HR system with key dates calendared

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Periodic review cadence established for updating restrictive covenants
- Template version tracked for future updates
