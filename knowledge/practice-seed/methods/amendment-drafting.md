# Amendment Drafting

Relevant when modifying the terms of an existing agreement through a formal amendment, addendum, or change order. Applies to changes in scope, pricing, term, parties, or any substantive contract provisions.

## Amendment vs. Amended & Restated Decision Criteria

| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Changes affect fewer than 5 provisions AND fewer than 3 prior amendments exist | **Point amendment** |
| Changes affect 5+ provisions, OR 3+ prior amendments already exist, OR cumulative amendments make the contract difficult to read | **Amended and restated agreement** (consolidates everything into one clean document) |
| Changes are purely administrative (address change, contact update, notice information) | **Simple letter agreement or notice** per the contract's administrative provisions |

**30% threshold:** If more than 30% of the original provisions have been modified across all amendments, strongly recommend an Amended and Restated Agreement over another point amendment.

## Change Tracking Table Template

| Section | Current Language | Proposed Language | Reason for Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 (Term) | "12-month initial term" | "24-month initial term" | Business requests extension |
| 7.2 (Liability) | "Cap of $100,000" | "Cap equal to 12 months of fees" | Aligns with liability position |

## Cascading Change Checklist

When modifying one provision, assess whether corresponding updates are needed elsewhere:

- **Changing scope:** May affect pricing, SLAs, liability cap, insurance requirements
- **Changing pricing:** May affect liability cap (if fee-based), payment terms, audit rights
- **Changing term:** May affect pricing, renewal mechanics, termination notice periods
- **Changing data processing:** May require DPA amendment
- **Adding parties:** May require assignment/novation analysis
- **Changing SLAs:** May affect remedies, credits, termination triggers

Also check:
- Does the change affect any defined terms used elsewhere?
- Does the change affect any calculation or formula (liability cap, fees, SLAs)?
- Does the change conflict with any provision not being amended?
- Does the change trigger any consent or notification requirements (notice to third parties, sub-processors, regulatory filings)?

## Amendment Structure Template

### Header
- Title: "Amendment No. [X] to [Agreement Name]"
- Date of the amendment
- Reference to the original agreement (full title, date, parties)
- Reference to all prior amendments: "as previously amended by Amendment No. 1 dated [date], Amendment No. 2 dated [date]" (or "as previously amended, the 'Agreement'")

### Recitals
- Identify the parties (matching the original agreement's party definitions)
- Reference the original agreement and all prior amendments
- Brief statement: "The parties wish to amend the Agreement to [brief description of purpose]"
- Context for the amendment (optional but helpful for interpretation)

### Operative Provisions — Modification Language Patterns

**Modification (full restatement — preferred for clarity):**
> Section [X] of the Agreement is hereby amended and restated in its entirety as follows: [new text]

**Modification (surgical edit):**
> Section [X] is hereby amended by replacing '[old text]' with '[new text]'

**Addition:**
> The following new Section [X] is hereby added to the Agreement: [new text]

**Deletion:**
> Section [X] of the Agreement is hereby deleted in its entirety.

### Required Boilerplate

**Ratification clause (required):**
> Except as expressly modified by this Amendment, all terms and conditions of the Agreement remain in full force and effect and are hereby ratified and confirmed.

**Conflict clause (required):**
> In the event of any conflict between this Amendment and the Agreement, this Amendment shall control.

**Counterparts clause:**
> This Amendment may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

**Defined terms:**
> Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Amendment shall have the meanings assigned to them in the Agreement.

### Signature Blocks
- Match the original agreement's signature format (party names, title lines, date lines)
- Ensure signatories have appropriate authority per the delegation of authority matrix
- Include witness/notarization lines if the original agreement required them

## Legal Review Depth Decision Matrix

| Change Type | Review Level | Target Time |
|---|---|---|
| Simple (term extension, pricing update, administrative) | Self-review sufficient | 1-2 hours |
| Moderate (scope expansion, new SLAs, party changes) | Peer review recommended | 4-8 hours |
| Material (liability restructuring, new data processing, material commercial terms) | Senior counsel review required | 1-3 days |

## Version Control Best Practices

- Maintain a contract lineage document listing: Original Agreement (date) -> Amendment No. 1 (date) -> Amendment No. 2 (date) -> etc.
- Number amendments sequentially ("Amendment No. 3" not "Third Amendment") for clarity
- Each amendment should reference all prior amendments in its recitals to maintain a clear chain
- Before drafting, map the current effective terms by incorporating all prior amendments — for each provision being changed, document the current effective language (which may come from the original or a prior amendment)
