# Partner Terms Checklist

## Partnership Structure

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Partnership type identified (strategic alliance, channel, technology, co-sell, referral, reseller, OEM) — determines the entire legal framework and risk profile
- Exclusivity scope determined (exclusive, non-exclusive, territory-limited) — exclusivity restricts future deals and creates dependency
- Term length and renewal mechanism appropriate for the partnership type — long-term exclusivity without performance minimums locks you in
- Performance milestones or minimum commitments defined (if applicable) — without minimums, an exclusive partner can underperform with no consequence

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Business model and commercial structure defined
- Territory and market segment allocations specified
- Ramp-up period and initial expectations documented
- Exit criteria for underperformance defined

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Partner tier or certification level requirements
- Competitive product restrictions (if any)

## Economic Terms

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Revenue sharing model clearly defined (percentage split, referral fees, reseller margins) — ambiguous rev-share is the top source of partner disputes
- Commission or referral fee trigger events defined (deal registration, closed sale, etc.) — unclear triggers lead to disputes about who earned what
- Audit rights for revenue/commission calculations — without audit rights, you cannot verify payment accuracy
- Payment terms and invoicing process documented — ensures cash flow predictability

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Pricing for reseller/channel arrangements specified
- Minimum purchase or revenue commitments (if applicable)
- Currency and exchange rate provisions for international partnerships
- True-up or reconciliation process for overpayments/underpayments
- Clawback provisions for reversed or disputed transactions

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Tiered pricing or volume discount structure
- Marketing development fund (MDF) allocation and reimbursement rules

## Branding and Marketing

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Trademark license scope and limitations defined — overbroad licenses create brand dilution and quality control risk
- Approval process for marketing materials using the other party's brand — unapproved use can damage brand reputation

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Brand usage guidelines referenced or attached
- Co-marketing obligations and budget allocation specified
- Joint press release and public announcement rights defined
- Use of partner logos on websites and materials approved

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Social media co-promotion guidelines established
- Marketing data sharing and lead attribution rules defined
- Trademark license wind-down period upon termination

## Customer Relationship

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Customer ownership and primary relationship clearly defined — ambiguous ownership creates competing claims and customer confusion
- Customer data ownership and usage rights specified — partner access to customer data creates privacy and competitive risk
- End-user license terms and flow-down requirements specified — your terms must flow through to end users to maintain legal protections

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Lead registration and protection process documented
- Customer support responsibilities allocated (Tier 1, Tier 2, escalation)
- Customer complaint and dispute handling process defined
- Customer satisfaction and NPS tracking responsibilities assigned

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Co-selling engagement rules
- Customer reference and case study rights

## IP and Technology

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- IP ownership for jointly developed technology clearly assigned — without clear assignment, both parties may claim co-ownership
- License grants for each party's technology precisely scoped — overbroad licenses give partner more rights than intended
- Restrictions on reverse engineering and competitive use — protects core IP from misuse by a partner who may become a competitor

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- API access and integration requirements defined
- Data sharing protocols and formats specified
- Open-source compliance for integrated components
- Interoperability commitments and testing requirements

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Technology roadmap sharing and coordination (if applicable)
- Source code access or escrow for critical integrations

## Compliance and Regulatory

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Anti-corruption and FCPA compliance provisions included — partner corruption can create direct liability for your organization
- Data privacy and protection obligations allocated — data sharing between partners triggers privacy law obligations
- Representations regarding compliance with applicable laws — partner non-compliance can create reputational and legal exposure

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Export control and sanctions compliance addressed
- Industry-specific regulatory requirements identified
- Compliance training and certification requirements specified
- Audit rights for compliance verification
- Insurance requirements specified

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Modern slavery / supply chain compliance
- Environmental and sustainability commitments

## Termination and Wind-Down

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Termination for cause with cure period — ensures you can exit for partner misconduct
- Termination upon change of control — protects against your partner being acquired by a competitor
- Wind-down obligations clearly defined — covers customer transition, pipeline revenue, data return, and trademark wind-down

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Termination for convenience with adequate notice period
- Revenue share for pipeline deals post-termination
- Return of confidential information and materials
- Ongoing support obligations for existing customers
- Survival provisions identified

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Post-termination non-compete or non-solicitation (if applicable)
- Trademark license wind-down period
- Transition assistance obligations

## Risk Allocation

### Must-Check (Tier 1)
- Limitation of liability proportionate to the partnership value — uncapped liability is disproportionate for most partnerships
- Indemnification obligations mutual and appropriate — one-sided indemnification shifts risk unfairly

### Should-Check (Tier 2)
- Insurance requirements proportionate to risk exposure
- Representations and warranties from both parties
- Each party responsible for its own compliance with applicable laws
- Force majeure provisions included

### Nice-to-Check (Tier 3)
- Escrow arrangements for disputed amounts
- Dispute resolution escalation process
