## Warranties Disclaimers

# Warranties & Disclaimers — Clause Library

## Express Warranty of Performance

### Standard
> Vendor warrants that the Services shall perform materially in accordance with the applicable documentation during the Term. If the Services fail to conform to this warranty, Vendor shall, at its expense, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformity. If Vendor is unable to correct the non-conformity within thirty (30) days, Customer may terminate the affected Services and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor warrants that the Services shall perform in all material respects in accordance with the documentation, specifications, and any representations made during the sales process. The Services shall be free from material defects and shall not materially degrade in functionality during the Term. If the Services fail to conform, Vendor shall promptly correct the non-conformity at no charge. If not corrected within fifteen (15) days, Customer may terminate the affected Services and receive a full refund of all fees paid during the twelve (12) months preceding the non-conformity.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor warrants that the Services shall perform substantially in accordance with the documentation generally available on Vendor's website. This warranty shall not apply to: (a) any features described as beta, preview, or experimental; (b) any issues caused by Customer's equipment, network, or third-party software; (c) any use not in strict compliance with the documentation; or (d) any free or trial-period usage. Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for breach of this warranty shall be for Vendor to use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformity.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor warrants that the Services shall perform materially in accordance with the documentation. Vendor's sole obligation for breach of this warranty is to use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformity or, if unable, provide a pro-rata refund.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with broad exclusions (beta features, customer equipment, strict documentation compliance) and a sole-remedy limited to correction efforts with no refund path is weak. Push for a refund backstop if correction fails. Ensure the warranty covers the full scope of the Services, not just a subset.

## Warranty of Authority and Enforceability

### Standard
> Each party represents and warrants that: (a) it is duly organized, validly existing, and in good standing under the laws of its jurisdiction of formation; (b) it has full corporate power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement; (c) the execution and performance of this Agreement have been duly authorized by all necessary corporate action; and (d) this Agreement constitutes a valid and binding obligation of such party, enforceable in accordance with its terms.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Each party represents and warrants that: (a) it is duly organized and in good standing; (b) it has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement; (c) execution has been duly authorized; (d) this Agreement is a valid and binding obligation; and (e) performance will not conflict with or violate any other agreement to which such party is bound, any applicable law, or any order of any court or governmental authority.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Each party represents that it has the authority to enter into this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party represents and warrants that it has the power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement and that this Agreement constitutes a valid and binding obligation.

### Notes
The authority and enforceability warranty is standard boilerplate and should be mutual. Vendor-Favorable brevity (single sentence) may be insufficient in cross-border transactions or when dealing with entities whose authority is unclear. The Aggressive version adds a no-conflict representation, which is useful when either party may be subject to exclusivity or non-compete obligations.

## Compliance with Laws

### Standard
> Each party warrants that it shall comply with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including applicable data protection, anti-corruption, export control, and employment laws.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents, warrants, and covenants that it shall comply with all applicable federal, state, local, and international laws, regulations, and industry standards in the performance of the Services, including all applicable data protection, privacy, anti-corruption, export control, accessibility, and consumer protection laws. Vendor shall promptly notify Customer of any material non-compliance or any governmental investigation related to the Services.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Each party shall comply with applicable laws in connection with its performance under this Agreement. Vendor does not warrant that the Services will enable Customer to comply with any specific law, regulation, or industry standard applicable to Customer's business.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party shall comply with all applicable laws in performing under this Agreement.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language disclaiming that the Services will enable regulatory compliance is reasonable for general-purpose SaaS but should be pushed back on when the Services are specifically marketed for compliance purposes (e.g., privacy management, financial reporting, healthcare IT). Ensure the warranty is forward-looking (a covenant), not just a point-in-time representation.

## Disclaimer of Implied Warranties

### Standard
> EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, THE SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" AND VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, WHETHER EXPRESS, IMPLIED, STATUTORY, OR OTHERWISE, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, TITLE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL WARRANTIES, WHETHER EXPRESS, IMPLIED, OR STATUTORY, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE. THE FOREGOING DISCLAIMER SHALL NOT LIMIT OR EXCLUDE ANY WARRANTIES THAT CANNOT BE EXCLUDED UNDER APPLICABLE LAW, NOR SHALL IT DIMINISH THE EXPRESS WARRANTIES SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT.

### Vendor-Favorable
> THE SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" AND "AS AVAILABLE." VENDOR MAKES NO REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND, WHETHER EXPRESS, IMPLIED, STATUTORY, OR OTHERWISE, REGARDING THE SERVICES, INCLUDING ANY WARRANTY THAT THE SERVICES WILL BE UNINTERRUPTED, ERROR-FREE, SECURE, OR MEET CUSTOMER'S REQUIREMENTS. VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL IMPLIED WARRANTIES, INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, TITLE, NON-INFRINGEMENT, AND ANY WARRANTIES ARISING FROM COURSE OF DEALING OR USAGE OF TRADE. CUSTOMER ASSUMES ALL RISK ASSOCIATED WITH ITS USE OF THE SERVICES.

### Minimum Acceptable
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXPRESS WARRANTIES IN THIS AGREEMENT, THE SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language disclaiming all warranties including that the Services will be secure or meet requirements may conflict with express performance warranties elsewhere in the agreement. Ensure that the disclaimer carves out the express warranties. The "AS AVAILABLE" formulation and customer-assumption-of-risk language should be resisted in enterprise agreements. In some jurisdictions (notably the EU), implied warranty disclaimers may not be fully enforceable.

## Limitation of Remedies

### Standard
> Customer's exclusive remedies for breach of warranty shall be as set forth in the applicable warranty section. If Vendor is unable to cure a warranty breach within the cure period, Customer may terminate the affected Services and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for the remainder of the Term.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> The remedies set forth in this Agreement for breach of warranty are in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available to Customer under this Agreement or at law or in equity, including the right to recover damages. If Vendor fails to cure a warranty breach within the cure period, Customer may terminate and receive a full refund of fees paid during the twelve (12) months preceding the breach.

### Vendor-Favorable
> CUSTOMER'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDY FOR ANY BREACH OF WARRANTY SHALL BE, AT VENDOR'S SOLE OPTION, (A) RE-PERFORMANCE OF THE DEFICIENT SERVICES, OR (B) A PRO-RATA CREDIT AGAINST FUTURE FEES. IN NO EVENT SHALL VENDOR BE LIABLE FOR DAMAGES ARISING FROM A WARRANTY BREACH. THIS LIMITATION OF REMEDIES APPLIES REGARDLESS OF WHETHER ANY REMEDY FAILS OF ITS ESSENTIAL PURPOSE.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If Vendor breaches its performance warranty and fails to cure within a reasonable period, Customer may terminate the affected Services and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language limiting remedies to re-performance or future credits (not refunds) with no damages path eliminates meaningful accountability. The "regardless of whether any remedy fails of its essential purpose" language attempts to preserve the limitation even when the remedy is illusory. Push for a refund backstop and ensure the limitation does not override essential purpose protections under UCC 2-719.

## Non-Infringement Warranty

### Standard
> Vendor represents and warrants that the Services and all deliverables provided under this Agreement do not and will not infringe, misappropriate, or otherwise violate any intellectual property rights of any third party. This warranty does not apply to infringement arising solely from: (a) modifications made by Customer without Vendor's authorization; (b) Customer's combination of the Services with products or services not provided or approved by Vendor, to the extent the infringement would not have occurred absent such combination; or (c) use of the Services in a manner not permitted by this Agreement or the documentation. If notified of or becoming aware of a potential infringement claim, Vendor shall promptly notify Customer and pursue the cure path set forth in the IP Indemnification section.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents, warrants, and covenants that: (a) the Services, all deliverables, and all components thereof (including third-party and open-source components) do not and will not infringe, misappropriate, or violate any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property right of any third party anywhere in the world; (b) Vendor has conducted reasonable IP clearance procedures prior to delivering the Services; and (c) Vendor has the full right and authority to grant the licenses and rights set forth in this Agreement without any conflict with third-party rights. This warranty does not apply to infringement arising solely from: (i) modifications made by Customer that Vendor did not approve in writing, or (ii) Customer-provided materials or specifications. The carve-out in (i) shall not apply if the modification was made following Vendor's instructions or recommendations.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor warrants that, to the best of its knowledge, the Services as delivered do not infringe any valid United States patent or registered copyright as of the Effective Date. This warranty does not apply to: (a) modifications by Customer; (b) combinations with non-Vendor products; (c) use outside the scope of the documentation; (d) open-source or third-party components; (e) any version other than the most current generally available release; or (f) Customer specifications or instructions. THIS WARRANTY IS IN LIEU OF ALL OTHER WARRANTIES REGARDING NON-INFRINGEMENT AND CONSTITUTES CUSTOMER'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDY FOR INFRINGEMENT CLAIMS.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor warrants that the Services, as provided by Vendor and used in accordance with this Agreement, do not infringe any third party's intellectual property rights. Standard carve-outs for Customer modifications and unauthorized combinations apply.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language limiting the warranty to "knowledge" at the "Effective Date" for US patents and registered copyrights only is extremely narrow -- it excludes trade secrets, unregistered copyrights, foreign IP rights, and any infringement that arises after the Effective Date. The broad exclusion list (open-source components, non-current versions, combinations) can swallow the warranty entirely. Push for worldwide coverage of all IP rights and resist the "sole and exclusive remedy" limitation that cuts off indemnification. The Aggressive version with an IP clearance representation is appropriate for custom development and high-value platform agreements.

## Malware-Free Warranty

### Standard
> Vendor represents and warrants that the Services and all deliverables shall be free from viruses, Trojan horses, worms, ransomware, spyware, adware, disabling code, time bombs, back doors, and any other malicious or harmful code ("Malware") at the time of delivery and throughout the Term. Vendor shall employ industry-standard measures to detect and prevent the introduction of Malware, including automated scanning of code prior to deployment. If Malware is discovered in the Services, Vendor shall: (a) notify Customer immediately upon discovery; (b) remediate the Malware at Vendor's sole expense within twenty-four (24) hours; and (c) conduct a root cause analysis and provide findings to Customer within ten (10) business days.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents, warrants, and covenants that: (a) the Services, all deliverables, updates, patches, and all components thereof shall be free from Malware at all times; (b) Vendor shall not insert or permit to be inserted into the Services any code that could disable, disrupt, erase, or otherwise harm the Services, Customer's systems, or Customer Data, including time bombs, logic bombs, and intentional back doors; (c) Vendor employs continuous, automated malware scanning and code review as part of its software development lifecycle; and (d) Vendor shall provide evidence of its malware prevention practices upon Customer's request. If Malware is discovered, Vendor shall: (i) notify Customer within four (4) hours; (ii) immediately isolate affected components; (iii) remediate within twenty-four (24) hours; (iv) provide a root cause analysis within five (5) business days; and (v) implement preventive measures to avoid recurrence. Vendor's breach of this warranty shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and shall not be subject to any cure period.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Services are free from known Malware at the time of each production release. Vendor employs industry-standard practices including automated scanning. Vendor does not guarantee that the Services will be entirely free from Malware at all times. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to remediate any confirmed Malware promptly after discovery. Customer acknowledges that no software can be guaranteed to be completely free from vulnerabilities or malicious code.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor warrants that the Services shall be free from Malware. Vendor shall employ industry-standard measures to prevent Malware and shall promptly remediate any Malware discovered in the Services at Vendor's expense.

### Notes
The Malware warranty is distinct from the security measures obligation -- it focuses specifically on the absence of harmful code rather than the broader security posture. Vendor-Favorable language with "commercially reasonable efforts" to be free from "known" Malware at "time of release" creates multiple escape hatches: unknown malware, malware introduced after release, and a low standard of effort. Push for an affirmative warranty rather than a best-efforts obligation. The prohibition on disabling code, time bombs, and back doors (Aggressive version) is critical -- these are sometimes inserted intentionally by vendors as license enforcement mechanisms and should be explicitly prohibited. The immediate-notification and rapid-remediation timelines are important because Malware in a SaaS environment can affect all customers simultaneously.

## Third-Party Components

### Standard
> Vendor represents and warrants that: (a) all third-party software, libraries, and components incorporated in the Services ("Third-Party Components") are properly licensed and that Vendor's use thereof complies with the applicable license terms; (b) no Third-Party Component is subject to a "copyleft" or similar open-source license (including GPL, AGPL, or SSPL) that would require Customer to disclose or license its own proprietary code, except as disclosed in writing to Customer prior to execution; and (c) Vendor shall maintain a current software bill of materials ("SBOM") identifying all Third-Party Components, their versions, and applicable licenses, and shall provide such SBOM to Customer upon request. Vendor shall promptly notify Customer of any Third-Party Component license change that materially affects Customer's rights under this Agreement.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents, warrants, and covenants that: (a) all Third-Party Components are properly licensed and Vendor has all rights necessary to incorporate and distribute them as part of the Services; (b) no Third-Party Component is subject to a copyleft license (GPL, LGPL, AGPL, SSPL, MPL, or any OSI-listed copyleft license) that would impose any obligation on Customer, including obligations to disclose source code, unless explicitly identified in Exhibit [X] and approved by Customer in writing; (c) all Third-Party Components are current, actively maintained, and free from known critical or high-severity vulnerabilities (as classified by CVE/CVSS scoring at 7.0 or above); (d) Vendor maintains a complete, machine-readable SBOM in SPDX or CycloneDX format, updated with each release, and provides it to Customer within five (5) business days of request; and (e) Vendor shall replace or remediate any Third-Party Component that becomes end-of-life, unsupported, or subject to a critical vulnerability within thirty (30) days. Vendor shall indemnify Customer for any claim arising from Vendor's use of Third-Party Components in violation of their license terms.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor represents that it has the right to use the third-party and open-source software incorporated in the Services. A list of open-source components and their licenses is available at [URL]. Vendor may update Third-Party Components from time to time in its sole discretion. Customer acknowledges that the Services incorporate open-source software and that such software is provided "as is" by its respective licensors. Vendor makes no warranty regarding the performance, security, or suitability of any Third-Party Component.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor warrants that all third-party and open-source components in the Services are properly licensed and that their use complies with applicable license terms. Vendor shall provide a list of such components and their licenses upon request.

### Notes
Third-party component risk has escalated significantly with supply chain attacks (e.g., SolarWinds, Log4j). Vendor-Favorable language disclaiming all warranties for third-party components and providing a URL-based list that can change without notice offers no contractual protection. Push for an SBOM obligation and copyleft disclosure. The Aggressive version's vulnerability management requirements (CVSS 7.0+ remediation within 30 days) reflect emerging best practices and regulatory expectations (e.g., EU Cyber Resilience Act). The copyleft warranty is critical because undisclosed copyleft licenses can create IP contamination risk for Customer's own codebase if Customer integrates with the Services.
