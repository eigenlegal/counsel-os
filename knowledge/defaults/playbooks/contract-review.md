# Contract Review Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when reviewing any commercial contract (SaaS, services, licensing, procurement) received from a counterparty or drafted internally. This is the primary workflow for evaluating contract risk and producing a redline or issues list.

## Prerequisites
- Load applicable position files from knowledge/defaults/positions/
- Load relevant legal area files from knowledge/law/areas/
- Identify the contract type and counterparty relationship (vendor, customer, partner)
- Obtain any prior correspondence or term sheets that inform the deal context
- Confirm the business owner and their priorities

## Process

### Step 1: Initial Classification
Classify the contract by type, value, and risk tier:
- **Type:** SaaS, Professional Services, License, Procurement, Partnership, Reseller, etc.
- **Value:** Annual contract value (ACV) and total contract value (TCV)
- **Risk Tier:** Low (<$100K ACV, standard terms), Medium ($100K-$1M ACV or non-standard terms), High (>$1M ACV, strategic relationship, or significant data processing)

Determine the appropriate depth of review based on risk tier.

### Step 2: Structural Review
Verify the contract contains all necessary components:
- Parties correctly identified (legal entity names, jurisdiction of incorporation)
- Recitals/background accurately describe the relationship
- Definitions section is complete and internally consistent
- Exhibits, schedules, and order forms are attached and referenced
- Signature blocks match the parties
- Effective date and term are clearly stated
- Order of precedence clause addresses conflicts between main agreement and exhibits

### Step 3: Commercial Terms Review
Evaluate core commercial terms against business requirements:
- Scope of services/license matches what was negotiated
- Pricing, payment terms, and fee structure align with the proposal
- Term length and renewal mechanics are acceptable
- SLAs and performance standards meet operational requirements
- Acceptance criteria for deliverables are defined
- Change order process is workable

### Step 4: Risk Assessment — Apply Position Files
For each major clause category, compare the contract language against the relevant position file and assign a GREEN/YELLOW/RED classification:
- Limitation of Liability
- Indemnification
- Termination & Renewal
- Data Protection
- Confidentiality
- IP Ownership
- Dispute Resolution
- Assignment & Change of Control
- Force Majeure
- Insurance Requirements
- Representations & Warranties
- Fees & Payment

### Step 5: Legal Compliance Check
Verify compliance with applicable legal requirements:
- Data protection / privacy law compliance (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Export control and sanctions provisions
- Anti-corruption / anti-bribery provisions
- Industry-specific regulatory requirements
- Governing law and jurisdiction appropriateness

### Step 6: Produce Issues List or Redline
For each identified issue:
1. State the clause reference and current language
2. Identify the risk (RED/YELLOW/GREEN)
3. Explain the concern
4. Propose alternative language or a negotiation position
5. Note whether the issue is a deal-breaker or a negotiation point

### Step 7: Internal Alignment
- Share the issues list with the business owner
- Identify which issues are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
- Agree on the negotiation strategy before sending the redline
- Document any business-approved deviations from standard positions

## Output Format
Produce a structured issues list or memorandum with:
1. **Executive Summary:** Contract type, counterparty, value, risk tier, overall assessment
2. **Issues Table:** Clause | Risk Level | Issue | Proposed Resolution | Priority
3. **Redline:** If applicable, marked-up contract with proposed changes
4. **Recommendation:** Approve, approve with conditions, or reject

## Calibration
- **Simple (Low Risk):** Focus on Steps 1, 4, and 6. Spot-check key clauses against positions. Target: 1-2 hours.
- **Standard (Medium Risk):** Complete all steps. Full position-file comparison. Target: 4-8 hours.
- **Complex (High Risk):** Complete all steps with deep analysis. Cross-reference legal area files. Engage subject-matter experts. Consider board/executive approval requirements. Target: 1-3 days.
