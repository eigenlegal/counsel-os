# Board & Governance Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when supporting board of directors meetings, committee meetings, governance matters, or corporate actions requiring board or shareholder approval. This includes preparing board materials, managing corporate minutes, handling written consents, and advising on fiduciary duties.

## Prerequisites
- Corporate governance documents (certificate of incorporation, bylaws, shareholder agreements)
- Board and committee charters
- Current board composition and committee assignments
- Meeting schedule and upcoming agenda items
- Prior board minutes and resolutions
- D&O insurance policy details
- Delegation of authority matrix

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Governance Requirement
Determine what triggers the governance action:
- **Regular Board Meeting:** Quarterly or as scheduled per the bylaws
- **Special Board Meeting:** Urgent matter requiring board action between regular meetings
- **Committee Meeting:** Audit, compensation, nominating/governance, or other committee
- **Written Consent:** Board action without a meeting (verify bylaws permit this)
- **Shareholder Action:** Annual meeting, special meeting, or written consent
- **Corporate Action:** Merger, acquisition, financing, equity issuance, officer appointment, material contract

Verify the governance requirements for the specific action:
- Is board approval required? (check bylaws, delegation of authority, shareholder agreements)
- Is committee approval or recommendation required?
- Is shareholder approval required?
- Are there any contractual consent requirements (e.g., investor consent rights)?
- What are the quorum requirements?
- What vote threshold is required (majority, supermajority, unanimous)?

### Step 2: Prepare the Meeting
For board and committee meetings:

**Notice:**
- Review bylaws for notice requirements (timing, form, content)
- Prepare and distribute notice within the required timeframe
- Include date, time, location (or virtual meeting details), and agenda
- For special meetings, state the purpose(s) for which the meeting is called

**Board Package:**
- Prepare and distribute materials at least 5-7 days in advance (or per governance guidelines)
- Standard board package includes:
  - Agenda
  - Prior meeting minutes for approval
  - CEO/management report
  - Financial statements and KPIs
  - Committee reports
  - Matters requiring board action (with supporting memoranda)
  - Any materials referenced in the agenda

**Resolutions:**
- Draft resolutions for all matters requiring formal board action
- Include appropriate recitals providing context
- Ensure resolutions are self-contained and clearly state the action being authorized
- Include delegation of authority for implementation details where appropriate
- Prepare in advance for legal review

### Step 3: Support the Meeting
During the meeting:
- Confirm quorum is present (in person or by valid remote participation)
- Note attendance, including arrivals and departures
- Record all actions taken and votes (including dissents and abstentions)
- Flag any conflicts of interest — directors should recuse from interested transactions
- Ensure proper handling of executive sessions (outside directors only)
- Note any follow-up items and assigned responsibilities
- If legal advice is provided, note that the advice was given in the presence of counsel (privilege)

### Step 4: Prepare Minutes
Draft minutes promptly after the meeting:
- Minutes should be a summary of actions taken, not a transcript
- Include: date, time, location, attendees, quorum confirmation
- Record each matter discussed and the action taken (approved, tabled, deferred)
- Record vote results, including any dissents or abstentions
- Note conflicts of interest and recusals
- Record any delegations of authority granted
- Include attachments or references to materials presented
- Circulate draft minutes for review before the next meeting
- Present minutes for approval at the next regular meeting

### Step 5: Handle Written Consents
When action is taken by written consent:
- Verify bylaws and applicable law permit action by written consent
- For Delaware corporations: Written consent must be signed by the minimum number of votes required for the action at a meeting with all directors present (typically majority of the board)
- Draft the written consent with appropriate recitals and resolutions
- Circulate to all directors (not just those signing)
- Collect signatures and file with corporate records
- Note the effective date (when the required number of signatures is obtained)

### Step 6: Annual Governance Review
Conduct periodic governance maintenance:
- Review and update the delegation of authority matrix
- Verify D&O insurance coverage and limits
- Review board and committee charters annually
- Assess board composition (independence, expertise, diversity)
- Review related-party transactions
- Update corporate records and minute books
- File required annual reports and franchise tax payments
- Review compliance with any governance-related contractual obligations (e.g., investor rights agreements)

## Output Format
1. **Meeting Notice:** Formal notice per bylaws requirements
2. **Board Package:** Agenda, minutes, reports, and supporting materials
3. **Resolutions:** Formal resolutions for all actions requiring board approval
4. **Minutes:** Approved minutes filed in the corporate minute book
5. **Written Consents:** Executed consents filed in the corporate records
6. **Governance Calendar:** Annual schedule of meetings, filings, and reviews

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Routine board meeting with standard agenda items. Target: 3-5 days preparation.
- **Standard:** Board meeting with material transactions requiring approval (significant contract, financing, hiring). Target: 1-2 weeks preparation.
- **Complex:** Board meeting involving M&A, major litigation, restructuring, or contested matters. Target: 2-4 weeks preparation. Engage outside counsel for transaction-specific advice. Prepare detailed memoranda on fiduciary duty considerations.
