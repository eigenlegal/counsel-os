# NDA Triage Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when receiving or initiating a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). NDAs are high-volume, and this playbook enables rapid triage to determine whether the NDA can be signed on template, requires minor edits, or needs full legal review.

## Prerequisites
- Load position file: knowledge/defaults/positions/confidentiality.md
- Identify the business purpose for the NDA (vendor evaluation, partnership discussion, M&A, employment)
- Determine the direction of disclosure: mutual, one-way (disclosing), or one-way (receiving)
- Confirm whether we have a standard NDA template to propose as an alternative

## Process

### Step 1: Template Check
Determine if the NDA is:
- **Our template:** Verify it has not been modified. If unmodified, approve immediately.
- **Counterparty template:** Proceed to Step 2 for triage review.
- **Industry standard (e.g., Bonterms mutual NDA):** Verify version, then expedite review.

### Step 2: Rapid Triage (5-Minute Assessment)
Review the following six critical elements:

1. **Mutual vs. One-Way:** Is the NDA mutual? If one-way, does the direction match our needs? A mutual NDA is preferred for most commercial relationships.

2. **Duration:** Is the confidentiality period between 2-5 years? Flag if under 2 years or over 7 years. Trade secret carve-out for perpetual protection should be present.

3. **Definition of Confidential Information:** Is the definition reasonably broad and covers oral, written, and electronic disclosures? Flag if it requires marking/labeling of all information.

4. **Permitted Use:** Is the permitted use limited to the stated business purpose? Flag if it permits broad or undefined use.

5. **Residuals Clause:** Does the NDA contain a residuals clause permitting use of "ideas, concepts, or techniques" retained in unaided memory? Flag for review if present — these can significantly undermine protections.

6. **Non-Standard Provisions:** Flag any provisions that do not belong in a standard NDA:
   - Non-solicitation / non-compete clauses
   - Standstill provisions
   - Exclusivity obligations
   - Broad IP assignment or license grants
   - Indemnification obligations
   - Liquidated damages

### Step 3: Classification
Based on the triage, classify the NDA:

- **FAST TRACK:** All six elements are acceptable. No non-standard provisions. Approve for signature with no further review.
- **MINOR EDITS:** 1-2 elements need adjustment but are not unusual. Make targeted edits and send back. No escalation needed.
- **FULL REVIEW:** 3+ elements need attention, non-standard provisions present, or the NDA relates to a high-sensitivity matter (M&A, competitive intelligence). Proceed with detailed review per Step 4.

### Step 4: Full Review (If Required)
Apply the full confidentiality position file analysis:
- Compare all provisions against the classification guide
- Draft a redline with proposed changes
- Flag any provisions that require business input (e.g., non-solicitation period, standstill)
- If the counterparty's NDA is significantly non-standard, consider proposing our template instead

### Step 5: Execution
- Route for signature per the signing authority matrix
- Log the NDA in the contract management system with key metadata:
  - Parties, effective date, expiration date, direction (mutual/one-way)
  - Any non-standard provisions accepted
  - Business owner and purpose

## Output Format
- **FAST TRACK:** Brief confirmation email to business owner: "NDA reviewed, approved for signature."
- **MINOR EDITS:** Redlined NDA with cover note explaining 1-2 changes.
- **FULL REVIEW:** Issues list with risk classifications and proposed redline.

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Mutual NDA on a recognized template or our paper. Target: 10-15 minutes.
- **Standard:** Counterparty template with 1-3 issues. Target: 30-60 minutes.
- **Complex:** NDA with non-standard provisions, M&A context, or competitive sensitivity. Target: 2-4 hours.
