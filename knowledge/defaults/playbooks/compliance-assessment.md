# Compliance Assessment Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when evaluating whether a product, service, process, or business initiative complies with applicable laws, regulations, and internal policies. Common triggers include new product launches, entry into new markets, regulatory changes, and periodic compliance reviews.

## Prerequisites
- Clear description of the product, service, or initiative being assessed
- Identification of applicable jurisdictions
- Relevant regulatory frameworks identified from knowledge/law/areas/
- Internal policies and compliance standards gathered
- Stakeholder interviews scheduled or completed
- Prior audit findings or compliance assessments reviewed

## Process

### Step 1: Scope Definition
Define the boundaries of the assessment:
- **Subject:** What product, service, process, or initiative is being assessed?
- **Jurisdictions:** Which countries, states, or regions are in scope?
- **Regulatory Domains:** Which areas of law apply (privacy, securities, employment, anti-corruption, export control, industry-specific)?
- **Standards:** Which internal policies and external standards apply (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)?
- **Timeline:** Point-in-time assessment or ongoing monitoring?

### Step 2: Regulatory Mapping
Map applicable regulations to the subject:
- Identify all applicable statutes and regulations
- Determine which regulatory bodies have oversight
- Map specific requirements to business functions and processes
- Identify any exemptions or safe harbors that may apply
- Note upcoming regulatory changes that may affect compliance
- Cross-reference with knowledge/law/areas/ for detailed requirements

### Step 3: Gap Analysis
Compare current state against requirements:
- Document current controls, policies, and procedures
- Identify gaps between current state and regulatory requirements
- Classify gaps by severity:
  - **Critical:** Immediate legal exposure; enforcement risk; must remediate before launch
  - **High:** Significant non-compliance; remediation required within 30 days
  - **Medium:** Partial compliance; remediation required within 90 days
  - **Low:** Best practice gaps; remediate within 6 months
- Assess the likelihood and impact of each gap being exploited or discovered

### Step 4: Evidence Collection
Gather evidence of compliance:
- Review documentation (policies, procedures, training records)
- Examine technical controls (access controls, encryption, logging)
- Interview process owners and operators
- Review third-party certifications and audit reports
- Test controls where possible (sampling, walkthroughs)
- Document evidence gathered and any limitations

### Step 5: Risk Assessment
Evaluate the overall risk profile:
- Aggregate gap findings into a risk heat map
- Consider the regulatory enforcement environment
- Assess the likelihood of regulatory inquiry or audit
- Evaluate potential penalties (fines, sanctions, license revocation)
- Consider reputational risk and customer impact
- Benchmark against industry peers where possible

### Step 6: Remediation Plan
Develop a prioritized remediation roadmap:
- For each gap, propose specific remediation actions
- Assign ownership and deadlines
- Estimate resource requirements (budget, personnel, technology)
- Identify quick wins vs. long-term structural changes
- Define interim risk mitigation measures for gaps that cannot be immediately resolved
- Establish metrics and milestones for tracking progress

### Step 7: Report and Monitor
Deliver findings and establish ongoing compliance:
- Present findings to stakeholders and leadership
- Obtain sign-off on the remediation plan
- Establish a monitoring cadence (quarterly, semi-annually)
- Integrate compliance requirements into existing business processes
- Set up alerts for regulatory changes affecting the assessment scope
- Schedule follow-up assessments to verify remediation

## Output Format
1. **Executive Summary:** Scope, overall risk rating, critical findings
2. **Regulatory Map:** Matrix of applicable regulations and requirements
3. **Gap Analysis:** Detailed findings with severity classifications
4. **Risk Assessment:** Heat map and narrative risk analysis
5. **Remediation Plan:** Prioritized actions with owners, deadlines, and resources
6. **Appendices:** Evidence inventory, regulatory references, interview summaries

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Single jurisdiction, established regulatory framework, periodic refresh. Target: 1-2 weeks.
- **Standard:** Multiple jurisdictions or regulatory domains, new product or market. Target: 3-6 weeks.
- **Complex:** Heavily regulated industry, multiple jurisdictions, novel regulatory questions, enforcement action response. Target: 2-3 months. Consider engaging outside counsel or specialized compliance consultants.
