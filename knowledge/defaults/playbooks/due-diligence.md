# Due Diligence Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when conducting legal due diligence on a target company in connection with a potential acquisition, merger, investment, or strategic partnership. Also applicable to vendor due diligence for critical/strategic vendor relationships.

## Prerequisites
- Signed NDA with the target/counterparty
- Access to the data room or document repository
- Due diligence checklist prepared (see checklists/due-diligence.md)
- Deal team identified (legal, finance, tax, HR, IT, operations)
- Timeline and key milestones established
- Materiality thresholds defined with the deal team
- Outside counsel engaged (if applicable)

## Process

### Step 1: Organize the Review
Set up the due diligence framework:
- Define workstreams and assign leads:
  - **Corporate/Governance:** Entity structure, organizational documents, board minutes, capitalization
  - **Contracts:** Material contracts, customer agreements, vendor agreements, partnership agreements
  - **IP:** Patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, licenses, open-source usage
  - **Litigation & Disputes:** Pending/threatened litigation, regulatory proceedings, settlements
  - **Employment:** Employee agreements, benefit plans, labor issues, key personnel
  - **Regulatory/Compliance:** Permits, licenses, regulatory filings, compliance programs
  - **Data Privacy & Security:** Data processing activities, privacy policies, breach history, certifications
  - **Real Estate & Assets:** Leases, owned property, material assets
  - **Tax:** Tax returns, audits, transfer pricing, NOLs
  - **Insurance:** Coverage types, limits, claims history
  - **Environmental:** Environmental liabilities, compliance, permits
- Create a document request list organized by workstream
- Establish a tracking system for documents requested, received, and reviewed
- Set up regular deal team check-ins

### Step 2: Document Collection
Manage the information gathering process:
- Submit the initial document request list to the target
- Track responses and follow up on missing documents
- Organize received documents by workstream in a structured folder system
- Flag documents that are incomplete, redacted, or raise immediate concerns
- Request management presentations and Q&A sessions for key areas
- Conduct site visits if applicable

### Step 3: Substantive Review
For each workstream, conduct a thorough review:

**Corporate/Governance:**
- Verify entity structure and good standing in all jurisdictions
- Review organizational documents for unusual provisions
- Review board and shareholder minutes for undisclosed commitments or disputes
- Verify capitalization table and equity instruments

**Contracts:**
- Identify all material contracts (by value, strategic importance, or risk)
- Review change of control / assignment provisions (will the deal trigger any consents?)
- Identify contracts with termination-on-change-of-control provisions
- Review customer concentration risk
- Identify any most-favored-nation, exclusivity, or non-compete provisions

**IP:**
- Verify ownership of key IP assets
- Review IP assignment agreements from employees and contractors
- Assess open-source usage and license compliance
- Identify any IP litigation or infringement claims
- Review key technology licenses (inbound and outbound)

**Litigation & Disputes:**
- Catalog all pending and threatened claims
- Assess potential exposure for each matter
- Review settlement agreements for ongoing obligations
- Identify patterns suggesting systemic issues

**Employment:**
- Review key employee agreements and non-competes
- Assess retention risk for critical personnel
- Review benefit plans for unfunded liabilities
- Identify any employment disputes or EEOC/labor board matters

**Data Privacy & Security:**
- Assess data processing activities and legal bases
- Review privacy policies and consent mechanisms
- Evaluate security posture and breach history
- Identify cross-border data transfer issues
- Review sub-processor relationships

### Step 4: Risk Assessment
Synthesize findings into an actionable risk analysis:
- Categorize findings by risk level:
  - **Deal-Breaker:** Issues that could prevent the transaction
  - **Significant:** Issues requiring purchase price adjustment or specific indemnification
  - **Moderate:** Issues requiring post-closing remediation
  - **Low:** Noted but manageable issues
- Quantify financial exposure where possible
- Identify issues requiring rep/warranty/indemnity coverage in the acquisition agreement
- Assess the overall risk profile of the target

### Step 5: Diligence Report
Prepare the due diligence report:
- Executive summary with key findings and recommendation
- Workstream-by-workstream detailed findings
- Risk matrix with severity, likelihood, and financial impact
- List of items requiring specific deal protections (reps, warranties, indemnities, escrow)
- List of consents required to close
- Recommendations for post-closing integration items

### Step 6: Deal Protection
Translate diligence findings into deal terms:
- Draft specific representations and warranties to address identified risks
- Recommend indemnification provisions for known and unknown liabilities
- Propose escrow or holdback amounts tied to specific risks
- Identify conditions precedent to closing
- Draft disclosure schedule items
- Recommend post-closing covenants for remediation items

## Output Format
1. **Executive Summary:** 2-3 pages covering key findings, deal-breakers, and overall recommendation
2. **Detailed Report:** Workstream-by-workstream analysis with findings, risk ratings, and supporting references
3. **Risk Matrix:** Heat map of all identified risks by severity and likelihood
4. **Deal Points Memo:** Recommended provisions for the acquisition agreement
5. **Open Items List:** Outstanding requests and unresolved questions
6. **Consent List:** All third-party consents required for closing

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Vendor due diligence or small acquisition (<$5M), limited scope. Target: 2-4 weeks.
- **Standard:** Mid-market acquisition ($5M-$100M), standard scope. Target: 4-8 weeks.
- **Complex:** Large acquisition (>$100M), cross-border, regulated industry, carve-out transaction. Target: 8-16 weeks. Dedicated deal team with outside counsel support.
