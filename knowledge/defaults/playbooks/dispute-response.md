# Dispute Response Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when the company receives a demand letter, threat of litigation, notice of breach, regulatory inquiry, or any formal or informal dispute communication from a counterparty, regulatory body, or third party.

## Prerequisites
- The dispute communication (demand letter, notice, complaint, inquiry)
- Relevant contracts and agreements with the counterparty
- Load relevant position files (especially dispute-resolution.md, limitation-of-liability.md, indemnification.md)
- Internal facts gathered from the business team
- Insurance policies reviewed for potential coverage
- Litigation hold assessment completed or in progress
- Outside counsel identified (if needed)

## Process

### Step 1: Triage and Classify
Assess the dispute immediately upon receipt:
- **Severity Level:**
  - **Critical:** Lawsuit filed, regulatory enforcement action, injunction sought, significant financial exposure (>$500K)
  - **High:** Formal demand letter with litigation threat, regulatory inquiry, breach notice with termination threat
  - **Medium:** Informal complaint, fee dispute, performance issue, warranty claim
  - **Low:** Customer complaint, minor contractual disagreement, invoice dispute
- **Response Deadline:** Identify any deadlines (answer to complaint, cure periods, regulatory response dates)
- **Privilege:** Assess whether communications should be marked privileged; engage outside counsel early if litigation is reasonably anticipated

### Step 2: Preserve Evidence
Immediately upon identifying a potential dispute:
- Issue a litigation hold notice to all relevant custodians
- Preserve all relevant documents, communications, and electronic records
- Suspend any document retention/destruction schedules for relevant materials
- Identify key custodians and relevant data sources
- Document the preservation steps taken
- See checklist: checklists/litigation-hold.md

### Step 3: Gather Facts
Conduct an internal investigation:
- Interview relevant business stakeholders (document interviews)
- Collect and review relevant contracts, correspondence, and records
- Establish a timeline of key events
- Identify favorable and unfavorable facts
- Assess the credibility and strength of the counterparty's claims
- Identify potential counterclaims or defenses

### Step 4: Legal Analysis
Analyze the dispute on the merits:
- Review the applicable contract provisions (breach, cure, remedies, dispute resolution)
- Identify applicable legal theories and defenses
- Assess the counterparty's likely damages claim and our exposure
- Evaluate the strength of our defenses (0-100% confidence)
- Identify potential counterclaims
- Review the dispute resolution clause — determine the required forum and process
- Check insurance coverage for defense costs and potential liability
- Assess whether indemnification obligations (ours or the counterparty's) apply

### Step 5: Develop Response Strategy
Determine the approach:
- **Resolve:** The claim has merit; negotiate a resolution (settlement, cure, commercial accommodation)
- **Defend:** The claim lacks merit; prepare a formal response disputing the allegations
- **Defer:** More information needed; respond acknowledging receipt without admitting or conceding
- **Escalate:** Critical exposure requiring executive and/or board notification

For each approach, consider:
- Cost of resolution vs. cost of defense
- Business relationship implications
- Precedent-setting concerns
- Reputational impact
- Insurance coverage implications

### Step 6: Draft Response
Prepare the formal response:
- **Tone:** Professional and measured. Do not escalate unnecessarily. Do not make admissions.
- **Content:**
  - Acknowledge receipt of the communication
  - Reserve all rights and defenses (do not waive any rights)
  - If disputing, state the grounds clearly and concisely
  - If seeking cure, outline the cure plan and timeline
  - If settling, propose terms (without admitting liability)
  - Reference the dispute resolution clause and applicable procedures
- **Review:** Have the response reviewed by senior counsel before sending
- **Delivery:** Send via the method specified in the contract's notice provisions

### Step 7: Manage to Resolution
Track the dispute through resolution:
- Maintain a dispute log with key dates, communications, and decisions
- Calendar all deadlines (response dates, cure periods, statute of limitations)
- Report status to leadership on a regular cadence
- Document settlement negotiations and any offers (for privilege and insurance purposes)
- If the dispute escalates to formal proceedings, engage outside counsel
- Conduct a post-resolution review to identify lessons learned and process improvements

## Output Format
1. **Triage Assessment:** Severity, exposure estimate, response deadline, privilege designation
2. **Fact Summary:** Timeline and key facts
3. **Legal Analysis:** Merits assessment, defenses, exposure range
4. **Strategy Recommendation:** Resolve/Defend/Defer/Escalate with rationale
5. **Draft Response:** Formal response letter or communication
6. **Action Items:** Next steps with owners and deadlines

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Low-severity dispute (invoice disagreement, minor service complaint). Target: Initial response within 2-3 business days.
- **Standard:** Medium-severity dispute (breach notice, warranty claim, regulatory inquiry). Target: Initial response within 5-10 business days; strategy developed within 2 weeks.
- **Complex:** High/Critical-severity dispute (litigation, regulatory enforcement, significant exposure). Target: Engage outside counsel within 24-48 hours; preservation notice within 24 hours; initial strategy within 1 week.
