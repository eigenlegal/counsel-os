# Legal Memo Playbook

## When to Use
Use this playbook when a business stakeholder or executive requests legal analysis on a specific question, risk, or proposed course of action. Legal memos document the analysis and recommendation for the record and may be subject to attorney-client privilege.

## Prerequisites
- Clear articulation of the question or issue to be analyzed
- Identification of the requesting party and audience
- Understanding of the business context and urgency
- Relevant facts gathered from stakeholders
- Applicable law identified from knowledge/law/areas/
- Privilege considerations assessed (mark as privileged and confidential if appropriate)

## Process

### Step 1: Define the Question
Articulate the precise legal question(s) to be answered:
- Restate the business question as a legal question
- Identify the relevant jurisdiction(s)
- Confirm scope: Is this a narrow question or a comprehensive analysis?
- Confirm timeline: When does the business need the answer?
- Identify whether this is a question of first impression or a recurring issue with established internal positions

### Step 2: Gather Facts
Collect and document all relevant facts:
- Interview the business stakeholder
- Review relevant contracts, correspondence, and internal documents
- Identify any factual assumptions that may affect the analysis
- Note any facts that are uncertain or disputed
- Document the source of each material fact

### Step 3: Research Applicable Law
Conduct legal research:
- Identify the governing jurisdiction and applicable law
- Review relevant statutes, regulations, and case law
- Check for recent legislative or regulatory changes
- Review internal precedent (prior memos on similar topics)
- Consult knowledge/law/areas/ files for relevant legal frameworks
- Identify any pending legislation or regulatory guidance that may affect the analysis

### Step 4: Analyze
Apply the law to the facts:
- Consider multiple legal theories or interpretations
- Assess the strength of each position (strong, moderate, weak)
- Identify risks and potential adverse outcomes
- Consider counterarguments and how they would be addressed
- Evaluate the practical business impact of each interpretation
- Distinguish between legal risk and business risk

### Step 5: Formulate Recommendation
Develop a clear, actionable recommendation:
- State the conclusion directly (do not bury the lead)
- Quantify risk where possible (likelihood and severity)
- Propose concrete next steps
- Identify risk mitigation measures
- Flag any areas requiring further analysis or expert consultation
- Present alternatives if the primary recommendation is not feasible

### Step 6: Draft the Memo
Write the memo following the output format below. Key drafting principles:
- Lead with the conclusion — busy executives read the top first
- Use plain language — avoid unnecessary legal jargon
- Be precise about certainty levels ("likely," "probably," "unclear," "remote risk")
- Separate legal analysis from business judgment
- Include relevant caveats and limitations
- Mark as "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION" if appropriate

### Step 7: Review and Deliver
- Self-review for accuracy, clarity, and completeness
- Peer review for significant matters
- Deliver to the requesting party with an offer to discuss
- File in the legal department's knowledge management system
- Calendar any follow-up actions or deadlines identified in the memo

## Output Format
```
PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION

LEGAL MEMORANDUM

TO:      [Requesting party]
FROM:    [Legal team]
DATE:    [Date]
RE:      [Subject — concise description]

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
   [2-3 sentence summary of the question and conclusion]

2. QUESTION PRESENTED
   [Precise legal question(s)]

3. SHORT ANSWER
   [Direct answer in 1-2 paragraphs]

4. FACTS
   [Relevant facts and assumptions]

5. ANALYSIS
   [Detailed legal analysis applying law to facts]

6. CONCLUSION & RECOMMENDATION
   [Actionable recommendation with risk assessment]

7. NEXT STEPS
   [Concrete actions, owners, and deadlines]
```

## Calibration
- **Simple:** Narrow question, clear law, established internal position. Target: 1-2 pages, 2-4 hours.
- **Standard:** Moderate complexity, multiple considerations, some ambiguity. Target: 3-6 pages, 1-2 days.
- **Complex:** Novel issue, multiple jurisdictions, significant business impact, board-level visibility. Target: 8-15 pages, 3-7 days. Consider engaging outside counsel.
