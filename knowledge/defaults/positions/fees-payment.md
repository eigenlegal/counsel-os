# Fees & Payment

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Fees specified in an order form or pricing schedule. Net 30 payment terms from invoice date. Annual price increases capped at CPI or 3-5%, whichever is lower, with 60-90 days' notice. Disputed invoices may be withheld in good faith without triggering breach, provided the undisputed portion is paid timely. Late payment interest at 1-1.5% per month or the maximum legal rate, whichever is lower.

**Acceptable range:** Payment terms of Net 15 to Net 60. Price increase caps of 0-7% annually. Price holds for the initial term with increases only upon renewal. Volume-based discounts or committed spend discounts. Usage-based pricing with transparent metering and audit rights. Late payment interest of 0.5-1.5% per month. Right to suspend services (not terminate) after 15-30 days of non-payment with notice.

**Escalate if:**
- Fees can be increased unilaterally without notice or cap during the term
- Payment terms shorter than Net 15 or require prepayment for extended periods (12+ months)
- No right to dispute invoices in good faith
- Late payment triggers automatic termination rather than suspension
- No audit rights on usage-based billing
- Hidden fees, implementation costs, or charges not disclosed in the agreement
- "Most favored customer" pricing requested without corresponding commitment
- Taxes and duties allocated entirely to one party without regard to applicable law

## Key Considerations

- Pricing models (subscription, usage-based, tiered, per-seat) have different risk profiles
- Prepayment for long periods concentrates risk — consider quarterly billing for large commitments
- Usage-based pricing requires transparent metering and regular usage reports
- True-up mechanisms for overages should be clearly defined
- Currency provisions matter for international contracts — specify the billing currency and who bears exchange rate risk
- Tax gross-up provisions should be mutual and reflect withholding tax requirements
- Benchmark/audit rights for usage-based billing protect against overcharges
- Ensure the agreement is clear about what is included vs. what incurs additional charges (professional services, support tiers, integrations)
- "Shelfware" provisions allow reduction of unused licenses at renewal

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Clear pricing schedule; Net 30 payment terms; annual increases capped at CPI or 3-5% with 60+ days' notice; good-faith dispute rights; transparent usage metering with audit rights; late payment suspension (not termination) after notice; all fees disclosed; reasonable late payment interest.

**YELLOW:** Net 15 payment terms; annual increases capped at 5-7%; no dispute right for invoices; late payment interest above 1.5%/month; limited usage visibility; price increases with only 30 days' notice; some ambiguity in what's included vs. extra.

**RED:** Unilateral price increases without cap or notice; payment terms shorter than Net 15; prepayment for 12+ months without protection; no invoice dispute right; automatic termination for late payment without cure; no usage-based billing transparency; hidden or undisclosed fees; late payment interest exceeding legal limits.
