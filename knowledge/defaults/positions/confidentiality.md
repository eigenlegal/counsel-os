# Confidentiality

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Mutual confidentiality obligations covering all non-public information disclosed by either party. Standard of care: at least the same degree of care used to protect own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care. Duration: 3-5 years from disclosure, or indefinite for trade secrets. Standard exclusions apply (publicly known, independently developed, rightfully received from third parties, required by law).

**Acceptable range:** Confidentiality periods of 2-5 years (longer for trade secrets and source code). Permitted disclosures to employees, contractors, and advisors on a need-to-know basis subject to binding confidentiality obligations. Compelled disclosure permitted with prompt notice and cooperation to seek protective orders. Return or destruction of confidential information upon termination with certification.

**Escalate if:**
- Confidentiality obligations are entirely one-sided
- Duration is less than 2 years for general confidential information
- No trade secret carve-out for extended/perpetual protection
- Receiving party has no obligation to limit internal dissemination
- No return/destruction obligation upon termination
- Compelled disclosure provision lacks notice requirement
- Residuals clause permits unrestricted use of ideas and concepts from confidential information
- No injunctive relief acknowledgment for breach

## Key Considerations

- Residuals clauses can effectively hollow out confidentiality protections — scrutinize carefully
- Definition of "Confidential Information" should be broad enough to capture all sensitive disclosures (written, oral, visual, electronic)
- Marking requirements (requiring information to be marked "Confidential") can create gaps — consider whether oral/visual disclosures need follow-up written designation
- Employee/contractor flow-down obligations must be robust
- Consider whether the agreement permits disclosure to potential acquirers or investors under NDA
- Remedies should acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate and equitable relief is appropriate
- Distinguish between confidentiality obligations in standalone NDAs vs. embedded in commercial agreements

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Mutual obligations; reasonable care standard; 3-5 year duration with trade secret carve-out; standard exclusions; need-to-know dissemination limits; return/destruction upon termination with certification; compelled disclosure with notice; injunctive relief acknowledged.

**YELLOW:** 2-year duration; marking requirements for all information (including oral); broad residuals clause; no explicit injunctive relief provision; permitted disclosure to affiliates without binding obligations; no certification of destruction.

**RED:** One-sided obligations; duration under 2 years; no trade secret protection; broad residuals clause that permits use of concepts and ideas without restriction; no return/destruction obligation; no notice for compelled disclosure; receiving party can share with affiliates freely.
