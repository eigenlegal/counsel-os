# Termination & Renewal

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Either party may terminate for material breach with 30 days' written notice and a cure period. Either party may terminate for insolvency/bankruptcy. Contracts auto-renew for successive 1-year terms unless either party provides 60-90 days' written notice of non-renewal before the end of the then-current term. Termination for convenience by either party with 30-90 days' notice (more common in services agreements).

**Acceptable range:** Cure periods of 15-45 days for breach. Non-renewal notice windows of 30-120 days. Auto-renewal terms matching the initial term length or converting to month-to-month. Termination for convenience may be limited to one party or eliminated entirely in committed-term deals. Post-termination transition assistance of 30-90 days at then-current rates.

**Escalate if:**
- No termination for cause right exists
- Cure period exceeds 60 days or is entirely at breaching party's discretion
- Auto-renewal locks in for multi-year terms without adequate notice provisions
- No termination right upon change of control of the other party
- Termination penalties exceed reasonable early-termination fees (e.g., remaining committed fees)
- No post-termination data return/deletion obligations
- Vendor can terminate for convenience but customer cannot

## Key Considerations

- Ensure data portability and return obligations survive termination
- Transition assistance provisions protect business continuity
- Wind-down periods should be sufficient for migration to alternative solutions
- Early termination fees should be proportionate — avoid liquidated damages that function as penalties
- Automatic renewal terms should not escalate pricing without caps or notice
- Survival clauses should be explicitly listed (confidentiality, indemnification, limitation of liability)
- Consider the practical impact of termination on dependent systems and integrations

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Mutual termination for cause with 30-day cure; mutual termination for convenience with 60+ days' notice; auto-renewal with 60-90 day non-renewal notice; post-termination data return within 30 days; reasonable transition assistance; clear survival provisions.

**YELLOW:** Asymmetric termination convenience rights; auto-renewal notice window under 60 days or over 120 days; no transition assistance; cure period over 45 days; early termination fees exceeding remaining committed value; no explicit data return timeline.

**RED:** No termination for cause right; auto-renewal into multi-year terms with short notice windows; vendor-only termination for convenience; punitive early termination fees; no data return or deletion obligations; no survival clause for key provisions.
