# Insurance Requirements

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Vendor maintains commercially reasonable insurance coverage including: Commercial General Liability (CGL) of $1-2M per occurrence / $2-4M aggregate; Professional Liability / Errors & Omissions (E&O) of $2-5M per claim; Cyber Liability / Technology E&O of $5-10M (for technology vendors processing personal data); Workers' Compensation per statutory requirements; Umbrella/Excess Liability of $5-10M. Coverage must be maintained during the term and for 2-3 years after termination.

**Acceptable range:** CGL: $1-5M per occurrence. Professional Liability: $1-10M depending on contract value and risk. Cyber Liability: $2-25M depending on volume and sensitivity of data processed. Workers' Comp: statutory minimums. Umbrella: $2-25M. Additional insured status for CGL. Certificate of insurance provided annually. Tail coverage of 1-3 years post-termination for claims-made policies.

**Escalate if:**
- Vendor refuses to carry cyber liability insurance while processing personal or sensitive data
- Coverage amounts are disproportionately low relative to contract value or risk exposure
- No professional liability / E&O coverage for a services engagement
- Vendor will not provide certificates of insurance upon request
- No requirement to maintain coverage post-termination for claims-made policies
- Insurance requirements are entirely one-sided (imposed on customer but not vendor)
- Vendor's insurance contains exclusions that would void coverage for likely claim scenarios
- Self-insured retention is unreasonably high

## Key Considerations

- Insurance requirements should be proportionate to the contract value, data sensitivity, and risk profile
- Cyber liability coverage is essential for any vendor processing personal data or having access to company systems
- Claims-made policies require tail coverage — verify the retroactive date covers the full contract period
- Additional insured status on CGL provides direct rights under the vendor's policy
- Waiver of subrogation may be appropriate to prevent insurer from pursuing claims against the other party
- Primary and non-contributory endorsement ensures vendor's coverage responds first
- Verify that the vendor's deductible/self-insured retention is reasonable relative to their financial capacity
- Industry-specific insurance may be required (e.g., media liability, clinical trials insurance)

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Appropriate coverage types and amounts proportionate to risk; cyber liability coverage for data-processing vendors; certificates of insurance provided annually; additional insured status; primary and non-contributory; waiver of subrogation; tail coverage for claims-made policies; 30 days' notice of material change or cancellation.

**YELLOW:** Coverage amounts at lower end of range; no additional insured status; no waiver of subrogation; certificates available on request rather than proactively; no explicit tail coverage requirement; self-insured retention slightly high; 10 days' notice of cancellation.

**RED:** No cyber liability for data-processing vendors; coverage amounts materially below market standard; vendor refuses to provide certificates; no professional liability for services engagements; no post-termination tail coverage; self-insured retention that effectively eliminates coverage; material exclusions in policies that void coverage for foreseeable claims.
