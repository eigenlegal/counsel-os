# IP Ownership

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing IP. Customer owns all customer data and customer-generated content. Vendor retains ownership of its platform, software, tools, and methodologies. Work product created specifically for the customer under a services engagement is typically owned by the customer (work-for-hire or assignment), subject to a license-back to the vendor for its pre-existing IP and general knowledge/tools embedded therein.

**Acceptable range:** Vendor ownership of custom deliverables with a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to customer is an alternative to full assignment. Vendor may retain rights to generalized improvements, methodologies, and know-how developed during the engagement. Joint ownership is disfavored but occasionally acceptable for co-developed innovations with clear exploitation rights defined.

**Escalate if:**
- Vendor claims ownership of customer data or customer-generated content
- No license grant to customer for vendor pre-existing IP embedded in deliverables
- Vendor claims ownership of custom work product without adequate license to customer
- Assignment provisions lack present-tense assignment language ("hereby assigns" vs. "agrees to assign")
- No moral rights waiver where applicable
- Vendor retains rights to use customer-specific deliverables for other clients without restriction
- IP assignment does not survive termination
- No provision for escrowed source code if vendor retains IP in critical deliverables

## Key Considerations

- Distinguish between (a) vendor's platform/SaaS IP, (b) custom deliverables/work product, and (c) customer data/content
- "Work made for hire" doctrine varies by jurisdiction — belt-and-suspenders approach (work-for-hire + assignment) is prudent
- License-back provisions for vendor pre-existing IP must be broad enough for customer to fully use the deliverables
- Consider whether vendor can use aggregated/anonymized data derived from customer usage — this should be explicitly addressed
- Open-source components in deliverables should be disclosed and license-compatible
- AI-generated content ownership is evolving — clarify ownership of outputs generated using AI tools
- Escrow arrangements protect customer if vendor ceases operations

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Clear delineation of pre-existing IP; customer owns customer data; custom work product assigned to customer with vendor license-back for pre-existing tools; present-tense assignment language; moral rights waiver; vendor's use of aggregated data explicitly addressed and limited; open-source disclosure.

**YELLOW:** Vendor retains ownership of custom deliverables with broad perpetual license to customer; no explicit moral rights waiver; vague treatment of aggregated/anonymized data; no open-source disclosure requirement; no escrow provision.

**RED:** Vendor claims ownership of customer data; no license to customer for vendor IP in deliverables; broad vendor rights to use customer-specific work for other clients; no assignment or license survives termination; vendor claims rights to AI-generated outputs using customer data; no distinction between pre-existing IP and new work product.
