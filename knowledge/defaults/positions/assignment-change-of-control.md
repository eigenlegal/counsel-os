# Assignment & Change of Control

## Market Standard

**Typical position:** Neither party may assign the agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, not to be unreasonably withheld. Exception: either party may assign to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets without consent, provided the assignee assumes all obligations.

**Acceptable range:** Assignment restrictions with consent (not unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed). Affiliate assignment permitted with notice. Change of control provisions may include a right to terminate within 30-60 days if the acquiring entity is a direct competitor. Anti-assignment provisions may be coupled with a right of first refusal or notification requirement rather than consent.

**Escalate if:**
- Vendor can freely assign but customer cannot
- No change of control provision — agreement is silent on M&A scenarios
- Assignment to a direct competitor is unrestricted
- Change of control triggers automatic termination rather than an option to terminate
- Assignee does not assume all obligations under the agreement
- Consent to assignment can be withheld in the other party's sole discretion (no reasonableness standard)
- Anti-assignment clause is overly broad and captures internal reorganizations

## Key Considerations

- Define "change of control" clearly — typically 50%+ voting power, merger, or asset sale
- Consider whether a change in control of a parent company triggers the provision
- IPO should generally not constitute a change of control
- Anti-assignment provisions that are too restrictive can impede legitimate business transactions
- Ensure the surviving entity in a merger is bound by the agreement's terms
- Consider the practical implications of assignment on data processing, security, and compliance obligations
- Address what happens to pricing commitments and SLA obligations post-assignment
- Government contracts and regulated industries may have additional assignment restrictions

## Classification Guide

**GREEN:** Mutual anti-assignment with consent (not unreasonably withheld); affiliate assignment with notice; change of control assignment permitted with assumption of obligations; option (not obligation) to terminate if acquirer is a direct competitor; clear definition of change of control excluding IPO.

**YELLOW:** Asymmetric assignment rights slightly favoring one party; no direct competitor restriction; change of control triggers pricing renegotiation; affiliate assignment without notice; vague change of control definition.

**RED:** Entirely one-sided assignment rights; free assignment to competitors without consent; automatic termination on change of control; no assumption of obligations by assignee; consent in sole discretion; assignment clause captures internal reorganizations or IPOs.
