# AI & Data Use — Clause Library

## AI Training Restrictions

### Standard
> Vendor shall not use Customer Data, Customer Confidential Information, or any data derived from Customer's use of the Services to train, develop, improve, or fine-tune any machine learning model, artificial intelligence system, or algorithm, whether owned by Vendor or any third party. This prohibition applies regardless of whether the data is aggregated, anonymized, or de-identified. Vendor shall ensure that its sub-processors comply with this restriction.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall not use Customer Data or any data derived from Customer's use of the Services (including usage patterns, queries, inputs, outputs, feedback, and metadata) to train, develop, improve, validate, benchmark, or fine-tune any machine learning model, AI system, algorithm, or similar technology. This restriction applies to all forms of the data, including aggregated, anonymized, de-identified, and synthetic data derived from Customer Data. Vendor represents that no Customer Data has been or will be included in any training dataset. If Vendor uses AI or ML components in the Services, Vendor shall disclose all such components and confirm they were not trained on Customer Data.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer acknowledges that the Services incorporate machine learning technology that improves through usage. Vendor may use aggregated, anonymized data derived from all customers' use of the Services (including Customer's use) to train, improve, and develop the Services, new features, and Vendor's products. Vendor shall not use identifiable Customer Data for training purposes without Customer's prior consent.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall not use Customer Data to train or develop machine learning models or AI systems without Customer's prior written consent.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language permitting use of "aggregated, anonymized data" for model training is the most contested AI-era provision. Whether anonymization is sufficient depends on the data type, volume, and re-identification risk. Push for an outright prohibition in sensitive data contexts. Even "anonymized" training data can embed patterns attributable to specific customers. The Aggressive version closes loopholes around metadata and synthetic data.

## AI Output Ownership

### Standard
> As between the parties, Customer shall own all outputs, results, reports, and content generated by the Services using Customer Data or Customer inputs ("Customer Outputs"). Vendor shall have no right, title, or interest in Customer Outputs. Vendor grants Customer a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use any Vendor-proprietary algorithms or processes embedded in the Customer Outputs solely to the extent necessary to use the Customer Outputs.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> All outputs, results, analyses, reports, recommendations, and content generated by the Services using Customer Data, Customer inputs, or Customer prompts ("Customer Outputs") shall be the sole and exclusive property of Customer. Vendor hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in Customer Outputs to Customer, including all intellectual property rights. Vendor makes no claim to any Customer Output and shall not use, store, or retain Customer Outputs except as necessary to provide the Services during an active session. Customer acknowledges that AI-generated outputs may not be eligible for intellectual property protection in all jurisdictions.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer may use outputs generated by the Services for its internal business purposes. Vendor retains all rights in the underlying algorithms, models, and technology used to generate outputs. Outputs may be similar or identical to outputs generated for other customers, and Vendor shall have no liability for any similarity. Vendor may use anonymized outputs to improve the Services.

### Minimum Acceptable
> As between the parties, Customer owns all outputs generated by the Services using Customer Data or Customer inputs. Vendor shall not use Customer Outputs for any purpose other than providing the Services.

### Notes
AI output ownership is evolving as IP law catches up with generative AI. The Aggressive version includes an honest acknowledgment that AI-generated outputs may lack IP protection. Vendor-Favorable language retaining rights to "underlying algorithms" while allowing use of "anonymized outputs" blurs the line between model IP and customer content. Push for clear customer ownership of outputs with no vendor retention.

## Algorithmic Accountability and Transparency

### Standard
> If the Services use automated decision-making, machine learning, or AI to process Customer Data or generate outputs that affect individuals, Vendor shall: (a) disclose the use of such technology in the documentation; (b) provide a general description of the logic involved, its significance, and the envisaged consequences; and (c) implement appropriate safeguards to ensure accuracy, minimize bias, and enable human review of automated decisions.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall provide Customer with: (a) a detailed description of all AI and ML components used in the Services, including their purpose, training data sources, and known limitations; (b) documentation of bias testing, fairness assessments, and accuracy metrics; (c) the ability to request human review of any automated decision; (d) an explanation of any specific automated decision upon Customer's request; and (e) prompt notification of material changes to AI or ML components. Vendor shall conduct and share annual algorithmic impact assessments. Vendor shall indemnify Customer against claims arising from biased or discriminatory outputs.

### Vendor-Favorable
> The Services may incorporate AI and ML technology. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the accuracy of AI-generated outputs but does not guarantee that outputs will be error-free, unbiased, or suitable for any particular purpose. Customer is solely responsible for reviewing, validating, and approving all AI-generated outputs before relying on them. Vendor shall have no liability for decisions made based on AI-generated outputs.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If the Services use AI or automated decision-making that affects individuals, Vendor shall disclose such use and provide a general description of the logic involved.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable blanket disclaimers on AI accuracy with full liability shift to Customer may be acceptable for general productivity tools but are inappropriate for high-stakes applications (hiring, lending, healthcare). Push for transparency, bias testing, and human review capabilities. EU AI Act and state AI legislation are creating mandatory requirements for AI transparency and accountability.

## Restrictions on Customer Data Use

### Standard
> Vendor shall use Customer Data solely for the purpose of providing the Services in accordance with this Agreement and Customer's documented instructions. Vendor shall not: (a) sell, rent, or disclose Customer Data to any third party; (b) use Customer Data for advertising, marketing, or profiling; (c) combine Customer Data with data from other sources; or (d) use Customer Data for Vendor's own product development, benchmarking, or competitive purposes.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall process Customer Data solely as necessary to provide the Services in accordance with Customer's written instructions. Vendor shall not: (a) sell, rent, share, or disclose Customer Data; (b) use Customer Data for advertising, marketing, profiling, or behavioral targeting; (c) combine or co-mingle Customer Data with data from other customers or third-party sources; (d) use Customer Data to develop, train, or improve any product, service, or technology; (e) create derivative works from Customer Data; or (f) retain Customer Data after termination except as required by law. These restrictions apply to all Vendor personnel and sub-processors.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor may use Customer Data to provide the Services, maintain system security, generate aggregated analytics, and improve Vendor's products and services. Vendor may use anonymized and aggregated data derived from Customer's use for any lawful purpose, including benchmarking, research, and product development. Vendor may share aggregated analytics with third parties provided they do not identify Customer.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall use Customer Data solely to provide the Services and shall not sell or share Customer Data with third parties for their independent use.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language granting broad rights to use aggregated data for "any lawful purpose" including product development effectively gives Vendor a free license to Customer's data in disguise. Push for explicit prohibitions on training, product development, and third-party sharing. CCPA and state privacy laws increasingly restrict "selling" and "sharing" of personal data, making loose data use terms a regulatory liability.

## Model Governance and Change Management

### Standard
> Vendor shall provide Customer with at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice before making material changes to any AI or ML model, algorithm, or automated decision-making component used in the Services. "Material changes" include changes to training data sources, model architecture, decision thresholds, or output methodology. Vendor shall document all material changes and their expected impact on Service functionality and accuracy.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall not make material changes to any AI or ML model used in the Services without Customer's prior written consent. Vendor shall provide: (a) a detailed description of the proposed change; (b) the rationale for the change; (c) testing and validation results; (d) the impact on accuracy, bias, and performance; and (e) a rollback plan. Customer may opt out of any model change and continue using the prior version for at least twelve (12) months. Vendor shall maintain version history and audit trails for all model changes.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor may update, modify, or replace AI and ML components of the Services at any time as part of its standard product development process. Customer acknowledges that the Services are continuously improved and that model outputs may change over time. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to notify Customer of material changes through its standard release notes or product blog.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall notify Customer of material changes to AI or ML components used in the Services. Customer may terminate the affected Services if a material change adversely impacts Customer's use.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language allowing unilateral model changes with no notice beyond "release notes" is problematic for customers relying on consistent AI outputs for compliance, auditing, or regulated decision-making. Push for advance notice and opt-out rights. In regulated industries (financial services, healthcare), model changes may trigger regulatory obligations such as model risk management reviews.
