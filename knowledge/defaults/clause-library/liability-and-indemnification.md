# Liability & Indemnification — Clause Library

## Limitation of Liability — Cap

### Standard Language
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS (DEFINED BELOW), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

### Aggressive Language
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS (DEFINED BELOW), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, OR OTHERWISE, SHALL EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE TOTAL AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR UNDER THE APPLICABLE ORDER FORM DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE FIRST EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM, OR (B) [DOLLAR AMOUNT]. THIS LIMITATION APPLIES REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES AND REGARDLESS OF WHETHER ANY REMEDY FAILS OF ITS ESSENTIAL PURPOSE.

### Minimum Acceptable
> NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID BY CUSTOMER UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

### Notes
The cap should be based on amounts "paid or payable" rather than just "paid" to ensure adequate recovery early in the contract term. A dollar-amount floor (Aggressive version) protects against low-value contracts with high-risk exposure. Ensure the cap is aggregate across all claims, not per-claim (which could result in exposure significantly exceeding the intended cap).

## Excluded Claims / Super Cap

### Standard Language
> "Excluded Claims" means: (a) either party's indemnification obligations under Section [X]; (b) either party's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section [Y]; (c) Vendor's breach of its data protection obligations under Section [Z]; (d) either party's infringement or misappropriation of the other party's intellectual property rights; and (e) either party's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud. Excluded Claims shall be subject to a separate aggregate cap equal to two (2) times the general liability cap set forth above.

### Aggressive Language
> The following obligations shall not be subject to the limitation of liability set forth above and shall be uncapped: (a) Vendor's indemnification obligations for intellectual property infringement under Section [X]; (b) Vendor's breach of its data protection and security obligations under Section [Z], including any liability arising from a Personal Data Breach; (c) Vendor's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section [Y]; (d) either party's liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence; (e) either party's liability for death or bodily injury caused by its negligence; and (f) Customer's obligation to pay fees due under this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> The limitation of liability shall not apply to: (a) either party's liability for fraud or willful misconduct; (b) either party's liability for death or bodily injury; or (c) Customer's obligation to pay undisputed fees.

### Notes
A super-cap (2x or 3x the general cap) for excluded claims is increasingly common as a middle ground between a general cap and uncapped liability. Fully uncapped liability (Aggressive version) for data breach and IP infringement provides maximum protection but may be resisted by vendors. At minimum, fraud, willful misconduct, and bodily injury should always be carved out.

## Consequential Damages Exclusion

### Standard Language
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR ANY LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, OR GOODWILL, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY AND EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

### Aggressive Language
> IN NO EVENT SHALL CUSTOMER BE LIABLE TO VENDOR FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT. VENDOR'S EXCLUSION OF CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES SHALL NOT APPLY TO: (A) VENDOR'S BREACH OF ITS DATA PROTECTION OR SECURITY OBLIGATIONS; (B) VENDOR'S BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY; (C) VENDOR'S INFRINGEMENT OF CUSTOMER'S INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS; OR (D) VENDOR'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

### Minimum Acceptable
> NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES ARISING UNDER THIS AGREEMENT, EXCEPT FOR DAMAGES ARISING FROM FRAUD OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

### Notes
The consequential damages exclusion is one of the most negotiated provisions in commercial contracts. Mutual exclusion with carve-outs for data breach, confidentiality, and IP infringement is the market standard for technology agreements. The Aggressive version makes the carve-outs asymmetric — appropriate when the vendor holds most of the risk (data processor, IP provider). Always ensure the carve-outs align with the Excluded Claims definition.

## Vendor IP Indemnification

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claim, suit, or proceeding alleging that the Services, as provided by Vendor and used by Customer in accordance with this Agreement, infringe or misappropriate any third party's intellectual property rights ("IP Claim"). Vendor's obligations under this section are conditioned on Customer: (a) providing prompt written notice of the IP Claim; (b) granting Vendor sole control of the defense and settlement; and (c) providing reasonable cooperation at Vendor's expense.

### Aggressive Language
> Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer, its Affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns (collectively, "Customer Indemnitees") from and against any and all third-party claims, actions, suits, proceedings, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs) arising from or relating to any allegation that the Services, Vendor's technology, any deliverables, or any materials provided by Vendor infringe, misappropriate, or otherwise violate any third party's intellectual property rights, proprietary rights, or other rights anywhere in the world. This indemnification obligation applies regardless of whether Customer's use conforms to the documentation, provided Customer's use does not involve unauthorized modification of Vendor's technology. Vendor's obligations are conditioned on Customer: (a) providing notice of the claim within thirty (30) days (provided that late notice shall only reduce Vendor's obligations to the extent Vendor is materially prejudiced); (b) granting Vendor control of the defense (with Customer's right to participate with its own counsel at its own expense); and (c) not agreeing to any settlement that imposes liability or obligations on Vendor without Vendor's consent.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall indemnify Customer against third-party claims alleging that the Services infringe a third party's intellectual property rights, subject to Customer providing prompt notice and reasonable cooperation.

### Notes
The Aggressive version broadens the scope beyond the Services to cover all Vendor-provided materials and removes the requirement that Customer's use strictly conform to documentation. It also includes a "late notice" grace provision. The Standard version is the most common market position. Minimum Acceptable lacks defense obligation details and cure path provisions — consider adding a cure path clause separately.

## IP Indemnification — Cure Path

### Standard Language
> If the Services become, or in Vendor's reasonable opinion are likely to become, the subject of an IP Claim, Vendor shall, at its option and expense: (a) procure for Customer the right to continue using the Services; (b) modify the Services so they are non-infringing without materially reducing their functionality; (c) replace the Services with a functionally equivalent non-infringing alternative; or (d) if options (a) through (c) are not commercially practicable, terminate the affected Services and refund to Customer any prepaid fees for the unused portion of the then-current Term.

### Aggressive Language
> If the Services become, or in Vendor's reasonable opinion are likely to become, the subject of an IP Claim, Vendor shall, at its option and expense: (a) procure for Customer the right to continue using the Services on terms no less favorable than this Agreement; (b) modify the Services so they are non-infringing without materially diminishing their functionality, performance, or security; or (c) replace the Services with a functionally equivalent, non-infringing alternative acceptable to Customer. If none of the foregoing options are commercially practicable within sixty (60) days, Customer may terminate the affected Services and Vendor shall refund all prepaid fees for the unused portion of the then-current Term, plus all fees paid during the twelve (12) months preceding the IP Claim.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If the Services are found to infringe, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to modify or replace the Services, or procure the right to continue use. If Vendor is unable to do so, Customer may terminate the affected Services and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees.

### Notes
The cure path should always culminate in a termination-with-refund option as a backstop. The Aggressive version adds a Customer acceptance right for replacement services and extends the refund beyond just prepaid fees. Ensure the cure path does not allow Vendor to materially degrade the Services under the guise of making them non-infringing.

## Customer Indemnification

### Standard Language
> Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from and against any third-party claim arising from: (a) Customer Data or content provided by Customer that infringes or misappropriates a third party's intellectual property rights; (b) Customer's use of the Services in violation of this Agreement or applicable law; or (c) Customer's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

### Aggressive Language
> Customer shall indemnify Vendor against third-party claims directly arising from Customer Data that infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, provided that Vendor provides prompt notice and Customer controls the defense. Customer's indemnification obligation shall be subject to the general limitation of liability cap set forth in this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Customer shall indemnify Vendor against third-party claims arising from Customer-provided content that infringes third-party intellectual property rights.

### Notes
Customer's indemnification should be narrowly scoped — limited to third-party IP claims arising from customer-provided content/data and customer's misuse of the service. Avoid broad customer indemnification that covers any claim "arising from Customer's use of the Services" as this could capture vendor-caused issues. The Aggressive version (from customer's perspective) caps the indemnification and narrows scope.
