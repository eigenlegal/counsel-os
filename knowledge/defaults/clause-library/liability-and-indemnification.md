# Liability & Indemnification — Clause Library

## Limitation of Liability — Cap

### Standard
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS (DEFINED BELOW), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS (DEFINED BELOW), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY SHALL EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE TOTAL AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER UNDER THE APPLICABLE ORDER FORM DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD PRECEDING THE FIRST EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM, OR (B) [DOLLAR AMOUNT]. THIS LIMITATION APPLIES REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES AND REGARDLESS OF WHETHER ANY REMEDY FAILS OF ITS ESSENTIAL PURPOSE.

### Vendor-Favorable
> IN NO EVENT SHALL VENDOR'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS ACTUALLY PAID BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR DURING THE THREE (3) MONTH PERIOD PRECEDING THE CLAIM. CUSTOMER'S LIABILITY SHALL NOT BE LIMITED UNDER THIS SECTION WITH RESPECT TO CUSTOMER'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS, BREACH OF LICENSE RESTRICTIONS, OR INFRINGEMENT OF VENDOR'S INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS.

### Minimum Acceptable
> NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID BY CUSTOMER DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

### Notes
The cap should be based on "paid or payable" rather than just "paid" to ensure recovery early in the contract. Vendor-Favorable language with a 3-month lookback dramatically reduces the cap; push for 12 months at minimum. Asymmetric caps (low cap for vendor, no cap for customer payment obligations) should be resisted. A dollar-amount floor (Aggressive version) protects against low-value contracts with high-risk exposure.

## Excluded Claims / Super Cap

### Standard
> "Excluded Claims" means: (a) indemnification obligations under Section [X]; (b) breach of confidentiality obligations; (c) Vendor's breach of data protection obligations; (d) infringement or misappropriation of intellectual property; and (e) gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud. Excluded Claims shall be subject to a separate aggregate cap equal to two (2) times the general liability cap.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> The following obligations shall not be subject to the limitation of liability and shall be uncapped: (a) Vendor's IP indemnification obligations; (b) Vendor's breach of data protection and security obligations, including any Personal Data Breach; (c) Vendor's breach of confidentiality; (d) either party's liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence; (e) liability for death or bodily injury caused by negligence; and (f) Customer's obligation to pay fees.

### Vendor-Favorable
> The limitation of liability shall not apply to: (a) either party's liability for fraud or intentional misconduct; (b) either party's liability for death or bodily injury caused by gross negligence; and (c) Customer's obligation to pay all fees due under this Agreement. All other claims, including claims for breach of data protection, confidentiality, or indemnification obligations, shall be subject to the general liability cap.

### Minimum Acceptable
> The limitation of liability shall not apply to: (a) either party's liability for fraud or willful misconduct; (b) either party's liability for death or bodily injury; or (c) Customer's obligation to pay undisputed fees.

### Notes
A super-cap (2x or 3x) for excluded claims is a common middle ground between capped and uncapped liability. Vendor-Favorable language subjecting data breach and IP indemnification to the general cap provides minimal protection for Customer's most significant risk areas. Push for at least a super-cap on data breach and IP claims. At minimum, fraud, willful misconduct, and bodily injury should always be carved out.

## Consequential Damages Exclusion

### Standard
> EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUDED CLAIMS, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR ANY LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, OR GOODWILL, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY AND EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> IN NO EVENT SHALL CUSTOMER BE LIABLE TO VENDOR FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES. VENDOR'S EXCLUSION OF CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES SHALL NOT APPLY TO: (A) VENDOR'S BREACH OF DATA PROTECTION OR SECURITY OBLIGATIONS; (B) VENDOR'S BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY; (C) VENDOR'S INFRINGEMENT OF CUSTOMER'S INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY; OR (D) VENDOR'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

### Vendor-Favorable
> IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING ANY LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, GOODWILL, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY, AND REGARDLESS OF WHETHER ANY REMEDY FAILS OF ITS ESSENTIAL PURPOSE. THIS EXCLUSION SHALL APPLY TO ALL CLAIMS INCLUDING INDEMNIFICATION, DATA BREACH, AND CONFIDENTIALITY CLAIMS.

### Minimum Acceptable
> NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES ARISING UNDER THIS AGREEMENT, EXCEPT FOR DAMAGES ARISING FROM FRAUD OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

### Notes
The consequential damages exclusion is one of the most negotiated provisions. Vendor-Favorable language applying the exclusion to all claims with no carve-outs eliminates recovery for the most likely damages in a data breach or IP infringement scenario. Push for carve-outs aligned with the Excluded Claims. The Aggressive version makes carve-outs asymmetric, which is appropriate when the vendor holds most of the risk.

## Vendor IP Indemnification

### Standard
> Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer from any third-party claim alleging that the Services, as provided by Vendor and used in accordance with this Agreement, infringe or misappropriate any third party's intellectual property rights ("IP Claim"). Vendor's obligations are conditioned on Customer: (a) providing prompt written notice; (b) granting Vendor sole control of the defense and settlement; and (c) providing reasonable cooperation at Vendor's expense.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer, its Affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns from any claim that the Services, Vendor's technology, deliverables, or materials provided by Vendor infringe or violate any third party's intellectual property rights worldwide. This obligation applies regardless of whether Customer's use conforms to documentation, provided Customer's use does not involve unauthorized modification. Vendor's obligations are conditioned on: (a) notice within thirty (30) days (late notice only reduces Vendor's obligations to the extent of material prejudice); (b) Vendor controls the defense (Customer may participate with its own counsel); and (c) no settlement imposing liability on Vendor without Vendor's consent.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor shall defend and indemnify Customer against third-party claims that the Services, as delivered by Vendor and used by Customer strictly in accordance with the documentation, infringe a valid United States patent or registered copyright. Vendor shall have no obligation for claims arising from: (a) modifications by Customer; (b) combination with non-Vendor products; (c) use not in accordance with documentation; (d) open-source components; (e) any version other than the most current release; or (f) Customer's specifications or instructions. This section states Vendor's sole obligation and Customer's exclusive remedy for IP infringement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall indemnify Customer against third-party claims alleging that the Services infringe a third party's intellectual property rights, subject to prompt notice and reasonable cooperation.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language narrows the indemnity to US patents and registered copyrights, excludes a long list of scenarios, limits to current-version use only, and makes it the exclusive remedy. Push to broaden coverage to all IP rights (including trade secrets), eliminate the current-version limitation, and ensure the indemnity is not the exclusive remedy.

## IP Indemnification — Cure Path

### Standard
> If the Services become or are likely to become the subject of an IP Claim, Vendor shall at its option and expense: (a) procure for Customer the right to continue using the Services; (b) modify the Services to be non-infringing without materially reducing functionality; (c) replace with a functionally equivalent non-infringing alternative; or (d) if (a)-(c) are not commercially practicable, terminate the affected Services and refund prepaid fees for the unused portion of the Term.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> If the Services become or are likely to become the subject of an IP Claim, Vendor shall: (a) procure the right to continue use on terms no less favorable; (b) modify the Services to be non-infringing without diminishing functionality, performance, or security; or (c) replace with a non-infringing alternative acceptable to Customer. If none are commercially practicable within sixty (60) days, Customer may terminate and receive all prepaid fees plus all fees paid during the preceding twelve (12) months.

### Vendor-Favorable
> If the Services are found to infringe by a final, non-appealable court order, Vendor may, at its sole option: (a) obtain a license for continued use; (b) modify or replace the Services to make them non-infringing; or (c) terminate the affected Services and refund prepaid, unused fees on a pro-rata basis. This section states Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for any intellectual property infringement claim.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If the Services are found to infringe, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to modify or replace the Services, or procure the right to continue use. If unable, Customer may terminate and receive a pro-rata refund.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language requiring a "final, non-appealable court order" before triggering the cure path means Customer could face years of uncertainty. Push for a trigger based on Vendor's reasonable opinion or any claim, not just a final judgment. Ensure the cure path does not allow Vendor to materially degrade the Services under the guise of making them non-infringing.

## Customer Indemnification

### Standard
> Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from third-party claims arising from: (a) Customer Data that infringes a third party's intellectual property; (b) Customer's use of the Services in violation of this Agreement or applicable law; or (c) Customer's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Customer shall indemnify Vendor against third-party claims directly arising from Customer Data that infringes a third party's intellectual property, provided Vendor provides prompt notice and Customer controls the defense. Customer's indemnification shall be subject to the general limitation of liability cap.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor and its affiliates from any and all third-party claims arising from or related to: (a) Customer's use of the Services; (b) Customer Data; (c) Customer's products or services; (d) Customer's breach of this Agreement; (e) Customer's violation of applicable law; or (f) Customer's negligence or misconduct. Customer's indemnification obligations shall not be subject to the limitation of liability.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Customer shall indemnify Vendor against third-party claims arising from Customer-provided content that infringes third-party intellectual property rights.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with broad indemnification covering "any claim arising from Customer's use of the Services" is overreaching and could capture vendor-caused issues. Push for narrow scope limited to customer-provided content and willful misuse. Reject uncapped customer indemnification and overly broad triggers.
