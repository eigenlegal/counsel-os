# Termination & Renewal — Clause Library

## Term and Auto-Renewal

### Standard Language
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of [one (1) year / as specified in the Order Form] (the "Initial Term"). Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive renewal terms of the same duration (each, a "Renewal Term"), unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. The Initial Term and all Renewal Terms are collectively referred to as the "Term."

### Aggressive Language
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the initial term specified in the applicable Order Form (the "Initial Term"). Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year renewal terms (each, a "Renewal Term"), unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. Vendor shall provide Customer with written notice of upcoming auto-renewal and any associated fee changes at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. If Vendor fails to provide such notice, the renewal shall be at the then-current pricing with no increase.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall continue for the term specified in the Order Form and shall automatically renew for successive one-year periods unless either party provides thirty (30) days' written notice of non-renewal.

### Notes
The Aggressive version requires Vendor to proactively notify Customer of upcoming renewals and price changes, preventing "surprise" renewals at increased rates. Renewal terms should generally convert to 1-year periods even if the initial term was multi-year, to avoid being locked into long renewal terms. The Minimum Acceptable has a short notice window (30 days) which may be insufficient for budget planning and vendor transition.

## Termination for Cause

### Standard Language
> Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they come due; (b) files for or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) has a receiver or trustee appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

### Aggressive Language
> Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if: (a) the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail (provided that payment breaches must be cured within fifteen (15) days); (b) the other party breaches any data protection, confidentiality, or security obligation under this Agreement, which breach shall be deemed incurable if it involves unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information or Personal Data Breach affecting more than [threshold] records; (c) the other party becomes the subject of any insolvency, bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding; or (d) the other party undergoes a change of control and the acquiring entity is a direct competitor of the terminating party, in which case the terminating party may terminate upon sixty (60) days' notice following the change of control. Termination under this section shall not limit either party's rights to seek damages or other remedies.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice. Either party may terminate immediately upon the other party's insolvency or bankruptcy.

### Notes
The Aggressive version adds shorter cure periods for payment breaches, incurable breach provisions for serious data/confidentiality violations, and change-of-control termination rights. The Standard version covers the essential bases. Ensure that termination for cause does not waive the right to seek damages — add an explicit preservation of rights clause.

## Termination for Convenience

### Standard Language
> Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the other party. If Customer terminates for convenience, Customer shall pay all fees for Services rendered through the effective date of termination. Vendor shall refund any prepaid fees for Services not yet rendered as of the effective date of termination.

### Aggressive Language
> Customer may terminate this Agreement (or any Order Form hereunder) for convenience at any time upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice. Upon termination for convenience, Vendor shall refund all prepaid fees for the unused portion of the then-current Term on a pro-rata basis. Customer shall not be liable for any early termination fee, penalty, or minimum commitment shortfall. Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice, and in such event, Vendor shall refund all prepaid fees for the unused portion of the then-current Term and provide transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days at no additional charge.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Either party may terminate this Agreement upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice. Upon termination, Vendor shall refund any prepaid fees for Services not yet provided.

### Notes
Termination for convenience is not available in all agreements — many committed-term deals intentionally omit it. The Aggressive version favors Customer with shorter notice, no penalties, and Vendor transition obligations. If termination for convenience is unavailable, ensure robust termination for cause provisions. Early termination fees (if applicable) should be clearly defined and reasonable.

## Post-Termination Obligations

### Standard Language
> Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (a) all rights and licenses granted hereunder shall immediately cease; (b) each party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other party and certify such return or destruction in writing within thirty (30) days; (c) Vendor shall make Customer Data available for download in a standard, machine-readable format for thirty (30) days following the effective date of termination, after which Vendor shall delete all Customer Data from its systems within thirty (30) days and certify such deletion; (d) all accrued payment obligations shall survive and become immediately due; and (e) the following sections shall survive termination: [list specific sections — typically Confidentiality, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, IP Ownership, Dispute Resolution, and this survival clause].

### Aggressive Language
> Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason: (a) all rights and licenses granted to Customer shall continue for a transition period of ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination ("Transition Period") to enable orderly migration; (b) during the Transition Period, Vendor shall provide transition assistance and cooperation as reasonably requested by Customer, at Vendor's then-current professional services rates (or at no additional charge if termination is due to Vendor's breach or Vendor's termination for convenience); (c) Vendor shall make all Customer Data available for export in a standard, industry-recognized, machine-readable format (including via API access) throughout the Transition Period; (d) upon expiration of the Transition Period, Vendor shall permanently and irrecoverably delete all Customer Data from its systems (including backups) within thirty (30) days and provide a written certification of deletion signed by an authorized officer; (e) each party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other party within thirty (30) days and certify such action; and (f) the following provisions shall survive: [specific section references].

### Minimum Acceptable
> Upon termination, Vendor shall return Customer Data in a commercially reasonable format within thirty (30) days. Each party shall return the other party's Confidential Information. Sections [X, Y, Z] shall survive termination.

### Notes
The Aggressive version includes a transition period with continued service access, which is critical for business continuity. Data portability (format and method of export) should be specified — avoid generic "commercially reasonable format" language where possible. Survival clauses should list specific sections rather than using generic language. Ensure deletion includes backups and any derivative datasets.

## Data Return and Deletion

### Standard Language
> Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon Customer's written request, Vendor shall: (a) make Customer Data available for export in a standard, machine-readable format for a period of thirty (30) days; and (b) thereafter delete all Customer Data from Vendor's systems, including all copies, backups, and archives, within thirty (30) additional days. Vendor shall provide written certification of deletion upon Customer's request. Vendor may retain Customer Data only to the extent required by applicable law, and any retained data shall remain subject to the confidentiality and data protection obligations of this Agreement.

### Aggressive Language
> Upon termination, expiration, or Customer's written request at any time during the Term: (a) Vendor shall immediately make all Customer Data available for export via API and bulk download in at least two (2) industry-standard, machine-readable, non-proprietary formats (e.g., CSV, JSON, XML) for a period of sixty (60) days; (b) Vendor shall provide reasonable technical assistance to support Customer's data migration at no additional charge; (c) upon expiration of the export period (or earlier upon Customer's written confirmation that export is complete), Vendor shall permanently and irrecoverably delete all Customer Data from all systems, servers, backups, archives, disaster recovery sites, and any other storage media within fifteen (15) days; (d) Vendor shall provide a written certification of deletion signed by an authorized officer, specifying the systems from which data was deleted and the method of deletion used; and (e) Vendor shall not retain any copies of Customer Data except as required by applicable law, in which case Vendor shall (i) notify Customer of the legal requirement, (ii) limit retention to the minimum required, and (iii) continue to protect such data under this Agreement until deleted.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Upon termination, Vendor shall make Customer Data available for retrieval for thirty (30) days and shall delete Customer Data from its systems thereafter.

### Notes
Data portability is becoming increasingly important and is a regulatory requirement in many jurisdictions (e.g., GDPR Article 20). Non-proprietary formats ensure Customer can actually use the exported data. The Aggressive version provides the strongest protection with multiple formats, migration assistance, and detailed deletion certification. Ensure the deletion obligation extends to backups and disaster recovery copies, which are often overlooked.
