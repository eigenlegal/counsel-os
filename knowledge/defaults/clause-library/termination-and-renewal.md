# Termination & Renewal — Clause Library

## Term and Auto-Renewal

### Standard
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of [one (1) year / as specified in the Order Form] (the "Initial Term"). Upon expiration, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive renewal terms of the same duration, unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current Term.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the initial term specified in the Order Form. Upon expiration, it shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year renewal terms, unless either party provides notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. Vendor shall provide written notice of upcoming renewal and any fee changes at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior. If Vendor fails to provide such notice, the renewal shall be at then-current pricing with no increase.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the initial term specified in the Order Form. Upon expiration, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive terms equal to the Initial Term, unless Customer provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. Fees for each renewal term shall increase by the greater of (a) the percentage specified in the Order Form, or (b) the annual change in the Consumer Price Index. If Customer fails to provide timely non-renewal notice, Customer shall be obligated to pay fees for the full renewal term.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall continue for the term specified in the Order Form and shall automatically renew for successive one-year periods unless either party provides thirty (30) days' written notice of non-renewal.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with 120-day non-renewal notice, multi-year renewal terms matching the initial term, and automatic price escalation is designed to lock customers in. Push for shorter notice periods (60-90 days), one-year renewals regardless of initial term length, and advance notice of fee changes. The Minimum Acceptable has a short notice window (30 days) which may be insufficient for budget planning.

## Termination for Cause

### Standard
> Either party may terminate upon written notice if the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice. Either party may terminate immediately if the other party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files for bankruptcy; (c) makes an assignment for creditors' benefit; or (d) has a receiver or trustee appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Either party may terminate if: (a) the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of notice (payment breaches must be cured within fifteen (15) days); (b) the other party breaches data protection, confidentiality, or security obligations, which shall be deemed incurable if involving unauthorized disclosure of Personal Data exceeding [threshold] records; (c) the other party becomes subject to insolvency or similar proceedings; or (d) the other party undergoes a change of control to a direct competitor, in which case the terminating party may terminate upon sixty (60) days' notice. Termination shall not limit either party's right to seek damages.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Customer: (a) fails to pay any undisputed amount within fifteen (15) days of written notice of non-payment; (b) breaches the license restrictions or acceptable use policy; or (c) becomes subject to any insolvency proceeding. Customer may terminate only if Vendor materially breaches and fails to cure within sixty (60) days of detailed written notice specifying the breach. Vendor's failure to meet service levels shall not constitute a material breach entitling Customer to terminate under this section.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Either party may terminate if the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice. Either party may terminate immediately upon the other party's insolvency or bankruptcy.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with asymmetric cure periods (15 days for customer, 60 days for vendor), immediate vendor termination for payment and acceptable use, and exclusion of SLA failures from material breach is heavily one-sided. Push for symmetric cure periods and ensure that chronic SLA failure qualifies as a material breach.

## Termination for Convenience

### Standard
> Either party may terminate for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice. If Customer terminates, Customer shall pay all fees for Services rendered through the effective date. Vendor shall refund prepaid fees for Services not yet rendered.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Customer may terminate this Agreement (or any Order Form) for convenience at any time upon thirty (30) days' notice. Upon termination, Vendor shall refund all prepaid fees for the unused Term pro-rata. Customer shall not be liable for any early termination fee or minimum commitment shortfall. Vendor may terminate for convenience upon ninety (90) days' notice and shall provide transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days at no charge.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer may terminate for convenience only upon payment of an early termination fee equal to the lesser of (a) the remaining fees due for the then-current Term, or (b) [dollar amount], plus sixty (60) days' prior written notice. Vendor may terminate for convenience upon thirty (30) days' notice, and Vendor's sole obligation shall be to refund prepaid fees for the unused portion of the Term on a pro-rata basis.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Either party may terminate upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice. Upon termination, Vendor shall refund any prepaid fees for Services not yet provided.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language imposing early termination fees equal to the remaining contract value makes termination for convenience functionally meaningless. If early termination fees are included, push for reasonable amounts that decline over the term. Asymmetric notice periods favoring vendor should be resisted. If termination for convenience is unavailable, ensure robust termination for cause provisions.

## Post-Termination Obligations

### Standard
> Upon termination or expiration: (a) all rights and licenses shall immediately cease; (b) each party shall return or destroy Confidential Information and certify within thirty (30) days; (c) Vendor shall make Customer Data available for download in a standard, machine-readable format for thirty (30) days, then delete within thirty (30) additional days and certify deletion; (d) accrued payment obligations survive and are immediately due; and (e) the following sections survive: [list sections -- Confidentiality, Liability, Indemnification, IP Ownership, Dispute Resolution, and survival clause].

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Upon termination or expiration for any reason: (a) Customer's rights continue for a ninety (90) day transition period; (b) Vendor shall provide transition assistance at Vendor's then-current rates (or at no charge if termination is due to Vendor's breach or Vendor's convenience); (c) Vendor shall make all Customer Data available for export in standard, machine-readable format (including via API) throughout the transition period; (d) upon expiration of the transition period, Vendor shall permanently delete all Customer Data (including backups) within thirty (30) days and provide signed officer certification; (e) each party shall return Confidential Information within thirty (30) days; and (f) specified provisions survive.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Upon termination or expiration: (a) all rights and licenses shall immediately terminate with no transition period; (b) Vendor shall make Customer Data available for download for fifteen (15) days following termination in Vendor's standard export format; (c) after the export period, Vendor shall delete Customer Data in accordance with its standard data retention policies; (d) all fees for the full Term (including any remaining committed period) shall become immediately due and payable; and (e) Customer shall certify destruction of all Vendor Confidential Information and materials within ten (10) days.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Upon termination, Vendor shall return Customer Data in a commercially reasonable format within thirty (30) days. Each party shall return Confidential Information. Sections [X, Y, Z] shall survive.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with no transition period, a 15-day export window, vendor-standard format, and acceleration of all remaining fees creates significant business risk. Push for at least a 30-day export window, standard formats, and reject fee acceleration clauses. The Aggressive version includes a transition period with continued access, which is critical for business continuity.

## Data Return and Deletion

### Standard
> Upon termination, expiration, or Customer's written request, Vendor shall: (a) make Customer Data available for export in a standard, machine-readable format for thirty (30) days; and (b) thereafter delete all Customer Data from Vendor's systems, including copies and backups, within thirty (30) additional days. Vendor shall certify deletion upon request. Vendor may retain data only as required by applicable law, subject to continued confidentiality and data protection obligations.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Upon termination, expiration, or Customer's request at any time: (a) Vendor shall make all Customer Data available for export via API and bulk download in at least two (2) non-proprietary formats (e.g., CSV, JSON, XML) for sixty (60) days; (b) Vendor shall provide technical migration assistance at no additional charge; (c) upon expiration of the export period, Vendor shall permanently delete all Customer Data from all systems, backups, and disaster recovery sites within fifteen (15) days; (d) Vendor shall provide signed officer certification specifying systems and deletion methods; and (e) Vendor shall not retain copies except as required by law, in which case Vendor shall notify Customer, limit retention, and protect data until deleted.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Upon termination, Vendor shall make Customer Data available for download in Vendor's standard export format for a period of fifteen (15) days. Customer is responsible for completing the export within this period. After the export period, Vendor shall delete Customer Data in accordance with its standard data retention and destruction policies. Vendor may retain anonymized or aggregated data derived from Customer Data indefinitely. Vendor shall have no obligation to maintain or export Customer Data in any particular format.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Upon termination, Vendor shall make Customer Data available for retrieval for thirty (30) days and shall delete Customer Data from its systems thereafter.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with a 15-day window, vendor-standard format only, and retention of anonymized/aggregated data provides weak protection. Push for non-proprietary formats and at least a 30-day export window. The right to retain anonymized data should be scrutinized carefully -- ensure the anonymization is genuine and the derived data does not enable re-identification. Deletion must extend to backups and disaster recovery copies.
