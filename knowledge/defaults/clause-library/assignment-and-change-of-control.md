# Assignment & Change of Control — Clause Library

## Assignment Consent

### Standard
> Neither party may assign this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the other party, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may assign this Agreement without consent to an Affiliate or to a successor in interest by reason of merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, provided that the assignee agrees in writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement. Any purported assignment in violation of this Section shall be void.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor may not assign this Agreement or any of its rights or obligations hereunder, whether by operation of law or otherwise, without Customer's prior written consent, which may be withheld in Customer's sole discretion. Customer may assign this Agreement without Vendor's consent: (a) to any Affiliate; (b) to any successor by merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of Customer's assets or business to which this Agreement relates; or (c) to any entity that acquires substantially all of the business unit of Customer that uses the Services. Notwithstanding any permitted assignment, Customer shall not be released from its pre-assignment obligations unless Vendor provides written consent to such release. Any purported assignment by Vendor in violation of this Section shall be void and shall constitute a material breach.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Either party may assign this Agreement without consent to an Affiliate, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, consolidation, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of such party's assets or equity interests. Either party may also assign this Agreement to a financing source as collateral security. Customer may not otherwise assign without Vendor's prior written consent. Vendor may assign its right to receive payment under this Agreement without restriction.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, not to be unreasonably withheld. Either party may assign without consent to an Affiliate or successor by merger or acquisition, provided the assignee assumes all obligations in writing.

### Notes
The Aggressive version creates asymmetry that is appropriate when Customer is the larger party or when vendor identity is critical (e.g., the customer selected the vendor for specific expertise or security posture). The Vendor-Favorable language permitting assignment to "financing sources as collateral" and unrestricted assignment of payment rights can result in third parties acquiring contract rights without the customer's knowledge. Watch for "by operation of law" carve-outs in Vendor-Favorable language — these can allow assignment through corporate restructuring without triggering the consent requirement. The customer's ability to assign to a business unit acquirer (Aggressive, clause (c)) is important in divestitures.

## Change of Control Definition

### Standard
> "Change of Control" means: (a) the acquisition, directly or indirectly, by any person or group of persons acting in concert, of beneficial ownership of fifty percent (50%) or more of the outstanding voting securities of a party; (b) a merger, consolidation, or similar transaction in which the party's pre-transaction equity holders hold less than fifty percent (50%) of the voting power of the surviving entity immediately after the transaction; or (c) the sale, lease, or other disposition of all or substantially all of a party's assets.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> "Change of Control" means the occurrence of any of the following with respect to Vendor: (a) the acquisition, directly or indirectly, by any person, entity, or group (as defined in Section 13(d)(3) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934) of beneficial ownership of forty percent (40%) or more of the outstanding voting securities or equity interests of Vendor; (b) a merger, consolidation, share exchange, or similar transaction involving Vendor in which Vendor's pre-transaction equity holders hold less than fifty percent (50%) of the voting power of the surviving or resulting entity; (c) the sale, transfer, exclusive license, or other disposition of all or substantially all of Vendor's assets, or all or substantially all of the assets used in connection with the Services; (d) the replacement of a majority of the members of Vendor's board of directors (or equivalent governing body) over any twelve (12) month period, other than replacements approved by a majority of the incumbent directors; or (e) a transaction or series of related transactions that results in Vendor becoming a subsidiary of, or controlled by, another entity. For purposes of this definition, indirect acquisitions through parent entities, holding companies, or multi-tier structures shall be considered.

### Vendor-Favorable
> "Change of Control" means a single transaction (and not a series of transactions) resulting in a third party acquiring more than fifty percent (50%) of a party's voting shares and simultaneously replacing a majority of the party's board of directors. Internal reorganizations, mergers among affiliated entities, transfers to holding companies, private equity transactions where management continuity is maintained, and initial public offerings shall not constitute a Change of Control.

### Minimum Acceptable
> "Change of Control" means: (a) the acquisition of fifty percent (50%) or more of the voting securities of a party; (b) a merger or consolidation in which the party is not the surviving entity or its equity holders hold less than fifty percent (50%) of the survivor's voting power; or (c) the sale of all or substantially all of a party's assets.

### Notes
The Vendor-Favorable definition is intentionally narrow — requiring both a 50%+ share acquisition AND simultaneous board replacement in a single transaction excludes most real-world change of control scenarios, including creeping acquisitions, asset sales, and private equity transactions. The extensive carve-outs (internal reorgs, PE transactions, IPOs) render the provision nearly meaningless. The Aggressive definition addresses common evasion techniques: the 40% threshold captures significant minority control, the board replacement trigger catches management buyouts, and the look-through provision prevents structuring around the definition via holding companies. "Substantially all of the assets used in connection with the Services" is an important variation that captures product line sales that would not meet the "substantially all assets" threshold at the entity level.

## Competitor Termination Right

### Standard
> If a party undergoes a Change of Control and the acquiring entity is a direct competitor of the other party, the non-acquired party may terminate this Agreement upon sixty (60) days' written notice, provided such notice is delivered within ninety (90) days of the non-acquired party's receipt of notice of the Change of Control. For purposes of this Section, "direct competitor" means an entity that derives more than twenty-five percent (25%) of its annual revenue from products or services that are substantially similar to and compete with the other party's core products or services.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> If Vendor undergoes a Change of Control and the acquiring or controlling entity: (a) is a Competitor of Customer (as defined below); (b) is subject to any legal, regulatory, or compliance restriction that would impair Customer's ability to use the Services; or (c) presents a material risk to Customer's confidential information, data security, or competitive position, then Customer may, at its option: (i) terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice, delivered within one hundred twenty (120) days of Customer's actual knowledge of the Change of Control; and (ii) receive a pro-rata refund of all prepaid fees for the unused portion of the Term. Vendor shall provide Customer with written notice of any Change of Control within ten (10) business days of closing. "Competitor" means any entity identified in Exhibit [X] (updated annually by mutual agreement), or any entity that directly competes with Customer in [description of Customer's market], as reasonably determined by Customer.
>
> In addition to the termination right, upon a Change of Control to a Competitor, Vendor shall: (x) immediately implement information barriers to prevent the Competitor from accessing Customer's Confidential Information and Customer Data; and (y) provide Customer with written certification that such barriers are in place within five (5) business days.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Neither party shall have the right to terminate solely by reason of a Change of Control of the other party. The parties agree that a Change of Control does not affect the rights or obligations of either party under this Agreement, and this Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If Vendor undergoes a Change of Control to a direct competitor of Customer, Customer may terminate this Agreement upon sixty (60) days' notice, provided such notice is given within ninety (90) days of becoming aware of the Change of Control. Customer shall receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for the unused Term.

### Notes
The Vendor-Favorable approach of eliminating the competitor termination right entirely is increasingly untenable given the pace of industry consolidation. Customers have a legitimate interest in not sharing confidential data and business information with a competitor that acquires their vendor. The definition of "competitor" is the most heavily negotiated aspect — vendors prefer a narrow, exhaustive list while customers prefer a functional definition. The exhibit-based approach with annual updates (Aggressive) offers a practical compromise. The information barrier requirement in the Aggressive version addresses the immediate data security concern independent of the termination right. Always pair this clause with an obligation for the acquired party to provide prompt notice of the Change of Control — without notice, the termination window is meaningless.

## Written Assumption

### Standard
> No assignment shall be effective unless and until the assignee executes a written instrument, in form and substance reasonably acceptable to the non-assigning party, assuming all obligations of the assignor under this Agreement. The assignor shall deliver a copy of the executed assumption agreement to the non-assigning party promptly following the assignment.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> As a condition precedent to any assignment by Vendor, the assignee shall execute and deliver to Customer a written assumption agreement, in form and substance satisfactory to Customer, pursuant to which the assignee: (a) unconditionally assumes all of Vendor's obligations under this Agreement, including all accrued but unperformed obligations and all representations, warranties, and indemnification obligations; (b) represents and warrants that it has the financial capacity and technical capability to perform all obligations; (c) agrees to be bound by all terms of this Agreement as if it were the original Vendor; and (d) consents to the jurisdiction specified in this Agreement. The assumption agreement shall be delivered to Customer at least fifteen (15) days prior to the effective date of the assignment. No assignment shall be effective until Customer has received the executed assumption agreement.

### Vendor-Favorable
> In the event of a permitted assignment, the assignee shall be bound by the terms of this Agreement. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to cause the assignee to execute a written assumption agreement, but failure to deliver such agreement shall not invalidate an otherwise permitted assignment.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Any assignee shall execute a written instrument assuming all obligations of the assignor under this Agreement. The assignor shall deliver a copy of such instrument to the non-assigning party within ten (10) business days of the assignment.

### Notes
The Vendor-Favorable language making the written assumption a "commercially reasonable efforts" obligation rather than a condition precedent significantly weakens this protection — an assignment can be effective without the non-assigning party ever receiving a binding commitment from the assignee. Insist on the written assumption as a condition precedent to effectiveness, not merely an obligation to use efforts. The Aggressive version's requirement for advance delivery gives the customer time to review and, if necessary, exercise other contractual rights before the assignment closes. The representation of financial capacity and technical capability in the Aggressive version provides an additional layer of protection against assignment to an entity that cannot perform.

## Effect on Terms

### Standard
> No assignment shall release the assignor from its obligations under this Agreement unless the non-assigning party expressly consents in writing to such release. Following a permitted assignment, this Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns. All terms, conditions, rights, and obligations shall continue in full force and effect following any assignment.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> No assignment by Vendor, whether or not consented to by Customer, shall release or discharge Vendor from any of its obligations under this Agreement, including obligations accrued prior to the assignment and any obligation to indemnify Customer arising from events occurring prior to or after the assignment. Vendor and the assignee shall be jointly and severally liable for all obligations under this Agreement for a period of twenty-four (24) months following the effective date of the assignment. Following any assignment: (a) all terms, conditions, service levels, and pricing shall remain unchanged; (b) the assignee shall not modify the Services in a manner that materially diminishes their functionality, security, or performance; and (c) the assignee shall honor all unexpired warranties, credits, and commitments made by Vendor.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Upon a permitted assignment, the assignor shall be fully released from all obligations arising after the effective date of the assignment. The assignee shall assume all obligations of the assignor prospectively, but the assignor shall have no liability for the assignee's performance or failure to perform.

### Minimum Acceptable
> No assignment shall release the assignor from its obligations unless the non-assigning party provides written consent to such release. The assignee shall be bound by all terms of this Agreement. All rights and obligations shall survive and continue following any assignment.

### Notes
The Vendor-Favorable automatic release upon assignment is problematic because it eliminates the customer's recourse against the original contracting party even if the assignee defaults. The joint and several liability provision in the Aggressive version provides the strongest protection but is often heavily resisted by vendors. At minimum, insist on no release without express written consent. The Aggressive provision preventing the assignee from materially diminishing the Services addresses a common post-acquisition concern where the acquirer downgrades or sunsets the acquired product. The 24-month joint liability period should be calibrated to the agreement's remaining term and the customer's transition timeline.
