# Governing Law & Jurisdiction — Clause Library

## Choice of Law

### Standard
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods shall not apply.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement and all claims arising out of or relating to it (including tort, statutory, and equitable claims) shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [Customer's home state], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods and the Uniform Computer Information Transactions Act shall not apply.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [Vendor's home state], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Customer agrees that it shall not assert any claim based on the laws of any other jurisdiction.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [a commercially reasonable US jurisdiction], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### Notes
Delaware and New York are the most commonly accepted neutral US governing law choices. For international deals, England & Wales or Singapore are widely accepted. Exclude CISG for software/services agreements to avoid unexpected default rules. The UCITA exclusion (Aggressive version) prevents application of a vendor-favorable software licensing statute adopted in Maryland and Virginia.

## Exclusive Jurisdiction and Venue

### Standard
> Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] for any action arising out of this Agreement. Each party waives any objection to jurisdiction or venue, including any objection based on inconvenient forum.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [Customer's preferred county and state]. Each party waives any objection to such jurisdiction or venue, including any objection based on inconvenient forum, lack of personal jurisdiction, or improper venue.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [Vendor's headquarters county and state]. Vendor may, at its election, bring any action against Customer in any court of competent jurisdiction where Customer is located or has assets.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Any action arising out of this Agreement shall be brought in the state or federal courts located in [a mutually agreed jurisdiction]. Each party consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

### Notes
Exclusive jurisdiction prevents parallel proceedings in multiple forums. Vendor-Favorable language with asymmetric jurisdiction (vendor can sue anywhere, customer must sue in vendor's forum) is aggressively one-sided. Push for mutual exclusive jurisdiction in a neutral or customer-favorable location.

## Consent to Personal Jurisdiction

### Standard
> Each party irrevocably consents to the personal jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] and waives any objection to personal jurisdiction in such courts, including any defense of lack of minimum contacts or similar constitutional limitation.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Each party irrevocably and unconditionally consents to the personal jurisdiction of the courts specified in this Agreement. Each party agrees that this consent constitutes a voluntary submission to jurisdiction and waives any right to challenge jurisdiction on constitutional or other grounds. Service of process may be effected by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the notice address specified in this Agreement, and shall be deemed effective upon receipt.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer consents to personal jurisdiction in [Vendor's home state] and waives all defenses of lack of personal jurisdiction, insufficiency of process, and inconvenient forum. Service of process may be made by any method permitted by law or by email to Customer's registered contact address.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party consents to the personal jurisdiction of the courts specified in this Agreement for purposes of any action arising hereunder.

### Notes
One-sided consent to jurisdiction (only customer consents) in Vendor-Favorable language should be resisted. Service by email is generally not recognized in most US jurisdictions without specific statutory authorization. Push for mutual consent with traditional service methods.

## Jury Waiver

### Standard
> EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION, PROCEEDING, OR COUNTERCLAIM ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE TRANSACTIONS CONTEMPLATED HEREBY.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY AND KNOWINGLY WAIVES ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION, PROCEEDING, OR COUNTERCLAIM ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT. EACH PARTY CERTIFIES THAT NO REPRESENTATIVE OF THE OTHER PARTY HAS REPRESENTED THAT SUCH PARTY WOULD NOT SEEK TO ENFORCE THIS WAIVER, AND ACKNOWLEDGES THAT IT HAS BEEN INDUCED TO ENTER INTO THIS AGREEMENT BY, AMONG OTHER THINGS, THE MUTUAL WAIVERS CONTAINED IN THIS SECTION.

### Vendor-Favorable
> CUSTOMER WAIVES ANY RIGHT IT MAY HAVE TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT. CUSTOMER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THIS WAIVER IS A MATERIAL INDUCEMENT FOR VENDOR TO ENTER INTO THIS AGREEMENT.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party waives any right to a jury trial in any action arising out of this Agreement.

### Notes
Jury waivers are common in commercial contracts but may not be enforceable in all jurisdictions (e.g., California courts have questioned their enforceability). A mutual waiver is standard; one-sided waivers (Vendor-Favorable) should be resisted. The Aggressive version includes certifications and acknowledgments that strengthen enforceability under New York and Delaware law.

## Forum Selection for International Contracts

### Standard
> For disputes between parties located in different countries, this Agreement shall be governed by the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales, except that either party may enforce a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [Customer's preferred jurisdiction]. Any dispute shall be resolved by arbitration under ICC Rules, with the seat of arbitration in [Customer's preferred city]. The language of arbitration shall be English. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court having jurisdiction under the New York Convention.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [Vendor's home state], USA. Customer irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts in [Vendor's county and state], regardless of the location of Customer or where the dispute arose. Customer waives any objection to jurisdiction, venue, or inconvenient forum.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of a mutually agreed jurisdiction. Each party consents to the jurisdiction of courts in such jurisdiction for the resolution of any disputes hereunder.

### Notes
For international contracts, arbitration with ICC or LCIA rules is often preferred over litigation due to the enforceability of arbitral awards under the New York Convention (ratified by over 170 countries). Vendor-Favorable language forcing international customers into US courts may be impractical and unfair. Consider the practical enforceability of judgments across borders.
