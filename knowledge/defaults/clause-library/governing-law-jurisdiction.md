## Governing Law Jurisdiction

# Governing Law & Jurisdiction — Clause Library

## Choice of Law

### Standard
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods shall not apply.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement and all claims arising out of or relating to it (including tort, statutory, and equitable claims) shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [Customer's home state], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods and the Uniform Computer Information Transactions Act shall not apply.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [Vendor's home state], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Customer agrees that it shall not assert any claim based on the laws of any other jurisdiction.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [a commercially reasonable US jurisdiction], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### Notes
Delaware and New York are the most commonly accepted neutral US governing law choices. For international deals, England & Wales or Singapore are widely accepted. Exclude CISG for software/services agreements to avoid unexpected default rules. The UCITA exclusion (Aggressive version) prevents application of a vendor-favorable software licensing statute adopted in Maryland and Virginia.

## Exclusive Jurisdiction and Venue

### Standard
> Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] for any action arising out of this Agreement. Each party waives any objection to jurisdiction or venue, including any objection based on inconvenient forum.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [Customer's preferred county and state]. Each party waives any objection to such jurisdiction or venue, including any objection based on inconvenient forum, lack of personal jurisdiction, or improper venue.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [Vendor's headquarters county and state]. Vendor may, at its election, bring any action against Customer in any court of competent jurisdiction where Customer is located or has assets.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Any action arising out of this Agreement shall be brought in the state or federal courts located in [a mutually agreed jurisdiction]. Each party consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

### Notes
Exclusive jurisdiction prevents parallel proceedings in multiple forums. Vendor-Favorable language with asymmetric jurisdiction (vendor can sue anywhere, customer must sue in vendor's forum) is aggressively one-sided. Push for mutual exclusive jurisdiction in a neutral or customer-favorable location.

## Consent to Personal Jurisdiction

### Standard
> Each party irrevocably consents to the personal jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] and waives any objection to personal jurisdiction in such courts, including any defense of lack of minimum contacts or similar constitutional limitation.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Each party irrevocably and unconditionally consents to the personal jurisdiction of the courts specified in this Agreement. Each party agrees that this consent constitutes a voluntary submission to jurisdiction and waives any right to challenge jurisdiction on constitutional or other grounds. Service of process may be effected by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the notice address specified in this Agreement, and shall be deemed effective upon receipt.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer consents to personal jurisdiction in [Vendor's home state] and waives all defenses of lack of personal jurisdiction, insufficiency of process, and inconvenient forum. Service of process may be made by any method permitted by law or by email to Customer's registered contact address.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party consents to the personal jurisdiction of the courts specified in this Agreement for purposes of any action arising hereunder.

### Notes
One-sided consent to jurisdiction (only customer consents) in Vendor-Favorable language should be resisted. Service by email is generally not recognized in most US jurisdictions without specific statutory authorization. Push for mutual consent with traditional service methods.

## Jury Waiver

### Standard
> EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION, PROCEEDING, OR COUNTERCLAIM ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE TRANSACTIONS CONTEMPLATED HEREBY.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY AND KNOWINGLY WAIVES ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION, PROCEEDING, OR COUNTERCLAIM ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT. EACH PARTY CERTIFIES THAT NO REPRESENTATIVE OF THE OTHER PARTY HAS REPRESENTED THAT SUCH PARTY WOULD NOT SEEK TO ENFORCE THIS WAIVER, AND ACKNOWLEDGES THAT IT HAS BEEN INDUCED TO ENTER INTO THIS AGREEMENT BY, AMONG OTHER THINGS, THE MUTUAL WAIVERS CONTAINED IN THIS SECTION.

### Vendor-Favorable
> CUSTOMER WAIVES ANY RIGHT IT MAY HAVE TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT. CUSTOMER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THIS WAIVER IS A MATERIAL INDUCEMENT FOR VENDOR TO ENTER INTO THIS AGREEMENT.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party waives any right to a jury trial in any action arising out of this Agreement.

### Notes
Jury waivers are common in commercial contracts but may not be enforceable in all jurisdictions (e.g., California courts have questioned their enforceability). A mutual waiver is standard; one-sided waivers (Vendor-Favorable) should be resisted. The Aggressive version includes certifications and acknowledgments that strengthen enforceability under New York and Delaware law.

## Forum Selection for International Contracts

### Standard
> For disputes between parties located in different countries, this Agreement shall be governed by the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales, except that either party may enforce a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [Customer's preferred jurisdiction]. Any dispute shall be resolved by arbitration under ICC Rules, with the seat of arbitration in [Customer's preferred city]. The language of arbitration shall be English. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court having jurisdiction under the New York Convention.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [Vendor's home state], USA. Customer irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts in [Vendor's county and state], regardless of the location of Customer or where the dispute arose. Customer waives any objection to jurisdiction, venue, or inconvenient forum.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of a mutually agreed jurisdiction. Each party consents to the jurisdiction of courts in such jurisdiction for the resolution of any disputes hereunder.

### Notes
For international contracts, arbitration with ICC or LCIA rules is often preferred over litigation due to the enforceability of arbitral awards under the New York Convention (ratified by over 170 countries). Vendor-Favorable language forcing international customers into US courts may be impractical and unfair. Consider the practical enforceability of judgments across borders.

## Multi-Jurisdictional Supplement

### Standard
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws specified in the Choice of Law section above. Notwithstanding the foregoing, to the extent that any mandatory provision of local law in the jurisdiction where Customer operates, where data subjects reside, or where Services are delivered cannot be waived by contract, such mandatory local law shall apply to the extent required, and the remainder of this Agreement shall be interpreted to give maximum effect to the parties' intent. Without limitation, the following mandatory regimes shall apply to their respective subject matter regardless of the governing law: (a) the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) with respect to personal data of EU/EEA data subjects; (b) the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) with respect to California residents' personal information; and (c) applicable consumer protection laws that by their terms apply regardless of contractual choice of law.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws specified in the Choice of Law section, subject to the following: (a) Mandatory local laws. Where any mandatory provision of applicable local law — including data protection, consumer protection, employment, competition, or financial regulation — applies to the subject matter of this Agreement regardless of the parties' choice of law, such mandatory provision shall prevail to the extent of any conflict. Neither party may assert that the governing law clause supersedes or preempts any such mandatory provision. (b) Data protection. The GDPR (and applicable national implementing legislation), UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and any other data protection law that applies based on the location of data subjects or the nature of the data processed shall apply in full, and the data processing provisions of this Agreement shall be interpreted to comply with the most restrictive applicable data protection regime. (c) Consumer protection. Where Customer serves end consumers in jurisdictions with mandatory consumer protection statutes (including but not limited to the EU Consumer Rights Directive, Australian Consumer Law, and US state UDAP statutes), Vendor's obligations under this Agreement shall not diminish Customer's ability to comply with such laws. (d) Regulatory floor. Where multiple mandatory regimes impose overlapping requirements on the same subject matter, the most protective standard shall apply. (e) Jurisdictional disclosure. Each party shall promptly notify the other if it becomes aware that the Services, Customer Data, or the parties' performance may trigger mandatory legal requirements in a jurisdiction not previously identified.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement shall be governed exclusively by the laws specified in the Choice of Law section. Customer acknowledges that it is responsible for determining and complying with all local laws applicable to its use of the Services. Vendor's obligations under this Agreement are determined solely by the governing law, and Vendor shall not be liable for Customer's failure to comply with local laws in any jurisdiction. If local law imposes obligations on Vendor that exceed those in this Agreement, Vendor may, at its election, comply with such local law or cease providing Services in the affected jurisdiction upon thirty (30) days' notice.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws specified above, except that mandatory provisions of data protection and consumer protection laws applicable to the parties' performance shall apply to their respective subject matter regardless of the governing law.

### Notes
Multi-jurisdictional issues arise in virtually every SaaS and cloud services agreement with users or data in multiple countries. Key considerations: (1) Mandatory law carve-out — most jurisdictions have laws that apply regardless of contractual choice of law (GDPR is the most prominent example). A contract governed by Delaware law does not eliminate GDPR obligations if EU personal data is processed. (2) Hierarchy of mandatory regimes — when multiple mandatory laws apply (e.g., GDPR and CCPA to the same data set), apply the most restrictive standard as a floor. (3) Vendor-Favorable risk — language placing all local-law compliance burden on the customer (Vendor-Favorable) ignores that many mandatory laws impose direct obligations on processors and service providers, not just controllers. (4) Practical enforceability — a Delaware governing law clause is largely irrelevant if a French data subject files a complaint with the CNIL. (5) Change of law risk — consider how changes to local mandatory law during the contract term will be handled.

## International Arbitration

### Standard
> Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, including its formation, interpretation, breach, or termination, that cannot be resolved by good-faith negotiation within thirty (30) days, shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by the International Chamber of Commerce ("ICC") under its Rules of Arbitration in effect at the time of filing. The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator appointed in accordance with the ICC Rules. The seat of arbitration shall be [City, Country]. The language of the arbitration shall be English. The arbitral award shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award may be entered in any court having jurisdiction under the Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (the "New York Convention"). Each party shall bear its own costs of the arbitration, and the parties shall share equally the fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the administering institution, unless the arbitrator determines otherwise in the award.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved as follows: (a) Negotiation. The parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation between senior executives within thirty (30) days of written notice of the dispute. (b) Arbitration. If negotiation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by the ICC under its Rules of Arbitration. The arbitration shall be conducted by three (3) arbitrators: each party shall nominate one arbitrator, and the two party-nominated arbitrators shall select the presiding arbitrator. If the parties cannot agree on the presiding arbitrator within fifteen (15) days, the ICC Court shall make the appointment. The seat of arbitration shall be [Customer's preferred neutral city — e.g., London, Singapore, or New York]. The language of the arbitration shall be English. (c) Governing procedural law. The arbitration shall be governed by the arbitration law of the seat. The arbitrator(s) shall apply the substantive law specified in the Choice of Law section. (d) Interim relief. Either party may seek interim or conservatory measures from any court of competent jurisdiction without waiving the right to arbitrate. (e) Confidentiality. The arbitration proceedings, all submissions, and the award shall be confidential, except as required by law or to enforce the award. (f) Costs. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party, as determined by the tribunal. (g) Enforcement. The award shall be final and binding and enforceable under the New York Convention.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator selected by Vendor from the AAA panel. The seat of arbitration shall be [Vendor's headquarters city]. The language of the arbitration shall be English. The arbitrator shall not have the power to award punitive, exemplary, or consequential damages. The arbitration and the award shall be confidential. Each party shall bear its own costs, and the parties shall share the arbitrator's fees equally, regardless of outcome. The arbitrator's award shall be final, and judicial review shall be limited to the grounds set forth in the Federal Arbitration Act.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Disputes that cannot be resolved by negotiation within thirty (30) days shall be resolved by arbitration under ICC Rules or LCIA Rules, as mutually agreed. The arbitration shall be conducted in English by a sole arbitrator in a mutually agreed neutral seat. The award shall be final and binding and enforceable under the New York Convention.

### Notes
International arbitration is the standard dispute resolution mechanism for cross-border contracts because arbitral awards are enforceable in 170+ countries under the New York Convention, whereas foreign court judgments often are not. Key negotiation points: (1) Institutional rules — ICC, LCIA (London Court of International Arbitration), SIAC (Singapore International Arbitration Centre), and UNCITRAL (ad hoc) are the most widely used. ICC is most common for high-value commercial disputes; SIAC is preferred for Asia-Pacific deals; LCIA for European deals. UNCITRAL rules work well for state-party arbitrations or where parties want more procedural flexibility. (2) Number of arbitrators — sole arbitrator is cost-effective for smaller disputes; three-arbitrator panels are appropriate for complex or high-value matters (typically above $1-5M). (3) Seat of arbitration — determines the procedural law governing the arbitration and the courts with supervisory jurisdiction. Choose a seat with a strong pro-arbitration legal framework (London, New York, Singapore, Paris, Hong Kong). (4) Language — must be practical for both parties; English is default for international commerce. (5) Confidentiality — arbitration is private by default but not always confidential; include an express confidentiality provision. (6) Interim relief — preserve the right to seek injunctive relief from courts without waiving arbitration. (7) Costs — "loser pays" provisions incentivize meritorious claims; equal sharing regardless of outcome (Vendor-Favorable) removes this incentive. (8) Arbitrator selection — unilateral selection by one party (Vendor-Favorable) undermines the neutrality of the process.
