## Entire Agreement / Integration

### Standard
> This Agreement, together with all Order Forms, Statements of Work, and exhibits attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, proposals, negotiations, representations, and communications, whether oral or written. No terms of any purchase order, invoice, or other business form shall modify or supplement this Agreement, even if accepted without objection.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements, proposals, and communications. Notwithstanding the foregoing, nothing in this Agreement shall be deemed to limit or supersede any Data Processing Agreement, Business Associate Agreement, or security addendum executed between the parties. No pre-printed terms on any purchase order, invoice, confirmation, or similar document shall have any force or effect.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement, together with Vendor's standard terms of service (as updated from time to time at [URL]) and applicable Order Forms, constitutes the entire agreement. In the event of any conflict, the order of precedence shall be: (1) the standard terms of service, (2) this Agreement, (3) the applicable Order Form.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language incorporating by reference a URL-based terms of service that can be "updated from time to time" allows unilateral amendment. Reject any entire agreement clause that references terms the vendor can change without consent. Ensure the precedence order favors the negotiated agreement over standard terms.

## Severability

### Standard
> If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable while preserving the parties' original intent. If modification is not possible, such provision shall be severed from this Agreement, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to be enforceable while preserving the original intent, or if modification is not possible, severed without affecting the remaining provisions. However, if the invalid provision is a limitation of liability, indemnification, or data protection obligation that is material to the allocation of risk, either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' notice.

### Vendor-Favorable
> If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, it shall be severed from this Agreement and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalidity of any provision shall not affect the validity of any limitation of liability, warranty disclaimer, or indemnification provision.

### Minimum Acceptable
> If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Notes
The Aggressive version addresses the risk that a court could strike a key risk-allocation provision. Vendor-Favorable language explicitly protecting limitation of liability and warranty disclaimers from the ripple effect of severability is one-sided. Standard severability with modification-before-severance is the most balanced approach.

## Waiver

### Standard
> No failure or delay by either party in exercising any right or remedy under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof, nor shall any single or partial exercise of any right or remedy preclude any further exercise of that right or remedy or the exercise of any other right or remedy. All waivers must be in writing and signed by an authorized representative of the waiving party.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> No waiver shall be effective unless in writing and signed by an authorized representative of the waiving party. No failure to exercise, or delay in exercising, any right shall constitute a waiver of that right. A waiver on one occasion shall not be construed as a waiver on any future occasion. Vendor's acceptance of late payment or performance shall not constitute a waiver of any breach.

### Vendor-Favorable
> No failure or delay by Vendor in exercising any right shall constitute a waiver. No waiver by Customer shall be effective unless in writing. Vendor may waive any Customer obligation orally or by course of conduct.

### Minimum Acceptable
> No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing. No waiver on one occasion shall constitute a waiver on any future occasion.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language creating asymmetric waiver rules (writing required from customer, oral OK from vendor) should always be rejected. Waiver provisions should be mutual and require writing from both parties.

## Counterparts

### Standard
> This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Execution and delivery of this Agreement by electronic means (including PDF, DocuSign, or other electronic signature platforms) shall be valid and sufficient.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement may be executed in counterparts. Electronic signatures transmitted by PDF, DocuSign, or other reliable electronic signature platform shall have the same legal effect as original ink signatures and shall be binding and enforceable. Each party waives any right to challenge the validity or enforceability of an electronic signature.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and delivered electronically.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

### Notes
The counterparts clause is rarely negotiated. Ensure electronic signature validity is addressed, particularly for cross-border transactions where e-signature laws may vary. The Aggressive version includes a waiver of challenge to electronic signatures, which provides additional certainty.

## Amendment

### Standard
> This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented except by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both parties. No amendment shall be effective unless it expressly references this Agreement and states the parties' intent to amend.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both parties that expressly references this Agreement. Vendor shall not modify the terms of this Agreement, any Order Form, or the Services by posting changes to a website, sending email notification, or any other unilateral means. Any attempt by Vendor to unilaterally amend shall be void and of no effect.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor may modify the terms of this Agreement, its privacy policy, acceptable use policy, and service documentation from time to time by posting updated terms on its website or providing Customer with thirty (30) days' notice. Customer's continued use of the Services after the effective date of any modification constitutes acceptance. Customer may terminate if it does not agree to the modification.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement may not be amended except by a written instrument signed by both parties.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language allowing unilateral amendment by posting to a website is common in click-through SaaS agreements but should be rejected in negotiated enterprise deals. Push for bilateral written amendments only. The terminate-if-you-disagree right in vendor-favorable language is cold comfort when switching costs are high.

## Survival

### Standard
> The following provisions shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason: Confidentiality, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, Intellectual Property Ownership, Data Return and Deletion, Dispute Resolution, and this survival clause. Additionally, any provision that by its nature is intended to survive shall survive.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration: Confidentiality (for the stated duration), Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, Intellectual Property Ownership, Customer Data ownership and return, Data Protection (with respect to any Personal Data retained), Dispute Resolution, Representations and Warranties (for claims arising during the Term), Audit Rights (for one (1) year following termination), and this survival clause.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Sections [X] (Limitation of Liability), [Y] (Warranty Disclaimer), [Z] (Payment Obligations), and [W] (Governing Law) shall survive termination. All other provisions shall terminate upon expiration or termination of this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Provisions that by their nature should survive termination shall survive, including Confidentiality, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, and Dispute Resolution.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language limiting survival to liability caps, warranty disclaimers, and payment obligations while excluding indemnification, confidentiality, and data protection creates significant post-termination risk. Always ensure indemnification, confidentiality, data protection, and IP ownership survive. Specific section references are preferred over "by its nature" language to avoid ambiguity.

## Relationship of Parties

### Standard
> The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment, or franchise relationship between the parties. Neither party shall have the authority to bind the other party, assume or create any obligation on behalf of the other party, or make any representation or warranty on behalf of the other party. Each party shall be solely responsible for the supervision, control, compensation, withholdings, and benefits of its own personnel.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> The relationship between the parties is strictly that of independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment, franchise, fiduciary, or any other relationship between the parties beyond that of independent contractors. Neither party has the authority to: (a) bind the other to any obligation, contract, or commitment; (b) make any representation or warranty on behalf of the other; (c) incur any liability on behalf of the other; or (d) represent itself as an agent, partner, or employee of the other. Vendor shall be solely responsible for: (i) the hiring, supervision, compensation, benefits, withholding, and termination of its personnel; (ii) compliance with all employment, labor, tax, and immigration laws applicable to its personnel; and (iii) maintaining adequate insurance coverage for its personnel. No Vendor personnel shall be eligible for or entitled to any Customer employee benefit, compensation plan, or other program.

### Vendor-Favorable
> The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates any employment, partnership, or agency relationship. Customer shall not direct or control the manner or means by which Vendor performs the Services.

### Minimum Acceptable
> The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship. Neither party may bind the other to any obligation.

### Notes
The independent contractor clause is critical for tax, liability, and employment law purposes. Vendor-Favorable brevity may be insufficient to prevent misclassification claims, particularly in long-term services engagements with dedicated personnel. The Aggressive version's detailed allocation of employment responsibilities is appropriate when Vendor personnel work on-site or are deeply embedded in Customer's operations, where the risk of deemed employment or agency is highest. Always include the prohibition on binding the other party to obligations. For international agreements, consider adding jurisdiction-specific language addressing local employment classification tests.

## Third-Party Beneficiaries

### Standard
> This Agreement is for the sole benefit of the parties hereto and their respective permitted successors and assigns. Nothing in this Agreement, express or implied, is intended to or shall confer upon any third party any legal or equitable right, benefit, or remedy of any nature whatsoever under or by reason of this Agreement, except that: (a) the Indemnified Parties identified in the indemnification provisions shall be third-party beneficiaries of those provisions; and (b) Customer's Affiliates that are authorized to use the Services under this Agreement may enforce the data protection and confidentiality provisions directly.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> This Agreement is for the sole benefit of the parties and their permitted successors and assigns. No third party shall have any right, benefit, or remedy under this Agreement except: (a) the parties' respective officers, directors, employees, agents, and Affiliates who are expressly identified as Indemnified Parties shall be third-party beneficiaries of the indemnification provisions; (b) Customer's Affiliates that are users of the Services shall be third-party beneficiaries of the warranty, data protection, security, confidentiality, and SLA provisions; and (c) data subjects whose Personal Data is processed under this Agreement shall be third-party beneficiaries of the data protection provisions to the extent required by applicable Data Protection Laws (including GDPR Standard Contractual Clauses). The rights of third-party beneficiaries under this section may be enforced only by or through the applicable party (Customer or Vendor), except where direct enforcement by the third-party beneficiary is required by applicable law.

### Vendor-Favorable
> This Agreement does not and is not intended to confer any rights or remedies upon any person or entity other than the parties. No third party, including any Customer Affiliate, employee, end user, or data subject, shall have any right to enforce any term of this Agreement. Customer is solely responsible for its obligations to its Affiliates, end users, and any other third parties.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement is for the benefit of the parties and their permitted successors and assigns only. No third party shall have any rights under this Agreement, except that Indemnified Parties may enforce the indemnification provisions.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language denying all third-party beneficiary rights, including for indemnified parties and Affiliates, may undermine the indemnification framework and create issues for Customer's group companies that use the Services. At minimum, ensure indemnified parties are recognized as beneficiaries of the indemnification provisions. The Aggressive version's data subject beneficiary rights are relevant for GDPR compliance, particularly when Standard Contractual Clauses are incorporated, as SCCs require data subjects to have enforceable rights. The "enforcement through the applicable party" mechanism in the Aggressive version preserves control while honoring beneficiary rights.

## Order of Precedence

### Standard
> In the event of a conflict or inconsistency between the terms of the documents that comprise this Agreement, the following order of precedence shall apply (highest to lowest): (1) the Data Processing Agreement or Data Processing Addendum ("DPA"); (2) the Master Services Agreement ("MSA") or main body of this Agreement; (3) the applicable Statement of Work ("SOW"); and (4) the applicable Order Form. Notwithstanding the foregoing, a SOW or Order Form may expressly override a specific provision of the MSA with respect to the particular engagement or transaction covered by that SOW or Order Form, provided such override is clearly identified and signed by both parties.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> In the event of a conflict between documents, the following order of precedence shall apply: (1) any Data Processing Agreement, Business Associate Agreement, or security addendum; (2) this Master Services Agreement; (3) the applicable Statement of Work; (4) the applicable Order Form; and (5) Vendor's standard documentation. No terms in any Order Form, SOW, purchase order, invoice, click-through, or browse-wrap agreement shall modify or supersede the terms of the MSA or DPA unless such modification is set forth in a written amendment signed by authorized representatives of both parties that expressly references the provision being modified. Vendor's standard documentation, acceptable use policies, and support policies referenced in this Agreement shall not contain terms that conflict with or diminish the rights granted to Customer under this Agreement; in the event of conflict, this Agreement shall control.

### Vendor-Favorable
> In the event of conflict between the documents forming this Agreement, the following order of precedence shall apply: (1) the Order Form (including any terms referenced therein); (2) Vendor's standard Terms of Service (as updated from time to time); (3) the applicable SOW; (4) any Data Processing Addendum. Customer's purchase orders, vendor onboarding forms, or other business documents shall not modify the terms of this Agreement regardless of any conflicting or additional terms contained therein.

### Minimum Acceptable
> In the event of conflict, the DPA shall take precedence over the MSA, the MSA over any SOW, and any SOW over the applicable Order Form.

### Notes
Order of precedence is critical for determining which terms control when documents conflict -- a common occurrence in complex enterprise agreements with multiple SOWs and Order Forms. Vendor-Favorable language placing the Order Form (often a Vendor-controlled template with pre-printed terms) and a unilaterally updatable Terms of Service above the negotiated MSA undermines the entire negotiation. The DPA should always sit at the top of the precedence hierarchy because data protection obligations are regulatory requirements that cannot be diluted by commercial terms. Resist any formulation that places Vendor's standard terms above the negotiated MSA. The Aggressive version's prohibition on click-through and browse-wrap modifications is important because vendors sometimes embed additional terms in product onboarding flows that conflict with the negotiated agreement.
