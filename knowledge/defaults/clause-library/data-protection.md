# Data Protection — Clause Library

## Data Processing Obligations

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall process Customer Personal Data only in accordance with Customer's documented instructions and solely for the purpose of providing the Services under this Agreement. Vendor shall not process Customer Personal Data for any other purpose, including for Vendor's own purposes or for the benefit of any third party.

### Aggressive Language
> Vendor shall process Customer Personal Data strictly in accordance with Customer's prior written instructions. Vendor shall not process, access, use, transfer, disclose, or otherwise make available Customer Personal Data for any purpose other than as expressly authorized by this Agreement and Customer's written instructions. Any processing outside the scope of Customer's instructions shall require Customer's prior written consent. Vendor shall immediately inform Customer if, in Vendor's opinion, an instruction from Customer infringes applicable Data Protection Laws.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall process Customer Personal Data in accordance with Customer's instructions and applicable Data Protection Laws. Vendor shall not use Customer Personal Data for purposes other than providing the Services.

### Notes
Use Standard for most SaaS and services agreements. Use Aggressive for engagements involving sensitive data categories (health, financial, children's data) or high-volume processing. Minimum Acceptable may suffice for low-risk Tier 3 vendors with limited data access.

## Data Breach Notification

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall notify Customer without undue delay, and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours, after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Personal Data. Such notification shall include: (a) the nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records concerned; (b) the likely consequences of the breach; (c) the measures taken or proposed to address the breach; and (d) the identity and contact details of Vendor's data protection officer or other contact point.

### Aggressive Language
> Vendor shall notify Customer within forty-eight (48) hours after becoming aware of any Security Incident that affects or may affect Customer Personal Data, including any unauthorized access, acquisition, use, disclosure, or destruction of Customer Personal Data. Such notification shall include all information reasonably necessary for Customer to comply with its own breach notification obligations under applicable law, including but not limited to: (a) the nature and scope of the incident; (b) the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects and data records; (c) the likely consequences; (d) all measures taken and proposed to mitigate the incident; (e) a timeline of events; and (f) a designated single point of contact. Vendor shall cooperate fully with Customer's investigation and provide updates at least every twenty-four (24) hours until the incident is resolved. Vendor shall not notify any regulatory authority or affected individuals on behalf of Customer without Customer's prior written consent.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall notify Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach involving Customer Personal Data. Such notification shall include a description of the nature of the breach and the measures taken to address it.

### Notes
Standard language aligns with GDPR Article 33 requirements. Aggressive language is appropriate for critical data processors handling sensitive information at scale. Minimum Acceptable lacks a specific time frame, which creates ambiguity — use only for low-risk engagements and consider adding a "within 72 hours" qualifier.

## Sub-Processor Management

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall maintain a list of sub-processors used to process Customer Personal Data, which shall be made available to Customer upon request. Vendor shall provide Customer with at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice before engaging a new sub-processor or replacing an existing sub-processor. Customer may object to the engagement of a new sub-processor on reasonable grounds related to data protection within fifteen (15) days of receiving notice. If Customer objects, the parties shall discuss Customer's concerns in good faith. If the parties are unable to resolve Customer's objection, Customer may terminate the affected Services without penalty. Vendor shall impose data protection obligations on sub-processors that are no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement.

### Aggressive Language
> Vendor shall not engage any sub-processor to process Customer Personal Data without Customer's prior written consent. Vendor shall maintain and provide to Customer a complete list of all sub-processors, including their name, location, and the processing activities performed. Vendor shall provide Customer with at least forty-five (45) days' prior written notice before engaging a new sub-processor. Customer may object to any new sub-processor for any reason within thirty (30) days. If Customer objects, Vendor shall not engage the sub-processor for processing Customer Personal Data and shall, at Customer's election, either (a) continue to provide the Services without the new sub-processor, or (b) permit Customer to terminate the affected Services and receive a pro-rata refund. Vendor shall be fully liable for the acts and omissions of its sub-processors.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor may engage sub-processors to process Customer Personal Data, provided that Vendor (a) maintains a current list of sub-processors, (b) imposes data protection obligations on sub-processors consistent with this Agreement, and (c) remains responsible for the acts and omissions of its sub-processors.

### Notes
Standard language balances transparency with operational flexibility. Aggressive language is appropriate for engagements involving highly sensitive data (financial, health, government) where sub-processor control is critical. Minimum Acceptable omits the objection right, which may be insufficient under GDPR Article 28(2) — use only where regulatory risk is low.

## Cross-Border Data Transfers

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall not transfer Customer Personal Data outside the European Economic Area (or the jurisdiction from which it was collected, as applicable) unless (a) the transfer is to a country that has been deemed to provide an adequate level of protection by the relevant authority, or (b) appropriate safeguards are in place, including the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (as amended or replaced from time to time), which the parties agree to execute as necessary. Vendor shall promptly inform Customer of any legal requirement that prevents it from complying with this provision.

### Aggressive Language
> Vendor shall process and store all Customer Personal Data exclusively within the European Economic Area (or the geographic region specified by Customer). Vendor shall not transfer, access, or make available Customer Personal Data outside the specified geographic region without Customer's prior written consent. If a transfer is authorized, Vendor shall implement all safeguards required by applicable Data Protection Laws, including the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses with the supplementary measures identified in the transfer impact assessment conducted by Vendor. Vendor shall conduct and provide to Customer a transfer impact assessment for each cross-border transfer, evaluating the laws of the recipient country. If Vendor determines that it cannot comply with this provision due to a change in law, it shall immediately notify Customer and cease the relevant processing.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall ensure that any transfer of Customer Personal Data to a country outside the European Economic Area is conducted in compliance with applicable Data Protection Laws, including by implementing appropriate transfer mechanisms.

### Notes
Standard language covers most scenarios and aligns with GDPR Chapter V requirements. Aggressive language with data localization is appropriate for highly regulated industries or when Customer's privacy program requires geographic restrictions. Minimum Acceptable defers to applicable law without specifying mechanisms — acceptable only for Tier 3 vendors with minimal data processing.

## Audit Rights

### Standard Language
> Vendor shall make available to Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and applicable Data Protection Laws. Upon reasonable notice (not less than thirty (30) days), Customer or its designated third-party auditor may conduct an audit of Vendor's data processing activities, facilities, and records, no more than once per twelve (12) months (unless required by a regulatory authority or following a data breach). Vendor shall cooperate with such audits at no additional cost to Customer. Alternatively, Vendor may satisfy audit requirements by providing a current SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent independent third-party audit report.

### Aggressive Language
> Customer shall have the right to audit Vendor's compliance with this Agreement and applicable Data Protection Laws at any time upon fifteen (15) days' written notice. Audits may include on-site inspections of Vendor's facilities, review of records, interviews with Vendor personnel, and technical testing. Vendor shall cooperate fully with all audits at no additional cost. The frequency of audits shall not be limited if an audit reveals material non-compliance or following a security incident. Vendor shall promptly remediate any non-compliance identified during an audit at its own expense. Customer's audit rights are in addition to (and not in lieu of) any third-party audit reports provided by Vendor.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall, upon Customer's reasonable request, provide evidence of its compliance with the data protection obligations under this Agreement, which may include providing a current SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent third-party certification.

### Notes
Standard language balances Customer's oversight needs with Vendor's operational burden. Aggressive language is appropriate for Tier 1 critical vendors processing sensitive data at scale. Minimum Acceptable relies on third-party certifications only, which may be insufficient for high-risk processing but is practical for Tier 3 vendors.
