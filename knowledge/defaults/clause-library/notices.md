# Notices — Clause Library

## General Notice Provision

### Standard
> All notices, requests, consents, and other communications under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when: (a) delivered personally; (b) sent by nationally recognized overnight courier (delivery confirmed); (c) sent by certified or registered mail, return receipt requested, postage prepaid; or (d) sent by email to the address specified below, provided that the sender receives electronic confirmation of delivery (not merely that the message was sent). Notices to each party shall be sent to the address set forth in the signature block or such other address as a party may designate by notice in accordance with this section.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) personal delivery; (b) nationally recognized overnight courier; or (c) certified mail, return receipt requested. Notices shall be deemed given upon receipt. Notices sent by email shall be effective only if followed by delivery via one of the foregoing methods within two (2) business days. Routine operational communications (support tickets, service alerts) may be sent by email but shall not constitute formal notice under this Agreement. Notices to Customer shall be sent to the General Counsel at the address specified below.

### Vendor-Favorable
> All notices shall be in writing and may be given by: (a) personal delivery; (b) overnight courier; (c) first-class mail; (d) email; or (e) in-app notification through the Services. Notices shall be deemed given: upon delivery for personal delivery and courier; three (3) days after mailing for first-class mail; upon transmission for email; and upon posting for in-app notifications. Customer is responsible for monitoring its email and the Services for notices. Vendor may send notices to the email address associated with Customer's account.

### Minimum Acceptable
> All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses specified by the parties. Notices shall be deemed given upon receipt by the recipient.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language allowing notice by in-app notification and email with deemed receipt upon transmission (not confirmed receipt) creates a risk that Customer misses critical notices such as auto-renewal, price increase, or termination. Push for methods that confirm receipt. Restrict email notice to operational communications, not contractual notices like termination or material changes.

## Deemed Receipt

### Standard
> Notices shall be deemed received: (a) on the date of personal delivery; (b) one (1) business day after deposit with an overnight courier; (c) three (3) business days after deposit in the mail via certified or registered mail; and (d) on the date of confirmed electronic delivery for email. If notice is received after 5:00 PM local time of the recipient or on a non-business day, it shall be deemed received on the next business day.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Notices shall be deemed received: (a) on the date of personal delivery; (b) one (1) business day after deposit with an overnight courier; and (c) five (5) business days after deposit via certified mail. Email shall not constitute effective notice for purposes of termination, non-renewal, material amendment, or claims-related communications. Deemed receipt shall not apply if the sending party has actual knowledge that the notice was not received.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Notices shall be deemed received: (a) on the date of personal delivery; (b) one (1) business day after sending by overnight courier; (c) three (3) days after mailing; and (d) upon transmission for email. Vendor shall have no obligation to confirm receipt of any notice. Customer shall be responsible for maintaining current contact information with Vendor.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Notices shall be deemed received upon actual receipt by the recipient or its authorized representative.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable deemed receipt "upon transmission" for email means a notice is effective when sent, regardless of whether Customer actually received it. Push for confirmed delivery or actual receipt. The Aggressive version restricts email for high-stakes notices (termination, non-renewal, amendments) where deemed receipt could cause material harm.

## Electronic Delivery

### Standard
> The parties agree that notices may be delivered by email to the email addresses specified below, provided that (a) the sender receives confirmation of delivery (such as a read receipt or delivery confirmation), and (b) email shall not be used for notices of termination, material breach, or indemnification claims unless the email is followed by delivery via overnight courier within two (2) business days.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Email shall be an acceptable method for routine operational communications (e.g., support, service updates, invoice delivery). Formal contractual notices, including notices of termination, non-renewal, material breach, indemnification claims, and material amendment, shall require delivery by overnight courier or certified mail and shall not be effective if delivered solely by email, in-app notification, or other electronic means.

### Vendor-Favorable
> The parties agree that all notices may be delivered by email, and email delivery shall be sufficient for all purposes under this Agreement, including notices of termination, non-renewal, and material changes. Vendor may also provide notices through its customer portal, in-app notifications, or by posting to Vendor's website.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Notices may be delivered by email provided the sender receives confirmation of delivery. Material notices (termination, breach) should also be sent by an additional method that confirms receipt.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable blanket acceptance of email for all notices including termination is risky. An email that lands in spam could result in a missed termination notice or auto-renewal. Push for a two-method approach for material notices: email plus a confirmed-delivery method (overnight courier or certified mail).

## Address Changes

### Standard
> Either party may change its notice address by providing at least ten (10) days' prior written notice to the other party in accordance with this section. Until the other party receives such notice of change, notices sent to the prior address shall be deemed effective. Each party shall maintain at least two (2) designated contacts for receiving notices (a primary and a backup).

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Either party may update its notice address by providing at least fifteen (15) days' prior written notice. The updating party shall confirm the change by sending notice to the other party's current address via overnight courier. Until the change is effective, notices to the prior address remain valid. Each party shall designate a primary contact and a legal department contact for receiving notices. Vendor shall maintain Customer's notice information in a secure, dedicated system and shall not use the notice address for marketing.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Either party may change its notice address by providing written notice or by updating its account information in Vendor's customer portal. Changes shall be effective upon receipt of notice or upon update in the portal. Customer is responsible for keeping its contact information current in Vendor's systems.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Either party may change its notice address by written notice to the other party. Changes are effective upon receipt.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language allowing address changes through an online portal without formal notice creates a risk that one party does not learn of the change. Push for formal written notice of address changes with a reasonable lead time. Dual contacts (legal and business) reduce the risk of missed communications.

## Notices for Legal Process

### Standard
> Notices of legal process, including service of summons, subpoenas, and regulatory demands, shall be delivered to the legal department of each party at the address specified below, with a copy by email to the designated legal contact. Such notices shall not be deemed effective if served only upon a registered agent or operational contact unless also delivered to the legal department.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> All legal process, including service of lawsuits, subpoenas, regulatory demands, and preservation notices, shall be served upon Customer's General Counsel at the address below. Service upon any other individual, agent, or address shall not be effective as against Customer. Vendor shall promptly notify Customer's General Counsel of any legal process received by Vendor that relates to Customer or Customer Data within forty-eight (48) hours of receipt, unless prohibited by law.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Legal process may be served upon either party at its registered agent address or principal place of business. Each party shall accept service of process by any method permitted by applicable law, including service by email.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Legal process shall be delivered to each party's legal department at the address specified below.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable acceptance of email service of process may not be valid in many jurisdictions. Ensure that the contract specifies a clear path for legal process that is consistent with applicable rules of civil procedure. The Aggressive version requiring notification of legal process affecting Customer Data is critical for data privacy and subpoena response.

## Escalation Notice

### Standard
> Notices related to dispute escalation and security incidents shall be subject to the following special requirements in addition to the general notice provisions:
>
> **Dispute Escalation.** Written notice of a dispute requiring escalation to senior management shall be delivered to the designated escalation contact for each party (as identified in the Order Form or a separately exchanged contact list) within five (5) business days of a party's determination that the dispute cannot be resolved through normal operational channels. The escalation notice shall describe the dispute, the resolution sought, and the proposed timeline for senior management engagement.
>
> **Security Incidents.** Notice of a confirmed or reasonably suspected security incident involving Customer Data shall be delivered to Customer's designated security contact by email and telephone within the timeframe specified in the Data Processing Agreement (or, if none, within twenty-four (24) hours of Vendor's becoming aware of the incident). Security incident notices shall not be subject to the deemed receipt provisions for general notices — they are effective only upon confirmed receipt by the designated security contact.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> The following categories of notice have separate timing, delivery, and recipient requirements that supersede the general notice provisions:
>
> **Dispute Escalation.** Either party may escalate a dispute by delivering written notice to the other party's designated escalation contact (as identified in Exhibit [X] or a separately exchanged contact list, which each party shall keep current). Escalation notice shall be delivered by email and overnight courier simultaneously. The notice shall include: (a) a description of the dispute; (b) a summary of prior resolution attempts; (c) the specific relief sought; and (d) the name and title of the senior executive who will represent the escalating party. The receiving party shall acknowledge receipt within one (1) business day and designate its senior executive representative within three (3) business days.
>
> **Security Incidents.** Notice of any confirmed or reasonably suspected security incident, data breach, or unauthorized access involving Customer Data shall be delivered to Customer's Chief Information Security Officer (or designated security contact) and General Counsel simultaneously by: (i) telephone to a 24/7 security hotline number designated by Customer; and (ii) email to the designated security contact addresses. Security incident notice shall be provided within the timeframe required by the Data Processing Agreement or applicable law, but in no event later than twenty-four (24) hours after Vendor becomes aware of the incident. The notice shall include all information then available regarding the nature, scope, and potential impact of the incident. Deemed receipt provisions shall not apply — security incident notices are effective only upon confirmed actual receipt. Vendor shall provide follow-up notices every four (4) hours until the incident is contained, and a comprehensive written incident report within seventy-two (72) hours.
>
> **Material Service Changes.** Notice of material changes to the Services (including deprecation, discontinuation, or material modification of features Customer uses) shall be delivered to Customer's designated business contact and legal contact at least ninety (90) days in advance by email and overnight courier.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Dispute escalation notices and security incident notifications shall be delivered in accordance with the general notice provisions of this Agreement. Vendor may provide security incident notifications by email to the account administrator's registered email address. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide security incident notification within a reasonable time after becoming aware of an incident, but delayed notification shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Dispute escalation notices shall be sent to designated escalation contacts identified by each party. Security incident notices shall be delivered to Customer's designated security contact within twenty-four (24) hours by email and telephone, and shall be effective upon confirmed receipt.

### Notes
Escalation and security incident notices serve fundamentally different purposes than routine contractual notices and should have distinct requirements. Key issues: (1) Security incident timing — twenty-four hours is a practical standard; GDPR requires supervisory authority notification within 72 hours, and the customer needs time to assess and report. Vendor-Favorable language with "commercially reasonable efforts" and no specific deadline is unacceptable. (2) Delivery confirmation — deemed receipt provisions (e.g., "deemed received 3 days after mailing") are inappropriate for time-sensitive security notifications. Push for confirmed actual receipt via telephone plus email. (3) Recipient — security incident notices should go to CISO/security contacts and legal, not the general business contact or account administrator. (4) Follow-up cadence — initial notice is just the beginning; require regular updates until containment and a comprehensive post-incident report. (5) Dispute escalation — clear escalation procedures with designated contacts and timelines prevent disputes from festering. (6) Material service changes — significant changes to the service should have their own notice requirements with longer lead times than routine notices.

## Multi-Party Notice

### Standard
> Where this Agreement or any Order Form involves Customer's Affiliates as authorized users or beneficiaries of the Services, notices under this Agreement shall be delivered to Customer (the contracting entity) at the address specified in the signature block. Customer shall be responsible for distributing notices to its Affiliates as appropriate. Vendor shall have no obligation to deliver notices directly to Customer's Affiliates unless a separate Order Form or addendum specifies otherwise. If an Affiliate is identified as a separate notice recipient in an Order Form, notices relating to that Affiliate's Services shall be sent to both Customer and the Affiliate at the addresses specified therein.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Where this Agreement covers multiple Customer entities (including Affiliates, subsidiaries, and divisions): (a) All material notices under this Agreement (including termination, material breach, security incidents, material service changes, and fee increases) shall be delivered to: (i) Customer's primary legal contact at the address specified in the signature block; and (ii) each Affiliate identified in an Order Form, at the notice address specified in such Order Form. (b) Customer shall designate a centralized notice coordinator (identified in Exhibit [X]) who shall receive copies of all notices in addition to entity-specific recipients. (c) Notices affecting a specific Affiliate's Services (e.g., a security incident affecting only that Affiliate's data) shall be delivered to that Affiliate directly in addition to the centralized coordinator. (d) For purposes of non-renewal and termination notices, each Order Form shall be treated independently — a non-renewal notice for one Affiliate's Order Form shall not affect other Affiliates' Order Forms. (e) Vendor shall maintain a current notice distribution list and confirm it with Customer annually. (f) The failure to deliver notice to all required recipients shall not invalidate the notice, but shall toll any deadline triggered by the notice until the last required recipient receives it.

### Vendor-Favorable
> All notices under this Agreement shall be sent exclusively to Customer at the single address specified in the signature block. Vendor shall have no obligation to identify, track, or deliver notices to Customer's Affiliates, subsidiaries, or related entities. Customer is solely responsible for all internal distribution of notices. If Customer designates Affiliates as authorized users, Customer represents and warrants that it has authority to receive notices on behalf of all such Affiliates, and notice to Customer shall constitute effective notice to all Affiliates.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Notices shall be delivered to Customer at the address specified in the signature block. Where an Order Form identifies an Affiliate as a separate service recipient, notices related to that Affiliate's Services shall also be delivered to the Affiliate at the address specified in the Order Form.

### Notes
Multi-party notice provisions become critical in enterprise agreements that cover multiple legal entities. Key issues: (1) Centralized vs. distributed notice — a single notice address (Vendor-Favorable) is administratively simple but risks critical notices not reaching the affected entity. The best approach combines a centralized coordinator with direct notice to affected entities. (2) Affiliate independence — if each Affiliate has its own Order Form, termination and renewal notices should be handled independently per Order Form, not as a single agreement-level event. (3) Security incident notice — when a breach affects a specific Affiliate's data, that Affiliate must receive direct notice to comply with its own regulatory notification obligations (e.g., under GDPR, each controller must be notified). (4) Authority to bind Affiliates — Vendor-Favorable language deeming notice to Customer as notice to all Affiliates may not be sufficient under applicable law, particularly for data protection notifications where each controller has independent obligations. (5) Notice list maintenance — as entities are added or removed over a multi-year agreement, the notice distribution list must be kept current. (6) Tolling — if a required recipient does not receive notice, deadlines (such as cure periods or non-renewal windows) should be tolled to prevent prejudice.
