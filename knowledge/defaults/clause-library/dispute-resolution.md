# Dispute Resolution — Clause Library

## Governing Law

### Standard Language
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods shall not apply to this Agreement.

### Aggressive Language
> This Agreement and all matters arising out of or relating to this Agreement, including tort claims, shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [Customer's home state], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [Customer's preferred county and state] for any action arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

### Minimum Acceptable
> This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [a commercially reasonable US jurisdiction], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

### Notes
Delaware and New York are the most commonly accepted neutral governing law choices for US commercial contracts. For international agreements, England & Wales or Singapore are widely accepted. Avoid governing law in jurisdictions with underdeveloped commercial law or that are inconvenient for both parties.

## Multi-Step Dispute Resolution (Escalation)

### Standard Language
> In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation between designated senior executives of each party for a period of thirty (30) days following written notice of the dispute. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, either party may submit the dispute to binding arbitration in accordance with Section [X], or pursue litigation in accordance with Section [Y].

### Aggressive Language
> Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved in accordance with the following procedures: (a) The parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation between designated senior executives (Vice President level or above) for a period of fifteen (15) days following written notice; (b) If not resolved, the parties shall submit the dispute to non-binding mediation administered by JAMS under its Mediation Rules for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days; (c) If not resolved through mediation, either party may initiate binding arbitration under Section [X]. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction at any time without first exhausting these procedures.

### Minimum Acceptable
> The parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through good-faith negotiation before initiating formal proceedings.

### Notes
Multi-step escalation reduces litigation costs and preserves business relationships. The Standard version provides a structured framework. The Aggressive version adds mandatory mediation and an explicit injunctive relief carve-out. The Minimum Acceptable is brief but may be difficult to enforce without more specificity.

## Arbitration Clause

### Standard Language
> Any dispute not resolved through negotiation shall be finally settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA Rules. The place of arbitration shall be [City, State]. The language of the arbitration shall be English. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction. Each party shall bear its own costs and attorneys' fees, unless the arbitrator determines that a different allocation is appropriate.

### Aggressive Language
> Any dispute not resolved through the procedures set forth in Section [X] shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS under its Comprehensive Arbitration Rules and Procedures. The arbitration shall be conducted by three (3) arbitrators, each of whom shall be a licensed attorney with at least fifteen (15) years of experience in commercial law. The place of arbitration shall be [Customer's preferred city]. The arbitration proceedings and all related documents shall be confidential. The arbitrator(s) shall issue a reasoned written award. The arbitrator(s) shall have the authority to award compensatory damages but shall not have the authority to award punitive damages. Discovery shall be limited to document requests and depositions of fact witnesses, and shall be completed within ninety (90) days. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement that is not resolved by negotiation shall be settled by binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of a nationally recognized arbitration organization. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator, and the arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding.

### Notes
For disputes under $1M, a single arbitrator is typical. For disputes over $1M, a panel of three is common. AAA and JAMS are the most widely used US arbitration administrators. For international disputes, ICC or LCIA rules are preferred due to New York Convention enforceability. Consider whether to include discovery limitations, confidentiality requirements, and fee-shifting provisions.

## Litigation Clause (Alternative to Arbitration)

### Standard Language
> Each party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] for any action, suit, or proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement. Each party waives any objection to jurisdiction or venue in such courts, including any objection based on inconvenient forum.

### Aggressive Language
> Each party irrevocably and unconditionally (a) submits to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in [Customer's preferred county and state]; (b) waives any objection to such jurisdiction or venue, including any objection based on inconvenient forum or lack of personal jurisdiction; (c) waives any right to a jury trial in any action arising out of or relating to this Agreement; and (d) agrees that service of process may be made by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the address specified in the Notices section. The prevailing party in any action shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Any litigation arising out of this Agreement shall be brought in the state or federal courts located in [a mutually acceptable jurisdiction]. Each party consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

### Notes
Exclusive jurisdiction is preferred over non-exclusive to avoid parallel proceedings. Jury trial waivers are common in commercial contracts but may not be enforceable in all jurisdictions — verify applicable law. Consider whether the venue is practical for both parties, especially in cross-border transactions.

## Injunctive Relief Carve-Out

### Standard Language
> Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm, including but not limited to breaches of confidentiality obligations, intellectual property rights, or data protection obligations, without first exhausting the dispute resolution procedures set forth in this Agreement and without the requirement of posting a bond or proving actual damages.

### Aggressive Language
> Each party acknowledges that a breach of its obligations regarding Confidential Information, intellectual property, or data protection under this Agreement would cause irreparable harm to the other party that cannot be adequately compensated by monetary damages alone. Accordingly, either party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and other equitable remedies from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the necessity of proving actual damages, posting a bond or other security, or exhausting any dispute resolution procedures under this Agreement. Such remedies shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, all other remedies available at law or in equity.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Nothing in this Agreement shall prevent either party from seeking injunctive or equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.

### Notes
The injunctive relief carve-out is critical in any agreement with arbitration or mandatory escalation procedures. Without it, a party may be unable to obtain urgent relief to prevent ongoing harm (e.g., data breach, IP infringement). The Aggressive version includes an acknowledgment of irreparable harm and waiver of bond requirements, which strengthens the requesting party's position.
