# Compliance & Regulatory — Clause Library

## Anti-Corruption / FCPA

### Standard
> Each party represents, warrants, and covenants that it shall comply with all applicable anti-corruption laws, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act ("FCPA") and the UK Bribery Act 2010. Neither party shall, directly or indirectly, offer, pay, promise to pay, or authorize the payment of any money or anything of value to any government official, political party, or candidate for the purpose of influencing any act or decision or securing any improper advantage. Each party shall maintain accurate books and records in accordance with applicable law.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents, warrants, and covenants that it shall comply with all applicable anti-corruption laws, including the FCPA, UK Bribery Act, and all local anti-bribery laws. Vendor has not and shall not offer, pay, or authorize payment of anything of value to any government official to influence any act or decision in connection with the Services. Vendor shall maintain an anti-corruption compliance program. Vendor shall promptly notify Customer of any actual or suspected violation and shall cooperate fully with any investigation. A breach of this section shall constitute a material, incurable breach entitling Customer to immediate termination.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Each party shall comply with applicable anti-corruption laws in connection with this Agreement. Each party represents that it has not been convicted of or pleaded guilty to any offense involving bribery or corruption.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party shall comply with all applicable anti-corruption laws, including the FCPA and UK Bribery Act, in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable brevity may be insufficient in high-risk geographies or when using agents, distributors, or sub-contractors. Push for notification obligations and termination rights for violations. The Aggressive version is appropriate when vendor operations touch government customers or high-corruption-risk jurisdictions. Immediate termination for FCPA violations should be non-negotiable.

## Export Controls and Sanctions

### Standard
> Each party shall comply with all applicable export control laws and regulations, including the U.S. Export Administration Regulations ("EAR"), the International Traffic in Arms Regulations ("ITAR"), and all applicable economic sanctions programs administered by the U.S. Office of Foreign Assets Control ("OFAC"). Neither party shall export, re-export, or transfer any technical data, software, or services provided under this Agreement to any country, entity, or person prohibited or restricted under applicable export control or sanctions laws.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents and warrants that: (a) it is not located in, organized under the laws of, or owned or controlled by nationals of any country subject to comprehensive U.S. sanctions; (b) it is not listed on any U.S. government denied or restricted party list; (c) the Services do not contain encryption or other technology subject to export licensing requirements that would restrict Customer's intended use; and (d) it shall comply with all applicable export controls and sanctions. Vendor shall screen all sub-processors and sub-contractors against applicable denied-party lists. A breach of this section shall be grounds for immediate termination.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer shall comply with all applicable export control and sanctions laws and shall not access or use the Services from any embargoed country or in violation of any applicable sanctions. Customer represents that it is not listed on any U.S. government denied or restricted party list. Vendor shall have no liability arising from Customer's failure to comply with export controls or sanctions.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party shall comply with all applicable export control laws and economic sanctions in connection with this Agreement.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language placing all export/sanctions compliance burden on Customer is one-sided. Both parties should bear compliance obligations appropriate to their role. Customer-side representations are reasonable, but push for reciprocal vendor representations, particularly regarding denied-party screening. Critical for cross-border transactions and cloud services with global infrastructure.

## ADA / Accessibility Compliance

### Standard
> Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Services comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA, as published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). Vendor shall provide a current Voluntary Product Accessibility Template (VPAT) or equivalent accessibility conformance report upon Customer's request. Vendor shall remediate material accessibility deficiencies identified by Customer within a commercially reasonable timeframe.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents and warrants that the Services comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA and all applicable accessibility laws, including Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act. Vendor shall provide an updated VPAT annually and upon each major release. Vendor shall remediate accessibility deficiencies at no additional charge within sixty (60) days of notification. Vendor shall indemnify Customer against third-party accessibility claims arising from the Services.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to address accessibility in the development of the Services. Customer acknowledges that no software can guarantee full compliance with all accessibility standards. Vendor shall provide a VPAT upon written request, which shall be provided for informational purposes only and shall not constitute a warranty of compliance.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Services accessible in accordance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language with "reasonable efforts" and an express disclaimer that the VPAT is not a warranty provides minimal protection. Push for at least a WCAG 2.1 Level AA commitment with a remediation obligation. Accessibility claims are increasing, and public-sector or regulated customers may have mandatory accessibility requirements. The Aggressive version with indemnification is appropriate for customer-facing applications.

## Anti-Money Laundering / KYC

### Standard
> Each party shall comply with all applicable anti-money laundering laws and regulations, including the Bank Secrecy Act, the USA PATRIOT Act, and applicable "know your customer" requirements. Each party shall maintain an anti-money laundering compliance program appropriate to the nature and scope of its business. Upon reasonable request, each party shall provide information and documentation necessary to satisfy the other party's KYC obligations.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor represents and warrants that it maintains a comprehensive anti-money laundering program that includes customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and employee training. Vendor shall promptly notify Customer if it becomes aware of any suspicious activity involving Customer's accounts or transactions processed through the Services. Vendor shall cooperate with Customer's AML compliance program and regulatory examinations. A material failure to comply with AML obligations shall constitute grounds for immediate termination.

### Vendor-Favorable
> Customer shall comply with all applicable anti-money laundering and KYC laws in connection with its use of the Services. Customer shall be solely responsible for conducting due diligence on its own customers and transactions. Vendor shall have no AML compliance obligation with respect to Customer's end users or transactions.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Each party shall comply with all applicable anti-money laundering laws and regulations in connection with this Agreement.

### Notes
AML provisions are primarily relevant for fintech, payment processing, and financial services agreements. Vendor-Favorable language disclaiming all responsibility for AML compliance may be unacceptable for regulated financial institutions. Push for mutual compliance obligations and cooperation commitments. The Aggressive version is appropriate for payment processors or platforms that handle financial transactions.

## Data Localization

### Standard
> Vendor shall process and store Customer Data within the geographic regions specified in the applicable Order Form or Data Processing Agreement. Vendor shall not transfer or process Customer Data outside the specified regions without Customer's prior written consent. If applicable law requires data localization, Vendor shall comply at no additional cost to Customer.

### Aggressive (Customer-Favorable)
> Vendor shall process and store all Customer Data exclusively within [specified country/region]. Vendor shall not transfer, access, or make Customer Data accessible from outside the specified region, including for support, maintenance, or troubleshooting purposes, without Customer's prior written consent. Vendor shall implement technical controls to enforce geographic restrictions. Vendor shall provide evidence of data localization compliance upon Customer's request.

### Vendor-Favorable
> The Services are provided using a global cloud infrastructure. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to store primary copies of Customer Data in the region specified in the Order Form, but may process Customer Data in other regions as necessary for service delivery, disaster recovery, and maintenance. Customer acknowledges that metadata, logs, and support data may be processed outside the specified region.

### Minimum Acceptable
> Vendor shall store Customer Data within the geographic region specified in the Order Form, subject to Vendor's disaster recovery and business continuity requirements.

### Notes
Vendor-Favorable language allowing global processing "as necessary for service delivery" with carve-outs for metadata, logs, and support data may undermine data localization commitments. Push for clearly defined exceptions and technical controls. Data localization requirements are becoming more common (GDPR, China, Russia, India) and should be addressed explicitly.
