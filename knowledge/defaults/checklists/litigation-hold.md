# Litigation Hold Checklist

## Trigger Assessment
- [ ] Identify the triggering event (lawsuit, demand letter, regulatory inquiry, anticipated claim)
- [ ] Determine the scope of the dispute (parties, claims, relevant time period)
- [ ] Assess whether preservation obligations have been triggered
- [ ] Identify the legal basis for the hold (pending litigation, reasonably anticipated litigation, regulatory investigation)
- [ ] Determine the preservation obligation start date
- [ ] Engage outside counsel (if applicable)

## Scope Definition
- [ ] Define the subject matter of the hold
- [ ] Identify relevant time period (start date through present, or specific date range)
- [ ] Identify relevant parties, entities, and counterparties
- [ ] Identify relevant projects, transactions, or business matters
- [ ] Define relevant document categories and types
- [ ] Identify relevant geographic locations and offices

## Key Custodian Identification
- [ ] Identify all individuals likely to have relevant documents or information
- [ ] Include current employees, former employees, contractors, and executives
- [ ] Identify custodians across all relevant departments:
  - [ ] Business team directly involved in the matter
  - [ ] Legal department
  - [ ] Finance/accounting
  - [ ] IT/engineering
  - [ ] Human resources
  - [ ] Executive leadership
  - [ ] Sales/business development
  - [ ] Customer support
- [ ] Document the custodian list and the basis for inclusion

## Data Source Identification
- [ ] Email systems (Exchange, Gmail, etc.)
- [ ] Document management systems (SharePoint, Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)
- [ ] Instant messaging and chat platforms (Slack, Teams, etc.)
- [ ] Project management tools (Jira, Asana, etc.)
- [ ] CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)
- [ ] Financial systems (ERP, billing, accounting)
- [ ] Source code repositories (if relevant)
- [ ] Mobile devices (phones, tablets)
- [ ] Personal devices used for work (BYOD)
- [ ] Laptops and desktops
- [ ] Shared network drives and file servers
- [ ] Cloud storage
- [ ] Backup tapes and archives
- [ ] Physical files and records
- [ ] Voicemail systems
- [ ] Video conferencing recordings
- [ ] Social media accounts (corporate)

## Litigation Hold Notice
- [ ] Draft the litigation hold notice, including:
  - [ ] Description of the matter and basis for the hold
  - [ ] Scope of documents and information to be preserved
  - [ ] Types of materials covered (electronic and physical)
  - [ ] Instruction to cease any destruction, deletion, or alteration
  - [ ] Instruction to preserve auto-delete settings and suspend retention policies
  - [ ] Specific examples of relevant documents
  - [ ] Contact person for questions
  - [ ] Acknowledgment requirement
- [ ] Have the notice reviewed by legal counsel
- [ ] Distribute the notice to all identified custodians
- [ ] Distribute to IT for system-level preservation
- [ ] Collect acknowledgments from all custodians
- [ ] Follow up with custodians who have not acknowledged

## Technical Preservation
- [ ] Suspend auto-deletion policies for relevant custodians and data sources
- [ ] Suspend email retention policies for relevant mailboxes
- [ ] Place legal holds in email/collaboration systems (Exchange, Google Vault, etc.)
- [ ] Preserve relevant backup tapes or archives from recycling
- [ ] Image or preserve relevant mobile devices
- [ ] Preserve departing employee devices and accounts (if applicable)
- [ ] Preserve relevant cloud storage and SaaS data
- [ ] Document all preservation steps taken and dates

## Ongoing Management
- [ ] Maintain a litigation hold log tracking:
  - [ ] Hold issuance date
  - [ ] All custodians notified
  - [ ] Acknowledgment status
  - [ ] Reminder dates sent
  - [ ] Data sources preserved
- [ ] Send periodic reminders to custodians (quarterly recommended)
- [ ] Update the custodian list as new relevant individuals are identified
- [ ] Issue supplemental hold notices if the scope changes
- [ ] Manage custodian departures — preserve accounts and devices before offboarding
- [ ] Coordinate with IT on system changes that could affect preserved data
- [ ] Document any preservation failures or gaps and steps taken to address them

## Hold Release
- [ ] Confirm the triggering matter has been fully resolved
- [ ] Obtain legal counsel approval to release the hold
- [ ] Issue a hold release notice to all custodians
- [ ] Coordinate with IT to release technical holds
- [ ] Resume normal retention and deletion policies
- [ ] Document the hold release in the litigation hold log
- [ ] Retain the hold log and notices as part of the matter file
