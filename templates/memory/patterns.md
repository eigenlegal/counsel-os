# Patterns & Insights Log

Record observations, lessons learned, and recurring patterns here. These entries help Counsel OS provide smarter advice over time by recognizing trends in your practice.

## Entry Format

```
### [DATE] — [Pattern Title]
**Category:** [negotiation / counterparty / clause / process / market trend]
**Observation:** [What you noticed]
**Evidence:** [Specific examples or data points]
**Implication:** [What this means for future work]
**Action taken:** [Any changes made as a result, or "monitoring"]
```

---

## Example Entries

### 2025-03-01 — Enterprise Customers Pushing Back on Data Breach Notification Timelines
**Category:** negotiation
**Observation:** 4 of our last 6 enterprise customers have requested shorter data breach notification windows (24 hours instead of our standard 72 hours).
**Evidence:** BigCo (24hr), MegaCorp (24hr), FinServe (48hr), HealthTech (24hr), NormalCo (accepted 72hr), SmallBiz (accepted 72hr).
**Implication:** The market may be moving toward shorter notification windows, especially for enterprise and regulated-industry customers. Our 72-hour standard may need revisiting, or we need a clear tier-based approach (24hr for Tier 1, 72hr for others).
**Action taken:** Monitoring. Will revisit after 10 data points.

### 2025-03-05 — Indemnification Asymmetry Is Our Most Common RED Flag
**Category:** clause
**Observation:** Indemnification is flagged RED more than any other clause type — primarily due to vendors requesting uncapped, one-sided indemnification.
**Evidence:** 12 of 18 vendor agreements reviewed in Q1 had one-sided indemnification favoring the vendor. 8 were successfully negotiated to mutual. 4 were escalated to GC.
**Implication:** We should add stronger counter-language to the clause library for this pattern. Consider adding a specific negotiation script for indemnification pushback.
**Action taken:** Added mutual indemnification counter-language to clause-library/indemnification/.

### 2025-03-10 — Counterparty Negotiation Velocity Correlates with Deal Size
**Category:** process
**Observation:** Deals under $100K close 3x faster when we send a "comments summary" email instead of a full redline. Counterparties respond to narrative comments faster than marked-up documents.
**Evidence:** Average close time for sub-$100K deals: 12 days (redline) vs. 4 days (comments summary). Sample size: 15 deals over 3 months.
**Implication:** For YELLOW-track deals under $100K, default to comments summary format in the deliver phase. Reserve full redlines for RED-track and high-value deals.
**Action taken:** Updated voice.md delivery preferences. Will track results.
