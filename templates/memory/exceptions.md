# Exceptions Log

Record deviations from your standard positions here. Each entry documents when and why you accepted terms outside your normal range. This log helps Counsel OS identify when an "exception" is becoming the new normal and suggest position updates.

## Entry Format

```
### [DATE] — [Brief Exception Title]
**Matter:** [counterparty/deal name]
**Standard position:** [What your standard says]
**What we accepted:** [The actual term agreed to]
**Why:** [Business justification for the deviation]
**Conditions:** [Any conditions or mitigating factors]
**Approved by:** [Who approved the exception]
**One-time or precedent:** [Is this a one-off or should it inform future deals?]
```

---

## Example Entry

### 2025-02-10 — Accepted Vendor's Governing Law (New York) for CloudServ Agreement
**Matter:** CloudServ Inc. / Infrastructure Services Agreement
**Standard position:** Delaware governing law, Delaware courts for disputes.
**What we accepted:** New York governing law, NYC courts, with 30-day mandatory mediation before litigation.
**Why:** CloudServ is a critical infrastructure vendor and refused to negotiate governing law. They are incorporated in New York and their standard terms are New York law. The substantive legal differences between Delaware and New York for this contract type are minimal.
**Conditions:** Added mutual jury trial waiver. Negotiated the mediation clause to cap mediation at 30 days to prevent delay tactics.
**Approved by:** Mike Rivera, VP Legal
**One-time or precedent:** One-time. We prefer Delaware but New York is acceptable for infrastructure vendors. Do not extend this to customer-facing agreements.
