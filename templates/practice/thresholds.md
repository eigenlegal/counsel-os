# Escalation Thresholds

## Review Tracks

Counsel OS assigns every matter a track based on complexity and risk. Define what falls into each track for your practice.

### GREEN Track — Auto-Approval
[Matters that can proceed with minimal or no review]

Example:
> - Mutual NDAs using our template or a recognized standard template
> - Vendor agreements under $25K annual value with standard terms
> - Renewals of existing agreements with no material changes
> - Standard employee offer letters using approved templates
> - DPA addenda using our standard form

[YOUR GREEN TRACK CRITERIA HERE]

### YELLOW Track — Single Review
[Matters that need one reviewer but not full committee review]

Example:
> - Vendor agreements $25K-$250K annual value
> - Customer contracts using our template with minor modifications
> - Counterparty paper (their template) under $100K
> - Amendment or SOW to existing agreement
> - First contract with a new counterparty (regardless of value)
> - Any agreement with non-standard IP provisions

[YOUR YELLOW TRACK CRITERIA HERE]

### RED Track — Full Review
[Matters requiring senior review, multiple reviewers, or committee approval]

Example:
> - Any agreement over $250K annual value
> - Counterparty paper over $100K
> - Agreements involving equity, warrants, or convertible instruments
> - Strategic partnerships or exclusivity arrangements
> - Government contracts
> - Agreements with uncapped liability exposure
> - First-of-kind deal structures
> - International agreements with non-US governing law
> - Any matter with regulatory implications

## Counterparty Tiers
[How counterparty identity affects the review process]

Example:
> **Tier 1 — Strategic partners:** Full review regardless of value. Relationship
> context from the counterparty entity file always loaded. GC involved in negotiation
> strategy. Examples: top 10 customers, key technology partners.
>
> **Tier 2 — Known counterparties:** Standard review process. Check
> the counterparty entity file for history and prior positions. Examples: established
> vendors, repeat customers.
>
> **Tier 3 — New counterparties:** Yellow track minimum. Extra diligence on
> corporate standing and financial stability. Create counterparty file after
> first engagement.
>
> **Tier 4 — High-risk counterparties:** Full review with enhanced diligence.
> Check sanctions lists, litigation history. Examples: foreign entities, companies
> in regulated industries, entities with known litigation history.
