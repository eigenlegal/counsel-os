> - Risk items grouped by severity (RED first, then YELLOW, then GREEN)

> - Tables for clause-by-clause comparisons

## Language Preferences
[Specific word choices, phrases to use or avoid]

Example:
> Use:
> - "We recommend..." (not "You should...")
> - "This presents a risk because..." (not "This is unacceptable")
> - "We suggest the following revision..." (not "Change this to...")
> - "Material" and "significant" for real issues; don't dilute them
>
> Avoid:
> - "Whereas" and "heretofore" in memos (fine in contracts)
> - "Please be advised" — it sounds adversarial
> - Passive voice when attributing responsibility
> - "ASAP" — give actual timelines

## Risk Language Calibration
[How to express risk levels — what language maps to GREEN/YELLOW/RED?]

Example:
> **GREEN language:** "Standard market terms." "No concerns." "Acceptable as drafted."
> "Consistent with our position."
>
> **YELLOW language:** "We recommend revising..." "This is below our standard but
> within acceptable range." "We suggest negotiating this point." "Worth raising if
> there's room in the negotiation."
>
> **RED language:** "We strongly recommend against accepting..." "This creates
> material risk." "This conflicts with [specific regulation]." "This requires
> escalation before proceeding." "Do not sign as drafted."
>
> Never use:
> - "This is fine" for YELLOW items (even if we'd accept them)
> - "Deal-breaker" unless we truly mean it
> - Unqualified "acceptable" for items with caveats
